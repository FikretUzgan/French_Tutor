### **Monday: Imparfait formation**

**Grammar (10min):**

**Imparfait = Imperfect tense (used to, was/were doing)**

**Formation:**
1. Take nous form of present: nous parl**ons**
2. Remove -ons: parl-
3. Add imparfait endings:

```
je parlais       I was speaking / used to speak
tu parlais       you were speaking
il/elle parlait  he/she was speaking
nous parlions    we were speaking
vous parliez     you were speaking
ils/elles parlaient  they were speaking
```

**Only irregular:** être → ét- (j'étais, tu étais...)

**All other verbs follow this pattern:**
- avoir → nous av**ons** → j'avais
- faire → nous fais**ons** → je faisais
- aller → nous all**ons** → j'allais

English comparison:
English: "used to" or "was/were -ing"
French: Imparfait (one form covers both!)
- I used to live → J'habitais
- I was living → J'habitais

Examples:
1. Quand j'étais petit, j'habitais à Paris. (When I was little, I lived/used to live in Paris.)
2. Il faisait beau hier. (It was nice yesterday.)
3. Nous parlions français à la maison. (We spoke French at home.)
4. Tu aimais le chocolat? (Did you use to like chocolate?)
5. Ils allaient au parc tous les jours. (They used to go to the park every day.)
6. Elle était fatiguée. (She was tired.)

**Vocabulary (5min):**
1. **Quand** [kɑ̃] - When
   - *Quand j'étais jeune, je jouais au football.* (When I was young, I used to play football.)
   
2. **Petit(e)** [pəti(t)] - Small/Little (young)
   - *Quand j'étais petit, j'aimais les dessins animés.* (When I was little, I liked cartoons.)
   
3. **Jeune** [ʒœn] - Young
   - *Il était très sportif quand il était jeune.* (He was very athletic when he was young.)

**Speaking (10min):**
Scenario: Childhood memories
```
AI: Comment était ton enfance?
YOU: [Describe using imparfait]
Expected: Quand j'étais petit(e), j'habitais... J'aimais... Je jouais...
```

**Mini Quiz (5min):**
1. Form imparfait stem: nous mangeons → ________
2. Conjugate: parler (je, imparfait)
3. Translate: I used to play football

**Homework (Text):**

### **Tuesday: Imparfait uses - descriptions & habits**

**Grammar (10min):**

**When to use Imparfait:**

**1. Descriptions in the past:**
- Weather: Il faisait froid. (It was cold.)
- Physical: Il était grand. (He was tall.)
- Mental/Emotional: J'étais content. (I was happy.)
- Age: J'avais dix ans. (I was ten.)

**2. Habitual/repeated actions (used to):**
- Tous les jours, je jouais. (Every day, I used to play.)
- Le samedi, nous allions au parc. (On Saturdays, we used to go to the park.)

**3. Background/setting:**
- Il faisait beau quand je suis sorti. (It was nice when I went out.)

**Key time markers for imparfait:**
- avant (before)
- autrefois (in the past)
- tous les jours (every day)
- le samedi (on Saturdays)
- toujours (always)
- souvent (often)
- généralement (generally)
- d'habitude (usually)

English comparison:
English distinguishes: "was doing" vs "used to do"
French: imparfait for BOTH
Look for habitual action or description = imparfait

Examples:
1. Il faisait beau ce jour-là. (It was nice that day. - description)
2. Je jouais au tennis tous les mercredis. (I used to play tennis every Wednesday. - habit)
3. Avant, nous habitions à Lyon. (Before, we used to live in Lyon.)
4. Elle était toujours gentille. (She was always kind. - description)
5. Quand j'étais petit, je mangeais beaucoup de bonbons. (When I was little, I ate a lot of candy. - habit)
6. Il y avait beaucoup de monde. (There were a lot of people. - description)

**Vocabulary (5min):**
1. **Avant** [avɑ̃] - Before
   - *Avant, j'habitais en France.* (Before, I lived in France.)
   
2. **Souvent** [suvɑ̃] - Often
   - *Nous allions souvent au cinéma.* (We often used to go to the cinema.)
   
3. **Toujours** [tuʒuʁ] - Always
   - *Elle était toujours contente.* (She was always happy.)

**Speaking (10min):**
Scenario: How things used to be
```
AI: Comment était ta vie il y a 10 ans?
YOU: [Describe habits and states]
Expected: Il y a 10 ans, j'habitais... Je travaillais... C'était...
```

**Mini Quiz (5min):**
1. Which tense for: "I was tired yesterday"?
2. Which tense for: "I used to play every day"?
3. Translate: When I was young, I was shy

### **Wednesday: Imparfait vs Passé Composé - Concept**

**Grammar (10min):**

**Imparfait vs Passé Composé - The KEY distinction:**

**Passé Composé:**
- ✅ Completed action
- ✅ Specific event
- ✅ One-time occurrence
- Keywords: hier, soudain, tout à coup, une fois

**Imparfait:**
- ✅ Ongoing action
- ✅ Description/background
- ✅ Repeated habit (used to)
- Keywords: souvent, toujours, tous les jours, pendant que

**English comparison:**
English shows this difference:
- "I ate" (specific) = passé composé
- "I was eating" (ongoing) = imparfait
- "I used to eat" (habit) = imparfait

But French shows it grammatically, English shows it with words!

**Visual:**
```
IMPARFAIT (background)
━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━━
    ⬇️ PC (event)
Il pleuvait quand je suis sorti.
(It was raining when I went out.)
```

Examples:
1. J'ai mangé une pomme. (I ate an apple. - completed) - PC
2. Je mangeais une pomme. (I was eating an apple. - ongoing) - IMP
3. Hier, j'ai vu Marie. (Yesterday, I saw Marie. - specific event) - PC
4. Avant, je voyais Marie tous les jours. (Before, I used to see Marie every day. - habit) - IMP
5. Il faisait beau quand nous sommes arrivés. (It was nice when we arrived.) - IMP + PC
6. Pendant que je lisais, le téléphone a sonné. (While I was reading, the phone rang.) - IMP + PC

**Vocabulary (5min):**
1. **Pendant que** [pɑ̃dɑ̃kə] - While
   - *Pendant que je travaillais, il est arrivé.* (While I was working, he arrived.)
   
2. **Soudain** [sudɛ̃] - Suddenly
   - *Soudain, j'ai entendu un bruit.* (Suddenly, I heard a noise.)
   
3. **Tout à coup** [tuʁaku] - All of a sudden
   - *Tout à coup, il a commencé à pleuvoir.* (All of a sudden, it started raining.)

**Speaking (10min):**
Scenario: Story with background and events
```
AI: Raconte une histoire (what was happening + what happened)
YOU: [Mix imparfait (background) + PC (events)]
Expected: Il faisait beau. Je marchais dans le parc quand j'ai vu...
```

**Mini Quiz (5min):**
1. Choose: I saw him yesterday. (a) PC (b) IMP
2. Choose: It was raining. (a) PC (b) IMP
3. Choose: I used to live in Paris. (a) PC (b) IMP

**Homework (Mixed):**
Write + Record: Story mixing both tenses (10 sentences minimum).

### **Thursday: Imparfait vs Passé Composé - Practice**

**Grammar (10min):**

**More nuanced distinctions:**

**STATE vs ACTION:**
- J'étais fatigué. (I was tired. - state) - IMP
- Je suis devenu fatigué. (I became tired. - change) - PC

**DURATION (unspecified vs specified):**
- J'habitais à Paris. (I lived in Paris. - no specified duration) - IMP
- J'ai habité à Paris pendant 3 ans. (I lived in Paris for 3 years. - specified) - PC

**IN PROGRESS vs COMPLETED:**
- Je lisais le livre. (I was reading the book. - not finished) - IMP
- J'ai lu le livre. (I read the book. - finished) - PC

**Common pairs:**
```
Il était 8h. (It was 8:00. - setting) - IMP
Je me suis levé à 8h. (I got up at 8:00. - action) - PC

Il faisait beau. (It was nice. - description) - IMP
Il a fait beau hier. (It was nice yesterday. - summary of day) - PC
```

English comparison:
These nuances exist in English but aren't always grammatically marked.
French forces you to choose the right tense!

Examples:
1. Pendant que je regardais la télé, mon ami a téléphoné. (While I was watching TV, my friend called.)
2. J'ai regardé la télé pendant deux heures. (I watched TV for two hours. - completed)
3. Quand j'étais jeune, j'aimais les bonbons. (When I was young, I liked candy. - state)
4. Un jour, j'ai mangé trop de bonbons. (One day, I ate too many candies. - specific event)
5. Il neigeait quand je suis sorti. (It was snowing when I went out.)
6. Hier, il a neigé toute la journée. (Yesterday, it snowed all day. - complete event)

**Vocabulary (5min):**
1. **Un jour** [œ̃ʒuʁ] - One day
   - *Un jour, j'ai décidé de partir.* (One day, I decided to leave.)
   
2. **Pendant** [pɑ̃dɑ̃] - During/For
   - *J'ai habité là-bas pendant 5 ans.* (I lived there for 5 years.)
   
3. **Devenir** [dəv(ə)niʁ] - To become
   - *Je suis devenu professeur.* (I became a teacher.)

**Speaking (10min):**
Scenario: Complex narrative
```
AI: Raconte ton meilleur souvenir d'enfance
YOU: [Set scene with IMP, tell events with PC]
Expected: J'avais 8 ans. C'était l'été. Il faisait beau. Un jour, nous sommes allés...
```

**Mini Quiz (5min):**
1. Fill: Hier, j'___ au parc. (specific trip) (a) allais (b) suis allé
2. Fill: Avant, j'___ au parc tous les jours. (habit) (a) allais (b) suis allé
3. Translate: While I was eating, he arrived

**Homework (Text):**
Write story: "An unforgettable day" - use both tenses correctly (15 sentences).

### **Friday: Weather & emotions in the past**

**Grammar (10min):**

**Common Imparfait Contexts:**

**Weather in the past:**
- Il faisait beau. (It was nice.)
- Il pleuvait. (It was raining.)
- Il neigeait. (It was snowing.)
- Il y avait du vent. (It was windy.)
- Il faisait froid/chaud. (It was cold/hot.)

**Emotions & states:**
- J'étais content(e). (I was happy.)
- Tu avais peur. (You were afraid.)
- Il était triste. (He was sad.)
- Nous étions fatigués. (We were tired.)
- Vous aviez faim. (You were hungry.)
- Elles étaient nerveuses. (They were nervous.)

**Physical descriptions:**
- Elle était grande. (She was tall.)
- Il portait un chapeau. (He was wearing a hat.)
- Ils avaient les cheveux longs. (They had long hair.)

**Complete narrative pattern:**
Setting (IMP) → Event (PC) → Reaction (IMP or PC)
- Il faisait beau. → Je suis sorti. → J'étais content.

English comparison:
English uses "was/were" + adjective (simple)
French uses imparfait for all past descriptions
Both languages agree here!

Examples:
1. Ce jour-là, il faisait très beau. (That day, it was very nice.)
2. J'étais content parce que c'était les vacances. (I was happy because it was vacation.)
3. Elle portait une belle robe rouge. (She was wearing a beautiful red dress.)
4. Nous avions très faim. (We were very hungry.)
5. Quand je suis arrivé, il pleuvait. (When I arrived, it was raining.)
6. Ils étaient fatigués après le voyage. (They were tired after the trip.)

**Vocabulary (5min):**
1. **Peur** [pœʁ] - Fear
   - *J'avais peur dans le noir.* (I was afraid in the dark.)
   
2. **Triste** [tʁist] - Sad
   - *Elle était triste ce jour-là.* (She was sad that day.)
   
3. **Nerveux/nerveuse** [nɛʁvø/nɛʁvøz] - Nervous
   - *J'étais nerveux avant l'examen.* (I was nervous before the exam.)

**Speaking (10min):**
Scenario: Describe a specific past day
```
AI: Comment était hier? Qu'est-ce que tu as fait?

Expected: Hier, il faisait beau. J'étais content. J'ai décidé de...
```

**Mini Quiz (5min):**

### **Monday: Storytelling structure**

**Grammar (10min):**

**French Story Structure:**

**Opening (Imparfait):**
- C'était + time/season
- Il faisait + weather
- Il y avait + description
- Characters were + description

**Body (Mixed):**
- Background/ongoing: Imparfait
- Events/actions: Passé Composé
- Use pendant que, quand, soudain

**Conclusion (Often PC):**
- Final event: PC
- Final state: IMP or PC

**Common story starters:**
- Il était une fois... (Once upon a time...)
- C'était en [year]... (It was in [year]...)
- Quand j'avais [age] ans... (When I was [age]...)
- Un jour... (One day...)

English comparison:
English narrative structure is similar
French grammatically enforces background vs events
Good for creating vivid, layered stories

Examples - Complete mini-story:
```
C'était l'hiver. Il neigeait beaucoup. (Setting - IMP)
J'avais 15 ans et j'habitais à Montréal. (Background - IMP)
Un jour, j'ai décidé de sortir. (Event - PC)
Je marchais dans la rue quand j'ai vu un chien. (Ongoing + Event - IMP + PC)
Il était petit et il avait froid. (Description - IMP)
Je l'ai pris et je l'ai ramené chez moi. (Events - PC)
Ma mère était surprise mais contente. (Reaction - IMP)
Ce chien est devenu mon meilleur ami. (Conclusion - PC)
```

**Vocabulary (5min):**
1. **Il était une fois** [iletɛ̃ynfwa] - Once upon a time
   - *Il était une fois un prince...* (Once upon a time there was a prince...)
   
2. **Endroit** [ɑ̃dʁwa] - Place
   - *C'était un bel endroit.* (It was a beautiful place.)
   
3. **Ramener** [ʁam(ə)ne] - To bring back
   - *J'ai ramené le chien chez moi.* (I brought the dog back to my home.)

### **Monday: Futur Simple formation**

**Grammar (10min):**

**Futur Simple = Simple future (will...)**

**Regular formation:**
Infinitive + endings

```
-er/-ir verbs: keep infinitive
parler → je parlerai
finir → je finirai

-re verbs: drop -e
vendre → je vendrai

Endings (all verbs):
-ai, -as, -a, -ons, -ez, -ont
```

**Example: parler**
```
je parlerai      I will speak
tu parleras      you will speak
il/elle parlera  he/she will speak
nous parlerons   we will speak
vous parlerez    you will speak
ils/elles parleront  they will speak
```

**Common irregulars:**
```
être → ser-     je serai
avoir → aur-    j'aurai
aller → ir-     j'irai
faire → fer-    je ferai
pouvoir → pourr- je pourrai
vouloir → voudr- je voudrai
venir → viendr-  je viendrai
voir → verr-     je verrai
```

English comparison:
English: "will" + verb (I will speak)
French: One word (Je parlerai)
French future is simpler - no auxiliary!

Examples:
1. Demain, je parlerai avec Marie. (Tomorrow, I will speak with Marie.)
2. Tu finiras ce travail? (Will you finish this work?)
3. Il sera content. (He will be happy.)
4. Nous irons à Paris. (We will go to Paris.)
5. Vous verrez un film? (Will you see a movie?)
6. Ils feront leurs devoirs. (They will do their homework.)

**Vocabulary (5min):**
1. **Prochain(e)** [pʁɔʃɛ̃/pʁɔʃɛn] - Next
   - *La semaine prochaine, je voyagerai.* (Next week, I will travel.)
   
2. **Futur** [fytyʁ] - Future
   - *Dans le futur, je serai médecin.* (In the future, I will be a doctor.)
   
3. **Un jour** [œ̃ʒuʁ] - One day (future)
   - *Un jour, je visiterai le Japon.* (One day, I will visit Japan.)

**Speaking (10min):**
Scenario: Life predictions
```
AI: Comment sera ta vie dans 10 ans?
YOU: [Describe using futur simple]
Expected: Dans 10 ans, je travaillerai... J'habiterai... J'aurai...
```

**Mini Quiz (5min):**
1. Form futur: je + parler
2. Form futur: nous + être
3. Translate: I will go to France

**Homework (Audio):**
Record predictions: Your life in 5 years (10 sentences).

### **Monday: Comparatives**

**Grammar (10min):**

**Comparatives = More/less/as...than**

**Adjectives:**
```
plus + adj + que = more...than
moins + adj + que = less...than
aussi + adj + que = as...as

Example:
Marie est plus grande que Paul. (Marie is taller than Paul.)
Paul est moins grand que Marie. (Paul is less tall than Marie.)
Je suis aussi grand que toi. (I am as tall as you.)
```

**Irregulars:**
```
bon → meilleur (better)
mauvais → pire (worse)
```

**Adverbs:**
```
plus vite que (faster than)
moins souvent que (less often than)
aussi bien que (as well as)
```

**Quantities:**
```
plus de... que (more...than)
moins de... que (fewer...than)
autant de... que (as many...as)

Example:
J'ai plus de livres que toi. (I have more books than you.)
```

English comparison:
English: more/less + adjective OR adjective + -er
French: ALWAYS plus/moins + adjective
No equivalent to "-er" endings (taller, faster)

Examples:
1. Paris est plus grand que Lyon. (Paris is bigger than Lyon.)
2. Ce film est moins intéressant que l'autre. (This movie is less interesting than the other.)
3. Elle est aussi intelligente que son frère. (She is as intelligent as her brother.)
4. C'est meilleur que ça. (It's better than that.)
5. J'ai plus d'argent que toi. (I have more money than you.)
6. Il court plus vite que moi. (He runs faster than me.)

**Vocabulary (5min):**
1. **Meilleur(e)** [mɛjœʁ] - Better
   - *Ce restaurant est meilleur.* (This restaurant is better.)
   
2. **Pire** [piʁ] - Worse
   - *C'est pire qu'avant.* (It's worse than before.)
   
3. **Autant** [otɑ̃] - As much/many
   - *J'ai autant de travail que toi.* (I have as much work as you.)

**Speaking (10min):**
Scenario: Compare things
```

YOU: [Use comparatives]
Expected: Ma ville est plus petite que Paris. Il y a moins de monde...
```

**Mini Quiz (5min):**
1. Translate: She is taller than me
2. Translate: I have more books than you
3. What's the comparative of "bon"?

**Homework (Text):**
Write 10 comparisons: your city vs another, yourself vs friend, past vs present.

### **Tuesday: Superlatives**

**Grammar (10min):**

**Superlatives = The most/least**

**Formation:**
```
le/la/les + plus/moins + adj (+ de)

Examples:
C'est le plus grand. (It's the biggest.)
C'est la moins chère. (It's the least expensive.)
Ce sont les plus beaux. (They're the most beautiful.)
```

**Irregulars:**
```
bon → le meilleur (the best)
mauvais → le pire (the worst)
```

**With nouns:**
```
le plus de... (the most...)
le moins de... (the least/fewest...)

Example:
C'est moi qui ai le plus de travail. (I'm the one who has the most work.)
```

**Position matters:**
Before noun: le plus grand homme (the tallest man)
After noun: l'homme le plus grand (the tallest man)

English comparison:
English: "the most" OR "-est" (biggest, fastest)
French: ALWAYS le/la/les + plus/moins
Agreement with gender/number essential!

Examples:
1. C'est le plus beau pays du monde. (It's the most beautiful country in the world.)
2. Elle est la plus intelligente de la classe. (She's the smartest in the class.)
3. C'est le meilleur restaurant de la ville. (It's the best restaurant in the city.)
4. Ce sont les films les moins intéressants. (These are the least interesting movies.)
5. Paris est la plus grande ville de France. (Paris is the biggest city in France.)
6. C'est moi qui parle le plus vite. (I'm the one who speaks the fastest.)

**Vocabulary (5min):**
1. **Monde** [mɔ̃d] - World
   - *C'est le plus beau pays du monde.* (It's the most beautiful country in the world.)
   
2. **Classe** [klɑs] - Class
   - *Il est le meilleur élève de la classe.* (He's the best student in the class.)
   
3. **Ville** [vil] - City
   - *C'est la plus grande ville du pays.* (It's the biggest city in the country.)

**Speaking (10min):**
Scenario: Talk about bests/worsts
```
AI: Quel est le meilleur film? La meilleure ville? etc.
YOU: [Give opinions with superlatives]
```

**Mini Quiz (5min):**
1. Translate: It's the most beautiful
2. Translate: She's the best

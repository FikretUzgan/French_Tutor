### Scaffolding Steps

**Current Events & Journalistic French:**

Engaging with news and current affairs requires specific vocabulary and structural understanding.

**1. NEWS ARTICLE STRUCTURE:**

```
HEADLINE (Titre): Captures main point dramatically
LEAD (Surtitre): Brief summary of who, what, when, where
BODY: Details, context, quotes
CONCLUSION: Implications, expert opinions
```

**2. JOURNALISTIC REGISTER:**

```
CHARACTERISTICS:
- Passive voice more common (for objectivity)
- Third person perspective
- Present tense for immediacy (even if narrating past)
- Reported speech without direct quotes when space-limited
- Formal register with sophisticated vocabulary
- Emphasis on facts, data, quotes over opinion

EXAMPLE DIFFERENCES:
INFORMAL: "Macron a dit qu'il faisait un truc génial pour l'économie."
JOURNALISTIC: "Le Président Macron a affirmé que ses réformes économiques visaient à relancer la croissance et à moderniser le marché du travail."

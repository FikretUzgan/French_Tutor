## Week 27 / Block 7 / CEFR Level B2.1

### Learning Outcomes (CEFR Descriptors)
- Can construct and present complex, multi-point arguments clearly and persuasively.
- Can analyse and evaluate the structure and effectiveness of argumentative texts.
- Can use a wide range of argument connectors, concession markers and persuasive techniques to enhance coherence and impact.

### Interaction Tier
**Tier:** 3
**Interaction Type:** Fully Initiated (student constructs argument, AI challenges, asks for deeper development or counter-arguments)

### Grammar Target
**Form:** Argumentation Structure and Persuasive Writing
**Complexity Rating:** 9/10
**Prerequisite Knowledge:** Advanced connectors, subjunctive mood, participle clauses, register variation, discourse markers
**Scaffolding Steps:**
1. Classic French argumentation structure: introduction → thèse → arguments + preuves → antithèse (contre-arguments) → réfutation → synthèse → conclusion
2. Key linguistic tools:
   - introducing arguments: tout d’abord, en premier lieu, d’une part
   - adding evidence: en effet, c’est-à-dire, par exemple, selon…
   - concession: bien que, certes… mais, il est vrai que… cependant
   - counter-argument & refutation: certes… mais, on pourrait objecter que… néanmoins
   - conclusion: en conclusion, finalement, il s’ensuit que
3. Persuasive techniques: rhetorical questions, appeal to logic/authority/emotion, statistics/examples, strong modal verbs
4. Show 2 model texts (one simple, one more developed)
5. Guided practice: complete missing parts of an incomplete argument
6. Production step 1: write thesis + 2 arguments + 1 counter-argument + refutation
7. Production step 2: write full 250–300 word argumentative text

### Vocabulary Set (20 words)
**Semantic Domain:** Argumentation, persuasion, concession, evidence, logical connectors
thèse, antithèse, synthèse, argument, contre-argument, réfutation, preuve, exemple, statistique, autorité, logique, émotion, certes, néanmoins, cependant, en revanche, il est vrai que, on pourrait objecter que, il s’ensuit que, il convient de souligner que, force est de constater que

### Speaking Scenario
**Domain:** Structured opinion + counter-arguments
Choose a controversial topic (remote work, AI in software development, social media regulation, universal basic income…).
Present your position (thèse), give 2–3 arguments, acknowledge at least one strong counter-argument and refute it convincingly.
Use at least **8 different discourse markers / argumentative phrases**.

### Reading/Listening Component
**Type:** Opinion article + audio version (180–220 seconds)
Article from a French newspaper or magazine (Le Monde, Le Figaro…) presenting a clear thesis-antithesis-synthesis structure
→ 10 questions (identify thesis, find concession markers, spot refutation, evaluate strength of arguments, suggest alternative connectors)

### Homework Assignment
**Type:** Text
Write a structured argumentative essay (280–350 words) taking a clear position on a contemporary societal / technological issue.

**Required elements:**
- Clear thesis statement
- At least 3 well-developed arguments with evidence/examples
- Acknowledgement and refutation of at least 2 counter-arguments
- Use of **at least 10 different argumentative / discourse markers**
- Integration of **at least 4 advanced subjunctive structures** from previous weeks

**Rubric:**
- [ ] Clear thesis and logical overall structure
- [ ] ≥ 3 strong arguments + evidence
- [ ] Counter-arguments acknowledged and convincingly refuted
- [ ] ≥ 10 argumentative/discourse markers used appropriately
- [ ] ≥ 4 advanced subjunctive structures correctly used
**Pass:** 4/5 criteria satisfied

### Assessment Checkpoint
**Mini-Quiz (8 questions):**
1. Complete: Le télétravail augmente la productivité, ……… il réduit les interactions humaines. → certes… mais / il est vrai que… cependant
2. Which phrase introduces a counter-argument?
   a) En effet b) On pourrait objecter que c) Par conséquent d) Tout d’abord
3. Translate: “It must be acknowledged that AI creates jobs, however it also displaces many workers.”
   → Il faut reconnaître que l’IA crée des emplois, cependant elle en supprime également beaucoup.
4. Find the concession marker: Bien que les réseaux sociaux connectent les gens, ils favorisent souvent la polarisation.
5. Strong refutation starter: “Certes, … mais il ne faut pas oublier que…”
6. Which structure is most persuasive here?
   “Selon une étude récente…” / “À mon avis personnel…” / “Tout le monde sait que…”
7. Complete with subjunctive: Il est regrettable que tant de gens ……… (ne pas comprendre) les dangers de la désinformation. → ne comprennent pas
8. Choose the best conclusion marker: en somme / en revanche / par exemple / d’abord

## EXAM STRUCTURE & TIMING

### SECTION 1: COMPRÉHENSION ORALE (30 minutes) - 25 points

**Part A: Interview/monologue (8-10 min)** 
- Listen to authentic French (news, documentary, podcast, etc.)
- Answer 6-8 comprehension questions
- Mix of multiple choice and open-ended

**Part B: Conversation/dialogue (5-7 min)**
- Listen to realistic conversation
- Answer 6-8 questions about details and inference

**Instructions:**
- Sketches will be played twice
- Questions visible before listening
- Write answers as you listen or just after

---

### SECTION 2: COMPRÉHENSION ÉCRITE (30 minutes) - 25 points

**Part A: News/article (5-7 min)** - 6-8 questions
- Modern French news article or online text
- Comprehension and inference questions

**Part B: Literary or intellectual text (12-15 min)** - 6-8 questions
- Complex text (literary excerpt, academic brief, philosophy, etc.)
- Requires deeper understanding of nuance and implicit meaning

**Instructions:**
- All questions visible with text
- Mix of multiple choice, short answer, and open-ended
- Focus on detail AND inference

---

### SECTION 3: PRODUCTION ÉCRITE (60 minutes) - 25 points

**Choose ONE of two essay tasks (250-320 words):**

**Option A: Formal essay on abstract topic**
- "Discuss the role of [institution/concept] in modern society"
- Present multiple perspectives
- Develop arguments logically
- Clear intro/body/conclusion structure

**Option B: Personal correspondence**
- Letter, email, or formal complaint
- Clear purpose and request
- Professional register
- Logical organization

**Evaluation Criteria:**
- Accomplishment of task (5 points): Does it answer the prompt fully?
- Coherence & organization (5 points): Clear structure, logical flow
- Vocabulary & variation (5 points): Appropriate & sophisticated word choice
- Grammar & syntax (5 points): Correct structures, minimal errors
- Neatness/presentation (0 points noted): Legible and professional

---

### SECTION 4: PRODUCTION ORALE (15 minutes total) - 25 points

**Part A: Individual presentation (3-4 min)**
- Choose from provided topics relating to current events, culture, or society
- Prepare for 10 minutes (written notes allowed)
- Present clearly and fluently
- Should feel like mini-speech, not just answer

**Part B: Interview/discussion (5-6 min)**
- Examiner asks follow-up questions
- Discuss broader implications of your topic
- Show ability to elaborate, defend position, handle questions

**Part C: Conversation exercise (2-3 min)**
- Role-play scenario (restaurant, hotel, problem-solving, etc.)
- Show ability to interact naturally and handle unexpected situations

**Evaluation Criteria for Speaking:**
- Fluency & ease of expression (0-5 points)
- Phonetic accuracy & intonation (0-5 points)
- Grammar & syntax (0-5 points)
- Vocabulary & expressions (0-5 points)
- Interaction & task completion (0-5 points)

## STRATEGIC REMINDERS FOR EXAM DAY

### Comprehension Orale
- Don't expect to understand every word
- Focus on main ideas and supporting details
- Take notes while listening; review before answering

### Compréhension Écrite
- Skim both texts first to get overview
- Read questions before detailed reading
- Infer meaning from context
- Pay attention to connecting words and logical flow

### Production Écrite
- Outline before writing (5 minutes)
- Use clear paragraphing
- Write complex sentences to demonstrate range
- Leave time for review (10-15 min)
- Legibility and neatness affect impression

### Production Orale
- Speak at natural pace (pauses are okay)
- Don't interrupt yourself constantly for corrections
- Elaborate on answers to show range  
- Ask for clarification if needed
- Maintain confidence

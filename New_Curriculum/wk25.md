## Week 25 / Block 7 / CEFR Level B2.1

### Learning Outcomes (CEFR Descriptors)
- Can express nuanced opinions and arguments on complex, abstract topics using idiomatic expressions naturally.
- Can use advanced subjunctive structures to convey doubt, wishes, subjective judgments and stylistic nuance with high accuracy.
- Can recognise and adapt idioms to formal/informal registers and explain their literal vs figurative meaning.

### Interaction Tier
**Tier:** 2→3 Transition
**Interaction Type:** Fully Initiated (student drives the topic, AI debates as a peer while still giving pedagogical feedback)

### Grammar Target
**Form:** Advanced Subjunctive + Idiomatic Expressions (stylistic choices, nuanced triggers, perfect subjunctive, register adaptation)
**Complexity Rating:** 8/10
**Prerequisite Knowledge:** Subjonctif présent & passé, conjonctions complexes, discourse markers, register differences
**Scaffolding Steps:**
1. Stylistic nuance reminder: subjonctif choice can create objective/subjective contrast (Je crois qu’il viendra vs Je doute qu’il vienne)
2. Yeni/advanced triggers: bien que / quoique, à moins que, quoi que, pourvu que, avant que… ne, de peur que, sans que, il est temps que, il est grand temps que
3. Subjonctif plus-que-parfait / subjonctif imparfait recognition (literary)
4. 15–18 example sentences (everyday → formal → literary transition)
5. Guided exercise: choose indicatif/subjonctif + adapt register
6. Transformation: 8 sentences → idiom-containing + subjonctif version
7. Production: 7–9 sentence paragraph (opinion + idiom + advanced subjonctif)

### Vocabulary Set (18 words)
**Semantic Domain:** Idiomatic expressions (body), emotions, work/stress, generosity/influence

### Speaking Scenario
**Domain:** Work/stress + personal traits discussion
Describe a stressful project you experienced as a software developer or team dynamics in your team.
Use at least **5 different idioms** and express your views with **3 advanced subjonctif** structures (bien que, pourvu que, il est temps que, etc.).

### Reading/Listening Component
**Type:** Short opinion text + audio (160–190 seconds)
“Le télétravail : avantages et inconvénients” (text includes 8–10 idioms + 6–7 subjonctif)
→ 10 questions (find idiom meaning, identify the subjonctif trigger, adapt register, reformulate)

### Homework Assignment
**Type:** Audio
Record yourself (100–140 seconds):
Defend your opinion on a topic (e.g., “Is remote work good or bad?”, “Will AI put software developers out of work?”).
Use **at least 6 different idioms** + **at least 5 advanced subjonctif** (with different triggers) in a monologue.

**Rubric:**
- [ ] ≥ 6 idioms used correctly and naturally
- [ ] ≥ 5 advanced subjonctif (different triggers) correct
- [ ] Topic developed coherently; argument is logical
- [ ] Pronunciation clear, fluency at B2.1–B2.2 transition level
- [ ] 90+ seconds of speaking time
**Pass:** 4/5 criteria met

### Assessment Checkpoint
**Mini-Quiz (8 questions):**
1. Complète : Bien que ce projet ……… (être) difficile, je continue. → soit
2. Idiom : “Il a le cœur sur la main” → meaning?
3. Traduis : “J’en ai plein le dos de ces bugs.” → I am fed up with these bugs.
4. Subjonctif : Pourvu que l’équipe ……… (gagner) ce sprint ! → gagne
5. Choisis : Il faut que tu ……… (arrêter) de procrastiner. → arrêtes
6. Idiom register: “Il a les chevilles qui enflent” → formal equivalent? → Il est très prétentieux / Il se vante trop.
7. Erreur : Je doute qu’il viendra. → correction?
8. Question : Il est grand temps que nous ……… (se mettre) au travail. → nous mettions

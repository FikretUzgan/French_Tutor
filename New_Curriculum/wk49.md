## PROJECT ASSIGNMENT

**Task:** Complete a substantial analysis of a French (or French-translated) literary work.

**Work Breakdown:** 
- Week 49: Reading & Research
- Week 50: Writing & Presentation Prep

### COMPONENT 1: Literary Analysis Essay (1500-1800 words)

**Sections:**

1. **Introduction (200 words)**
   - Author, work title, publication date, brief context
   - Thesis: What is your interpretive claim about this work?
   - Preview: How will you argue this thesis?

2. **Body Section 1: Textual & Stylistic Analysis (400 words)**
   - Analyze 2-3 specific passages
   - How do style, language, structure serve meaning?
   - Use literary terminology precisely
   - Quote extensively with proper citation

3. **Body Section 2: Thematic & Cultural Analysis (400 words)**
   - Connect work to broader themes/contexts
   - How does historical/cultural context shape meaning?
   - What is the work's significance?

4. **Body Section 3: Critical Interpretation (400 words)**
   - Acknowledge different scholarly interpretations
   - Present counter-arguments to your thesis
   - Defend your interpretation with evidence
   - Discuss limitations and complexities

5. **Conclusion (200 words)**
   - Restate thesis in light of analysis
   - Broader implications: Why does this work matter?
   - Questions for further discussion

**Language Requirements:**
- Sophisticated vocabulary throughout
- Complex sentence structures (participial clauses, advanced subjunctive, etc.)
- Use of literary terminology (15+ literary terms, used correctly)
- Advanced connectors linking ideas
- Proper MLA/APA citation format
- Minimal grammatical errors (aim for 95%+ accuracy)

**Content Requirements:**
- Textual evidence from work (minimum 8-10 direct quotes)
- Reference to 3+ scholarly sources/interpretations
- Engagement with literary tradition (how does this work fit into French literature?)
- Critical analysis (not just summary)

### COMPONENT 2: Oral Presentation (10-15 minutes)

**Deliver a presentation on your analysis:**

**Structure:**
- Introduction: Hook + thesis (2 min)
- Main argument with textual evidence (7-8 min)
- Discussion of interpretations/counter-arguments (2-3 min)
- Questions & discussion (flexible)

**Presentation Elements:**
- Visual aids optional (images from work, author context, etc.)
- Read from notes, not essay verbatim
- Maintain eye contact and natural pacing
- Use rhetorical devices for engagement
- Speak with confidence and clarity

### COMPONENT 3: Peer Discussion (Flexible)

**Prepare for extended discussion by:**
- Anticipating challenging questions
- Being ready to defend choices
- Connecting to broader themes
- Engaging with others' interpretations

## Guidance for Success

1. **Choose carefully:** Pick work you're genuinely interested in - you'll be spending weeks with it
2. **Read completely:** Don't rely on summaries; read full text
3. **Annotate extensively:** Mark important passages, note reactions, record questions
4. **Research context:** Understand author's life, historical period, literary movement
5. **Engage with criticism:** Read 2-3 scholarly articles/reviews
6. **Develop strong thesis:** Your argument should be specific and debatable
7. **Draft and revise:** First draft won't be final
8. **Seek feedback:** Have a partner/instructor review draft
9. **Practice presentation:** Don't read essay word-for-word at presentation
10. **Prepare for discussion:** Think about challenges and counterarguments

## TASKS FOR WEEK 50

### TASK 1: Complete Essay Draft (Days 1-4)

**Goals:**
- Complete full first draft of 1500-1800 word essay
- Include all required sections (intro, 3 body sections, conclusion)
- Incorporate at least 8-10 textual quotes with analysis
- Include 3+ scholarly sources/references
- Rough citations in place

**Expectations:**
- Essay should be comprehensive but doesn't need perfection yet
- May have some awkward phrasing or minor errors
- Structure should be clear and logical
- Arguments should be well-supported

**Checklist:**
- [ ] Introduction with hook, context, and thesis
- [ ] Body paragraph 1: Textual/stylistic analysis (400 words)
- [ ] Body paragraph 2: Thematic/cultural analysis (400 words)
- [ ] Body paragraph 3: Critical interpretation (400 words)
- [ ] Conclusion: Synthesis and significance (200 words)
- [ ] All quotes integrated with page numbers
- [ ] References listed
- [ ] Approximately 1500-1800 words

### TASK 2: Peer Review or Instructor Feedback (Days 5-6)

**Option A: Exchange with partner**
- You review partner's essay (brief feedback)
- Partner reviews yours

**Option B: Self-review checklist**
- [ ] Does thesis clearly answer the interpretive question?
- [ ] Does each paragraph support the thesis?
- [ ] Are quotes well-integrated and analyzed?
- [ ] Is there smooth transition between paragraphs?
- [ ] Do I acknowledge complexity and counter-interpretations?
- [ ] Is my conclusion meaningful and not just repetitive?
- [ ] Are there any confusing passages I should revise?

### TASK 3: Revision & Polish (Days 5-7)

**Revise based on feedback:**
- Strengthen any weak arguments
- Clarify confusing passages
- Improve transitions between paragraphs
- Check quotations for accuracy
- Verify citations and format
- Proofread for grammar and spelling:
  - [ ] Subject-verb agreement
  - [ ] Tense consistency
  - [ ] Pronoun reference clarity
  - [ ] Accent marks on French words
  - [ ] Spelling (especially French-specific patterns)

**Final checks:**
- [ ] Word count: 1500-1800 words (count a few random sections to verify)
- [ ] Formatting: Consistent font, margins, spacing
- [ ] Title page: Title, author, date, course (if applicable)
- [ ] Bibliography/Works Cited: Proper format (MLA/APA)
- [ ] Legibility: Clear, professional appearance

### TASK 4: Presentation Preparation (Days 4-6)

**Create presentation materials:**

**Option A: Presentation slides (recommended)**
- Title slide: Work title, author, your name, date
- Slide 2: Context (publication date, author background, brief summary)
- Slide 3-4: Your thesis and how you'll support it
- Slide 5-7: Key textual examples with analysis
- Slide 8-9: Thematic/cultural significance
- Slide 10: Scholarly perspectives and counter-arguments
- Slide 11: Conclusion/significance

**Option B: Note cards**
- 5-7 cards with key points and supporting details
- Include 2-3 important quotes
- Mark transitions between sections

**Presentation content (10-15 minutes):**
- Introduction: Hook + context + thesis (2 min)
- Main argument with textual evidence (6-8 min)
- Discussion of interpretations/counter-arguments (2-3 min)
- Conclusion (1 min)

**Language checklist:**
- [ ] Complex sentences demonstrating range
- [ ] Sophisticated vocabulary
- [ ] Smooth transitions between ideas
- [ ] Subjunctive usage appropriate to content
- [ ] Clear pronunciation (practice beforehand!)
- [ ] Natural pacing (not rushed, not monotone)

### TASK 5: Practice Presentation (Days 6-7)

**Deliver your presentation 2-3 times for practice:**

1. **Record yourself** (audio or video)
   - Listen/watch for clarity, pacing, pronunciation
   - Note any areas needing improvement

2. **Present to a study partner or friend** (if possible)
   - Ask for honest feedback:
     - Was the argument clear?
     - Did you maintain interest?
     - Any unclear passages?
     - Did you speak clearly?
   - Practice handling questions

3. **Present alone** (final rehearsal)
   - Aim for smooth delivery maintaining notes
   - Time yourself (should be 10-15 min)
   - Build confidence

**Delivery Goals:**
- Speak clearly at natural pace
- Refer to notes only occasionally
- Maintain confidence
- Engage audience (eye contact, gestures)
- Respond thoughtfully to questions

## COMMON PITFALLS TO AVOID

- **Avoiding:** Plot summary without analysis (not analysis)
- **Avoiding:** Unclear thesis or thesis that's too obvious
- **Avoiding:** Quotes without integrated analysis ("quote + new sentence")
- **Avoiding:** Presentation that reads essay word-for-word
- **Avoiding:** Grammar errors in final submission
- **Avoiding:** Improper or incomplete citations
- **Avoiding:** Presentation that's too fast or too slow
- **Avoiding:** Failure to engage with counter-interpretations

### Scaffolding Steps

**Advanced Literary Analysis - Deepening Interpretation:**

Building on week 29 (literature introduction), B2.2 students engage with longer texts and more sophisticated analytical frameworks.

**1. DEEP CHARACTERIZATION ANALYSIS:**

```
Beyond appearance: What reveals character?
- Le discours direct: Ce qu'un personnage dit
- Le discours indirect: Comment d'autres le décrivent
- Les actions: Ce qu'il choisit de faire
- Les pensées intimes: Son monologue intérieur (s'il existe)
- Les contrastes: Incohérences révélatrices

Example: In Camus' "L'Étranger," Meursault doesn'
t display expected emotions at his mother's funeral.
La forme du texte révèle son caractère détaché, presque pathologique.
```

**2. THEMATIC ANALYSIS - Going Deeper:**

```
SURFACE THEME: What the story seems about
"C'est une histoires d'amour entre deux jeunes gens"

DEEPER THEMES: What it's really about
- L'impossibilité de la compréhension entre les humains
- Le rôle des structures sociales dans le destin individuel
- La nature de l'amour véritable vs. l'illusion romantique

UNIVERSAL THEMES:
- Mortality / Death (la mort, l'impermanence)
- Identity / Self-realization (l'identité, la découverte de soi)
- Freedom vs. Constraint (la liberté vs. les contraintes)
- Power and Hierarchy (le pouvoir, les hiérarchies sociales)
- Love and Loneliness (l'amour, la solitude)
```

**3. STYLISTIC ELEMENTS & AUTHOR'S TECHNIQUE:**

```
SENTENCE LENGTH & RHYTHM:
Short sentences: Tension, urgency, immediacy
Long sentences: Complexity, psychological depth, reflection

VOCABULARY CHOICE:
Abstract vs. Concrete: What does the author emphasize?
Formal vs. Informal: Tone and accessibility
Repeated words/concepts: Obsessions, preoccupations

IMAGERY & SYMBOLISM:
Recurring images: What patterns emerge?
Color, light, darkness, water, nature: What do they signify?
Objects as symbols: What ideas do they represent?

NARRATIVE PERSPECTIVE:
First person (je): Immediacy, unreliability potential
Third person limited: Access to one character's mind
Third person omniscient: Godlike overview, dramatic irony
```

**4. HISTORICAL & CULTURAL CONTEXT:**

```
When was it written? What was happening?
- Post-war disillusionment? (1920s, 1945)
- Political upheaval? (1789, 1848, 1968)
- Social change? (Industrial, sexual, technological revolutions)

Author's background:
- Political affiliations
- Personal experiences / autobiographical elements
- Influence of literary movements

Intertextuality:
- References to other works
- Literary traditions being followed or subverted
- Allusions and their significance
```

## Speaking Scenario - Literary Discussion
**Domain:** Analyzing and discussing a literary work  
**Example interaction:**

AI: Avez-vous lu "Le Stranger" de Camus?  
Student: Oui, et j'ai trouvé extrêmement intéressant. Le personnage principal, Meursault, est absolument détaché émotionnellement.  
AI: Comment Camus transmet-il cette détachement?  
Student: Par le style minimaliste. Les phrases sont courtes. Meursault ne réfléchit pas profondément sur ses émotions. Il décrit simplement les événements factuellement.  
AI: C'est brillant. Et la signification plus large?  
Student: Je pense que Camus explore l'absurdité de l'existence. Meursault ne peut pas donner sens aux attentes sociales. Son indifférence est sa rébellion contre un monde sans signification.

## Mini Quiz
1. "L'omniscience narratoriale" means:
   (a) The narrator knows all characters' thoughts (b) The narrator is confused (c) The narrator is never wrong
   Answer: (a)

2. Which is a theme vs. a plot?
   - Plot: "Un homme vend sa maison"
   - Theme: "La place de la propriété dans l'identité humaine"

3. Identify stylistic element: "Je suis. Tu es. Il est. Nous sommes. Vous êtes. Ils sont."
   Answer: This shows repetitive structure - might indicate rhythm, meditation, or list-like accumulation

## Homework (Text) - Literary Analysis Essay

Write a 600-700 word analysis of a literary work you've read (in French, if possible; if not, use translated work). Requirements:

**Structure:**
1. **Introduction:** Title, author, publication context, brief plot summary, analytical hook (50 words)
2. **Body 1: Character Analysis** (150 words)
   - How does the author reveal character? (Actions, speech, thoughts)
   - What makes this character significant?
3. **Body 2: Thematic Analysis** (150 words)
   - What is the deeper theme beyond plot?
   - How does author convey this theme?
4. **Body 3: Stylistic Analysis** (150 words)
   - What techniques create effect? (Imagery, sentence structure, vocabulary choice, tone)
   - How do these techniques reinforce theme?
5. **Conclusion:** Synthetic statement about significance (50-100 words)

**Language Requirements:**
- Use literary terminology appropriately (at least 5 different terms)
- Use past tenses for plot summary, present for analysis
- Vary sentence structure for sophistication
- Use advanced connectors for coherence
- Support claims with textual evidence ("Par exemple," "Comme Balzac l'écrit," etc.)

Rubric:
- Depth of analysis (35%)
- Evidence & textual support (25%)
- Literary terminology usage (20%)
- Writing quality & organization (20%)

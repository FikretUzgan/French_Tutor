# Week 1 / Block 1 / CEFR Level A1.1

**Theme:** Greetings and Basic Introductions  
**Homework Rotation:** Text (Week 1)

### Learning Outcomes (CEFR Descriptors – A1 Breakthrough)
- Can understand and use very familiar everyday expressions and very basic phrases aimed at the satisfaction of needs of a concrete type.
- Can introduce him/herself and others and can ask and answer questions about personal details such as name, nationality.
- Can interact in a simple way provided the other person talks slowly and clearly and is prepared to help.

### Interaction Tier
**Tier:** 1  
**Interaction Type:** Reactive (AI asks simple questions, student responds briefly, AI provides positive reinforcement and gentle correction)

### Grammar Target
**Form:** Être (present tense)  
**Complexity Rating:** 1/10  
**Prerequisite Knowledge:** None (starting from zero)

**Scaffolding Steps:**
1. Explanation: The verb "être" means "to be". It is used like "I am", "you are" in English.
2. Conjugation table:

| Pronoun   | Conjugation | English          |
|-----------|-------------|------------------|
| je        | suis        | I am             |
| tu        | es          | you are (informal)|
| il/elle/on| est         | he/she/it is     |
| nous      | sommes      | we are           |
| vous      | êtes        | you are (formal/plural)|
| ils/elles | sont        | they are         |

3. Negative form: ne … pas → Je ne suis pas turc.
4. Questions: Est-ce que + subject + verb → Est-ce que tu es étudiant ?  
   Or inversion: Es-tu étudiant ?
5. 6–8 realistic examples:
   - Je suis Fikret.
   - Tu es turc ?
   - Elle est étudiante.
   - Nous sommes à Istanbul.
   - Vous êtes professeur ?
   - Ils sont français.

### Vocabulary Set (15 words)
**Semantic Domain:** Greetings and Personal Identity

1. bonjour            – hello  
2. salut              – hi (informal)  
3. au revoir          – goodbye  
4. je m'appelle …     – my name is …  
5. comment vous appelez-vous ? – what is your name? (formal)  
6. comment t'appelles-tu ? – what is your name? (informal)  
7. enchanté(e)        – nice to meet you  
8. merci              – thank you  
9. de rien            – you're welcome  
10. je suis           – I am  
11. français(e)       – French  
12. turc / turque     – Turkish  
13. étudiant(e)       – student  
14. professeur        – teacher  
15. comment ça va ?   – how are you?

### Speaking Scenario
**Domain:** First meeting  
**Example interaction:**

AI: Bonjour ! Je m'appelle Marie. Et toi ?  
Student: Bonjour, je m'appelle Fikret.  
AI: Enchantée, Fikret ! Tu es turc ?  
Student: Oui, je suis turc. Et toi ?  
AI: Je suis française. Comment ça va ?  
(AI praises effort, gently corrects if needed, keeps interaction short and encouraging)

### Reading/Listening Component
**Type:** Short text + slow audio  
**Text example (5–6 sentences):**

Bonjour, je m'appelle Ali.  
Je suis turc.  
Je suis étudiant.  
J'habite à Istanbul.  
Et toi ? Comment t'appelles-tu ?

**Listening task:**  
Listen and answer:  
1. What is the person's name?  
2. Where is he from?  
3. Is he a student?

### Homework Assignment
**Type:** Text  
**Task:**  
Write a 6–10 sentence self-introduction (name, nationality, student or not, how you are, etc.).  
Example start:  
"Bonjour, je m'appelle … Je suis …"

**Rubric (AI evaluation criteria):**
- [ ] At least 5 words from the vocabulary set used correctly  
- [ ] "Être" verb conjugated correctly at least 3 times  
- [ ] Sentences are simple but understandable  
- [ ] Minimal spelling/grammar issues (AI will suggest improvements)  
**Pass Threshold:** 4/4 criteria met → proceed to next week  
**Failure Protocol:** Remedial loop – rewrite the introduction with different details (e.g. introduce a friend instead)

### Assessment Checkpoint (Mini-Quiz – 5 questions)
1. Conjugate: Je ___ turc. → suis  
2. Translate: Hello, my name is Fikret. → Bonjour, je m'appelle Fikret.  
3. Form a question: Are you a student? → Es-tu étudiant ? / Tu es étudiant ?  
4. Make negative: Je suis professeur. → Je ne suis pas professeur.  
5. Listen and choose: In the audio, who says "enchantée"?

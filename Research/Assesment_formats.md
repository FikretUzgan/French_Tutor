# Assessment Formats for A1-A2 French Learning

## DELF Exam Structure (A1/A2 Levels)

### DELF A1 (Total: 100 points)

| Component | Duration | Point Value | Question Types |
|-----------|----------|------------|-----------------|
| **Listening (Compréhension Orale)** | 25 min | 25 pts | 3 tasks: (1) Identify key info in short dialogues (5 q), (2) Listening to announcements (5 q), (3) Phone messages (3 q) |
| **Reading (Compréhension Écrite)** | 30 min | 25 pts | 3 tasks: (1) Identify key info in short texts (5 q), (2) Short functional texts (emails, notes; 4 q), (3) Write short messages (1 free response) |
| **Writing (Production Écrite)** | 30 min | 25 pts | 2 tasks: (1) Simple fill-in-the-blank (postcard/note - 50 words), (2) Simple personal message (50 words) |
| **Speaking (Production Orale)** | 15 min | 25 pts | 3 tasks: (1) Introduce yourself + simple questions (interview style), (2) Imitate phrases from dialogue, (3) Respond to simple prompt spontaneously |

**Pass Requirement:** 50/100 points

---

### DELF A2 (Total: 100 points)

| Component | Duration | Point Value | Question Types |
|-----------|----------|------------|-----------------|
| **Listening** | 30 min | 25 pts | 4 tasks: (1) Identify facts in conversations (8 q), (2) Announcements/notices (6 q), (3) Phone conversation (5 q), (4) Understanding key points in longer dialogue (4 q) |
| **Reading** | 45 min | 25 pts | 4 tasks: (1) Match texts with descriptions (5 q), (2) Find specific info in real documents (6 q), (3) Comprehension questions about narrative text (5 q), (4) Write short reply to written request (free response) |
| **Writing** | 45 min | 25 pts | 2 tasks: (1) Email/note response to request (120-150 words), (2) Short narrative or opinion text (120-150 words) |
| **Speaking** | 20 min | 25 pts | 3 tasks: (1) Interview (personal questions), (2) Role play dialogue (ordering, asking for directions), (3) Discussion of document or opinion on topic |

**Pass Requirement:** 50/100 points

## Common Quiz/Test Formats in Language Learning Apps

### Recognition/Receptive Tasks
- **Multiple choice** - Select correct option from 3-4 choices
- **True/False** - Evaluate statement as correct/incorrect
- **Matching** - Words to definitions, sentences to scenarios
- **Identify correct form** - From conjugation options
- **Listen & select** - Audio + 3-4 options

### Production/Expressive Tasks
- **Fill-in-the-blank** - Supply missing word/form
- **Translation** - English → French or vice versa
- **Conjugation exercise** - Supply correct verb form
- **Short answer** - 1-2 words
- **Sentence completion** - Supply 1+ words to complete sentence
- **Free response writing** - Paragraph or dialogue on prompt
- **Speaking/recording** - Student records response to prompt

### Comprehension/Application Tasks
- **Reading comprehension questions** - Factual + inferential
- **Error detection** - "Which sentence is incorrect?"
- **Grammar correction** - "Fix this sentence"
- **Vocabulary in context** - "What does this word mean in this sentence?"
- **Reordering** - Arrange words into correct sentence

## Evaluation Methods by Skill

### Listening Comprehension
**Gist Listening (Overall Understanding)**
- Example question: "What's the main topic?"
- Format: Multiple choice with main topics

**Specific Detail Listening (Information Retrieval)**
- Example question: "Where are they?" / "Who did what?"
- Format: True/False or short answer

**Inferential Listening (Making Connections)**
- Example question: "Why did they say that?" / "What will happen next?"
- Format: Multiple choice with inference options

---

### Reading Comprehension
**Skimming (Find Main Idea)**
- Task: Multiple choice with main topics
- Example: Read title + first sentence → Choose main idea

**Scanning (Find Specific Detail)**
- Task: True/False or short answer
- Example: "What time does the store open?"

**Careful Reading (Detailed Understanding)**
- Task: Comprehension questions, inference questions
- Example: "Why did the character make that choice?"

---

### Writing Assessment

**Accuracy Rubric (0-5 scale):**
- Grammar correctness
- Spelling accuracy
- Punctuation usage
- Word choice appropriateness

**Fluency Rubric (0-5 scale):**
- Response length (meets word count)
- Sentence variety (simple + complex sentences)
- Clarity of ideas
- Organization/coherence

**Task Completion (0-5 scale):**
- Does response address the prompt fully?
- All required elements included?
- Appropriate register for context?

**Vocabulary Range (0-3 scale):**
- Variety of words used
- Appropriate register
- Correct usage in context

---

### Speaking Assessment

**Fluency (0-5 scale):**
- Smooth delivery without long pauses
- Natural pacing
- Use of filler words (euh, bien, etc.)

**Accuracy (0-5 scale):**
- Grammatical correctness
- Pronunciation clarity
- Verb conjugation accuracy

**Interaction (0-5 scale):**
- Responds to questions appropriately
- Asks clarification when needed
- Engages in conversation naturally

**Vocabulary Range (0-3 scale):**
- Word choice appropriateness
- Variety of expressions used
- Register appropriateness

## Best Practices for Writing Quiz Questions

### DELF A1/A2 Alignment
- Mix question types: 30% recognition, 40% production, 30% comprehension
- Include listening component (25-30% of questions)
- Include speaking component (15-20% through role-play)
- Writing should be scaffolded (fill-in-blank before free-form)

### Question Design
1. **Clear & unambiguous** - Student knows exactly what's being asked
2. **Age-appropriate context** - Relevant scenarios for learners
3. **Vocabulary-controlled** - Use words from current + previous lessons only
4. **Multiple skill levels** - Mix easy (recognition) + hard (production) questions
5. **Immediate feedback** - Especially for grammar/conjugation exercises

### Pacing Recommendations
- **Daily quiz:** 5-10 questions (5-10 min)
- **Weekly quiz:** 15-20 questions (20-30 min)
- **Unit exam:** 30-40 questions (45-60 min, all 4 skills)
- **DELF mock exam:** 100 points spread across all components

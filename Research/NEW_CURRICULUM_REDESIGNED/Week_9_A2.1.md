### GRAMMAR SECTION (10 min)

#### Explanation

**1. Welcome to the Imparfait - Your Second Past Tense!**

You've already mastered the **passé composé** for talking about completed actions in the past ("I ate," "I went"). Now you're learning the **imparfait** (imperfect tense), which is used for **ongoing actions, repeated habits, descriptions, and background information** in the past. Think of passé composé as a photograph (one moment captured) and imparfait as a video playing in the background (continuous action). The good news? Imparfait is **incredibly regular** - almost every verb follows the same pattern, with only ONE exception (être)!

**2. How to Form the Imparfait - The Simple Formula**

The imparfait is formed using a super consistent pattern:

**Step 1:** Take the **nous** form of the present tense
**Step 2:** Drop the **-ons** ending
**Step 3:** Add the imparfait endings: **-ais, -ais, -ait, -ions, -iez, -aient**

**Example with "parler" (to speak):**
- Present nous form: nous **parl**ons
- Drop -ons: **parl-**
- Add endings: je parlais, tu parlais, il parlait, etc.

**The imparfait endings (same for ALL verbs):**
- je **-ais**
- tu **-ais**
- il/elle/on **-ait**
- nous **-ions**
- vous **-iez**
- ils/elles **-aient**

**Key observation:** Je, tu, il/elle/on, and ils/elles all sound IDENTICAL in pronunciation! Only nous and vous sound different.

**3. Regular -ER Verbs in Imparfait**

**Parler (to speak) - stem: parl-**
- je **parlais** (I was speaking / I used to speak / I spoke)
- tu **parlais** (you were speaking / you used to speak)
- il/elle/on **parlait** (he/she/one was speaking)
- nous **parlions** (we were speaking)
- vous **parliez** (you were speaking)
- ils/elles **parlaient** (they were speaking)

**More -ER examples:**

**Manger (to eat) - stem: mange-**
- je **mangeais** (I was eating / I used to eat)
- tu **mangeais**
- il/elle **mangeait**
- nous **mangions**
- vous **mangiez**
- ils/elles **mangeaient**

**Habiter (to live) - stem: habit-**
- j'**habitais** à Paris (I used to live in Paris)
- nous **habitions** près de la mer (We used to live near the sea)

**Travailler (to work) - stem: travaill-**
- je **travaillais** dans un restaurant (I was working in a restaurant)
- ils **travaillaient** le week-end (They used to work on weekends)

**Note for -GER verbs:** Keep the "e" before "a" to maintain soft "g" sound:
- nous mangions (not "mangons" because imparfait needs soft g)

**4. Regular -IR and -RE Verbs in Imparfait**

**-IR Verbs:**

**Finir (to finish) - stem: finiss-** (from nous finissons)
- je **finissais** (I was finishing)
- tu **finissais**
- il/elle **finissait**
- nous **finissions**
- vous **finissiez**
- ils/elles **finissaient**

**Choisir (to choose) - stem: choisiss-**
- je **choisissais** (I was choosing / I used to choose)
- nous **choisissions**

**-RE Verbs:**

**Vendre (to sell) - stem: vend-** (from nous vendons)
- je **vendais** (I was selling / I used to sell)
- tu **vendais**
- il/elle **vendait**
- nous **vendions**
- vous **vendiez**
- ils/elles **vendaient**

**Attendre (to wait) - stem: attend-**
- j'**attendais** le bus (I was waiting for the bus)
- nous **attendions** depuis une heure (We had been waiting for an hour)

**Répondre (to answer) - stem: répond-**
- il **répondait** toujours rapidement (He always answered quickly)
- elles **répondaient** aux questions (They were answering the questions)

**5. Real-World Usage - When to Use Imparfait**

**Usage 1: Ongoing actions in the past (like a video playing)**
- Je **regardais** la télé quand tu as appelé. (I was watching TV when you called.)
- Il **pleuvait** ce matin. (It was raining this morning.)

**Usage 2: Repeated habits in the past**
- Quand j'**étais** petit, je **jouais** au football tous les jours. (When I was little, I played soccer every day.)
- Nous **allions** à la plage chaque été. (We used to go to the beach each summer.)

**Usage 3: Descriptions in the past**
- La maison **était** grande et belle. (The house was big and beautiful.)
- Il **faisait** beau. (The weather was nice.)

**Common time markers with imparfait:**
- **quand j'étais petit(e)** (when I was little)
- **tous les jours** (every day)
- **chaque semaine** (each week)
- **toujours** (always)
- **souvent** (often)
- **autrefois** (in the past / formerly)

**Scene 1: Childhood Memories**
- A: Qu'est-ce que tu **faisais** quand tu **étais** petit? (What did you use to do when you were little?)
- B: Je **jouais** dans le jardin et je **regardais** des dessins animés. (I used to play in the garden and watch cartoons.)

**Scene 2: Describing Past Situation**
- A: Comment **était** ton ancien appartement? (What was your old apartment like?)
- B: Il **était** petit mais confortable. J'**habitais** au troisième étage. (It was small but comfortable. I lived on the third floor.)

**Cultural Note:**
The imparfait is essential for storytelling in French! When French speakers tell stories, they use imparfait to "set the scene" (il faisait beau, j'étais jeune, nous habitions à Paris) and passé composé for specific events (je suis allé, j'ai vu, nous avons mangé).

## DAY 42 - A2.1.42: Imparfait Irregular Verbs (être, avoir, faire)

### Metadata
- **CEFR Level:** A2.1
- **Grammar Topic:** Imparfait irregular verbs - être, avoir, faire + other common irregulars
- **Vocabulary Domain:** Descriptions, possession in past, weather, activities
- **Content Identifiers:** imparfait_irregular, être_avoir_faire, common_irregulars
- **Speaking Tier:** 2 (Guided conversation)
- **Duration:** 30 minutes
- **Day Number:** 42

---

### GRAMMAR SECTION (10 min)

#### Explanation

**1. The Almost-Perfect Pattern (with One Exception!)**

Yesterday you learned that imparfait follows a super regular pattern: take the nous form, drop -ons, add endings. Good news - this works for **almost every verb** in French! There's only **ONE exception: être** (to be). All other "irregular" verbs in present tense become regular in imparfait! Verbs like avoir, faire, aller, prendre, vouloir, pouvoir - they all follow the standard pattern. Today we'll focus on the most common ones you'll use constantly when describing the past.

**2. ÊTRE - The Only Truly Irregular Verb in Imparfait**

**Être** doesn't follow the usual pattern. Instead of "nous sommes → som- + endings," être uses a completely different stem: **ét-**

**Être (to be) - stem: ét-**
- j'**étais** (I was)
- tu **étais** (you were)
- il/elle/on **était** (he/she/one was)
- nous **étions** (we were)
- vous **étiez** (you were)
- ils/elles **étaient** (they were)

**Common uses of être in imparfait:**

**Descriptions:**
- La maison **était** grande. (The house was big.)
- Ils **étaient** contents. (They were happy.)
- C'**était** magnifique! (It was magnificent!)

**Age:**
- J'**avais** dix ans quand j'**étais** à Paris. (I was ten years old when I was in Paris.)
- Quand tu **étais** petit... (When you were little...)

**Location:**
- Nous **étions** en France. (We were in France.)
- Où **étais**-tu? (Where were you?)

**State of being:**
- J'**étais** fatigué. (I was tired.)
- Elle **était** malade. (She was sick.)

**3. AVOIR - Regular Pattern Despite Being Irregular in Present**

**Avoir** seems irregular in present (j'ai, tu as, il a...), but in imparfait it's perfectly regular!

**Step 1:** Nous form in present: nous **avons**
**Step 2:** Drop -ons: **av-**
**Step 3:** Add endings: **av-** + ais/ais/ait/ions/iez/aient

**Avoir (to have) - stem: av-**
- j'**avais** (I had / I used to have)
- tu **avais** (you had)
- il/elle/on **avait** (he/she/one had)
- nous **avions** (we had)
- vous **aviez** (you had)
- ils/elles **avaient** (they had)

**Common uses of avoir in imparfait:**

**Possession:**
- J'**avais** une voiture rouge. (I had a red car.)
- Nous **avions** un chat. (We had a cat.)

**Age (avoir + number):**
- J'**avais** vingt ans. (I was twenty years old.)
- Elle **avait** quinze ans. (She was fifteen.)

**Expressions with avoir:**
- J'**avais** faim. (I was hungry.)
- Tu **avais** peur? (Were you scared?)
- Il **avait** sommeil. (He was sleepy.)
- Nous **avions** besoin de repos. (We needed rest.)

**4. FAIRE - Another Regular-in-Imparfait Verb**

**Faire** is irregular in present, but follows the pattern perfectly in imparfait!

**Step 1:** Nous form: nous **faisons**
**Step 2:** Drop -ons: **fais-**
**Step 3:** Add endings

**Faire (to do/make) - stem: fais-**
- je **faisais** (I was doing / I used to do)
- tu **faisais** (you were doing)
- il/elle/on **faisait** (he/she/one was doing)
- nous **faisions** (we were doing)
- vous **faisiez** (you were doing)
- ils/elles **faisaient** (they were doing)

**Common uses of faire in imparfait:**

**Weather (il fait):**
- Il **faisait** beau. (The weather was nice.)
- Il **faisait** froid. (It was cold.)
- Il **faisait** chaud en été. (It was hot in summer.)

**Activities:**
- Je **faisais** du sport. (I did sports / I used to do sports.)
- Nous **faisions** nos devoirs. (We were doing our homework.)
- Qu'est-ce que tu **faisais**? (What were you doing?)

**Making/Doing:**
- Elle **faisait** la cuisine. (She was cooking / used to cook.)
- Ils **faisaient** du vélo. (They rode bikes / were riding bikes.)

**5. Other Common "Irregular" Verbs - All Regular in Imparfait!**

**Aller (to go) - stem: all-** (from nous allons)
- j'**allais**, tu **allais**, il **allait**, nous **allions**, vous **alliez**, ils **allaient**
- Nous **allions** à la plage chaque été. (We used to go to the beach each summer.)

**Vouloir (to want) - stem: voul-** (from nous voulons)
- je **voulais**, tu **voulais**, il **voulait**, nous **voulions**, vous **vouliez**, ils **voulaient**
- Je **voulais** être médecin. (I wanted to be a doctor.)

**Pouvoir (can) - stem: pouv-** (from nous pouvons)
- je **pouvais**, tu **pouvais**, il **pouvait**, nous **pouvions**, vous **pouviez**, ils **pouvaient**
- Je ne **pouvais** pas dormir. (I couldn't sleep.)

**Prendre (to take) - stem: pren-** (from nous prenons)
- je **prenais**, tu **prenais**, il **prenait**, nous **prenions**, vous **preniez**, ils **prenaient**
- Nous **prenions** le bus. (We used to take the bus.)

**Real-World Examples - All Together:**

**Scene: Describing Childhood**
- Quand j'**étais** petit, j'**avais** beaucoup d'amis. Nous **faisions** du vélo ensemble et nous **allions** au parc tous les jours. C'**était** magnifique!
- (When I was little, I had many friends. We rode bikes together and we went to the park every day. It was magnificent!)

**Cultural Note:**
French speakers use être and avoir in imparfait constantly when storytelling. "Il était une fois..." (Once upon a time, there was...) is how every French fairy tale begins - notice the imparfait of être!

### Metadata

- **Grammar Topic:** When to use imparfait - detailed usage rules
- **Vocabulary Domain:** Storytelling, past descriptions, habitual actions
- **Content Identifiers:** imparfait_usage, descriptions_past, habitual_actions, ongoing_past
- **Speaking Tier:** 2→3 (Transitioning to freer conversation)
- **Duration:** 30 minutes
- **Day Number:** 43

---

### GRAMMAR SECTION (10 min)

#### Explanation

**1. Understanding the "Video" Nature of Imparfait**

Now that you know HOW to form imparfait, it's crucial to understand WHEN to use it. Think of French past tenses like a movie: **passé composé** is for specific actions that happened (like pressing pause to capture one frame), while **imparfait** is the background video playing (ongoing, continuous, descriptive). Imparfait paints the scene, sets the mood, and describes what was happening. Today you'll master the four main uses of imparfait that will make your French storytelling natural and fluent!

**2. Usage #1: Descriptions in the Past (Setting the Scene)**

Use imparfait to **describe** how things, people, or situations **were** in the past.

**Physical descriptions:**
- La maison **était** grande et belle. (The house was big and beautiful.)
- Il **avait** les cheveux blonds. (He had blond hair.)
- Elle **portait** une robe rouge. (She was wearing a red dress.)

**Emotional/Mental states:**
- J'**étais** content. (I was happy.)
- Nous **avions** peur. (We were scared.)
- Ils **pensaient** à leurs vacances. (They were thinking about their vacation.)

**Weather:**
- Il **faisait** beau. (The weather was nice.)
- Il **pleuvait**. (It was raining.)
- Il **neigeait** en hiver. (It snowed in winter.)

**Time:**
- Il **était** huit heures. (It was eight o'clock.)
- C'**était** le matin. (It was morning.)

**Location/Situation:**
- Nous **étions** à Paris. (We were in Paris.)
- Il y **avait** beaucoup de monde. (There were many people.)

**3. Usage #2: Repeated/Habitual Actions in the Past**

Use imparfait for actions that **happened regularly** or **repeatedly** in the past - your habits, routines, and what you "used to" do.

**Key time markers:**
- **tous les jours** (every day)
- **chaque semaine** (each week)
- **toujours** (always)
- **souvent** (often)
- **d'habitude** (usually)
- **le lundi/mardi...** (on Mondays/Tuesdays...)
- **autrefois** (in the past)

**Examples:**
- Je **jouais** au tennis tous les samedis. (I played tennis every Saturday.)
- Nous **allions** au marché chaque dimanche. (We went to the market each Sunday.)
- Elle **mangeait** toujours du pain au petit-déjeuner. (She always ate bread for breakfast.)
- D'habitude, ils **prenaient** le bus. (Usually, they took the bus.)
- Quand j'**étais** jeune, je **lisais** beaucoup. (When I was young, I read a lot.)

**Pattern:** If you can translate with "used to" or "would" (habitual), use imparfait!
- I **used to live** in Paris → J'**habitais** à Paris
- We **would go** to the beach → Nous **allions** à la plage

**4. Usage #3: Ongoing/Progressive Actions (Was/Were + -ing)**

Use imparfait for actions that **were in progress** - what was happening at a particular moment in the past.

**Pattern:** If you can say "was/were + verb-ing" in English, use imparfait!

**Examples:**
- Je **regardais** la télé. (I was watching TV.)
- Tu **dormais**? (Were you sleeping?)
- Il **travaillait** dans son bureau. (He was working in his office.)
- Nous **parlions** français. (We were speaking French.)
- Qu'est-ce que tu **faisais**? (What were you doing?)

**Common scenario - setting up an interruption:**
When one action interrupts another, the background action (what was happening) is in imparfait, and the interrupting action is in passé composé:

- Je **regardais** la télé quand tu **as appelé**. (I was watching TV when you called.)
- Il **dormait** quand je **suis arrivé**. (He was sleeping when I arrived.)
- Nous **mangions** quand le téléphone **a sonné**. (We were eating when the phone rang.)

**5. Usage #4: Age, Time, and Background Information**

**Age (avoir + number):**
- J'**avais** dix ans. (I was ten years old.)
- Elle **avait** vingt-cinq ans quand elle **est partie**. (She was 25 when she left.)

**Time of day:**
- Il **était** midi. (It was noon.)
- Il **était** tard. (It was late.)

**Background info in stories:**
- Il y **avait** beaucoup de gens. (There were many people.)
- C'**était** un dimanche. (It was a Sunday.)
- Le soleil **brillait**. (The sun was shining.)

**Complete narrative example combining all uses:**

Il **était** sept heures du matin. (time) Il **faisait** beau. (weather) Je **dormais** encore (ongoing) quand mon téléphone **a sonné** (interruption - PC). C'**était** mon ami Paul. (description) Il **voulait** aller au parc. (ongoing desire) Nous **allions** souvent au parc le dimanche. (habit)

*(It was seven in the morning. The weather was nice. I was still sleeping when my phone rang. It was my friend Paul. He wanted to go to the park. We often went to the park on Sundays.)*

**Cultural Note:**
French storytelling relies heavily on the imparfait/passé composé contrast. Children's books, news reports, and everyday anecdotes all use this pattern: imparfait sets the scene, passé composé moves the action forward. Mastering this makes you sound like a native storyteller!

## DAY 44 - A2.1.44: Imparfait Practice (Weather, Age, Time)

### Metadata
- **CEFR Level:** A2.1
- **Grammar Topic:** Imparfait special uses - weather, age, time, il y avait
- **Vocabulary Domain:** Weather expressions, age, time telling, existence
- **Content Identifiers:** imparfait_practice, weather, age, time, il_y_avait
- **Speaking Tier:** 2→3 (Guided to free)
- **Duration:** 30 minutes
- **Day Number:** 44

---

### GRAMMAR SECTION (10 min)

#### Explanation

**1. Special Contexts That Always Use Imparfait**

Today we'll focus on three special contexts where French ALWAYS uses imparfait in the past: **weather expressions, age, and time**. These are fixed patterns - you can't use passé composé for these even if it's a one-time event! Additionally, we'll master **il y avait** (there was/there were), one of the most useful expressions for describing past scenes. These patterns are so common that mastering them will immediately improve your French storytelling!

**2. Weather Expressions - Always Imparfait in Past**

**Key rule:** All weather descriptions in the past use **il faisait** (imparfait), NEVER **il a fait** (passé composé).

**Basic weather with faire:**
- Il **faisait** beau. (The weather was nice.)
- Il **faisait** mauvais. (The weather was bad.)
- Il **faisait** chaud. (It was hot.)
- Il **faisait** froid. (It was cold.)
- Il **faisait** frais. (It was cool/chilly.)
- Il **faisait** doux. (It was mild.)
- Il **faisait** bon. (The weather was pleasant.)

**Temperature:**
- Il **faisait** 25 degrés. (It was 25 degrees.)
- Il **faisait** moins cinq. (It was minus five.)

**Weather verbs in imparfait:**
- Il **pleuvait**. (It was raining.)
- Il **neigeait**. (It was snowing.)
- Le soleil **brillait**. (The sun was shining.)
- Le vent **soufflait**. (The wind was blowing.)
- Il y **avait** du vent. (It was windy / There was wind.)
- Il y **avait** des nuages. (There were clouds.)
- Il y **avait** du soleil. (It was sunny / There was sun.)

**Complete weather description:**
- Hier, il **faisait** très beau. Le soleil **brillait** et il **faisait** 28 degrés. Il n'y **avait** pas de nuages.
- (Yesterday, the weather was very nice. The sun was shining and it was 28 degrees. There were no clouds.)

**3. Age - Always "avoir" in Imparfait**

**Pattern:** Subject + **avait/avais/avaient** + number + **an(s)**

**Examples:**
- J'**avais** dix ans. (I was ten years old.)
- Tu **avais** quel âge? (How old were you?)
- Il **avait** quinze ans. (He was fifteen years old.)
- Elle **avait** vingt-cinq ans quand elle est partie. (She was 25 when she left.)
- Nous **avions** le même âge. (We were the same age.)
- Vous **aviez** trente ans? (Were you thirty?)
- Ils **avaient** huit ans. (They were eight years old.)

**Questions about age:**
- Quel âge **avais**-tu? (How old were you?)
- Quel âge **avait**-il? (How old was he?)
- Vous **aviez** quel âge? (How old were you?)

**Common scenario:**
- Quand j'**avais** dix ans, j'**habitais** à Lyon. (When I was ten, I lived in Lyon.)
- Elle **avait** vingt ans quand elle **a rencontré** son mari. (She was twenty when she met her husband.)

**4. Time - Always "Être" in Imparfait**

**Pattern:** Il **était** + time

**Clock time:**
- Il **était** huit heures. (It was eight o'clock.)
- Il **était** midi. (It was noon.)
- Il **était** minuit. (It was midnight.)
- Il **était** neuf heures et demie. (It was 9:30.)
- Il **était** quinze heures. (It was 3 PM / 15h.)

**Time of day:**
- C'**était** le matin. (It was morning.)
- C'**était** l'après-midi. (It was afternoon.)
- C'**était** le soir. (It was evening.)
- Il **était** tôt. (It was early.)
- Il **était** tard. (It was late.)

**Days/Dates:**
- C'**était** lundi. (It was Monday.)
- C'**était** le 15 juillet. (It was July 15th.)
- C'**était** mon anniversaire. (It was my birthday.)

**In storytelling:**
- Il **était** sept heures du matin quand je me suis réveillé. Il **faisait** encore nuit.
- (It was seven in the morning when I woke up. It was still dark.)

**5. Il y avait - There Was/There Were**

**Il y avoir** becomes **il y avait** in imparfait - one of the most common expressions for describing what existed in a past scene!

**Formation:** **Il y avait** + noun (singular or plural)

**Examples:**
- Il y **avait** beaucoup de monde. (There were many people.)
- Il y **avait** un grand arbre. (There was a big tree.)
- Il y **avait** trois restaurants. (There were three restaurants.)
- Il y **avait** du bruit. (There was noise.)
- Il n'y **avait** personne. (There was nobody.)
- Il n'y **avait** rien. (There was nothing.)

**Questions:**
- Est-ce qu'il y **avait** beaucoup de gens? (Were there many people?)
- Qu'est-ce qu'il y **avait** dans la maison? (What was there in the house?)
- Il y **avait** un jardin? (Was there a garden?)

**Complete scene description (all elements together):**

C'**était** samedi matin. Il **était** neuf heures. Il **faisait** beau et le soleil **brillait**. Il y **avait** beaucoup de gens dans le parc. Les enfants **jouaient** et les parents **parlaient**. J'**avais** 25 ans à l'époque et j'**habitais** près du parc.

*(It was Saturday morning. It was nine o'clock. The weather was nice and the sun was shining. There were many people in the park. The children were playing and the parents were talking. I was 25 at the time and I lived near the park.)*

**Cultural Note:**
French weather conversation is an art! Just like English speakers, French people love to talk about the weather. "Il faisait quel temps?" (What was the weather like?) is a common conversation starter when discussing trips or events.

### REVIEW SECTION (15 min)

#### Week 9 Grammar Summary

**1. Imparfait Formation (Regular Verbs)**

**Formula:** nous form (present) → drop -ons → add endings

**Endings (all verbs):** -ais, -ais, -ait, -ions, -iez, -aient

**All regular -ER, -IR, -RE verbs:**
- Parler → nous parlons → parl- → je parlais, tu parlais, etc.
- Finir → nous finissons → finiss- → je finissais, tu finissais, etc.
- Vendre → nous vendons → vend- → je vendais, tu vendais, etc.

---

**2. Être - The Only Irregular Verb**

**Stem: ét-** (not from nous sommes!)
- j'étais, tu étais, il/elle était, nous étions, vous étiez, ils/elles étaient

---

**3. Common "Irregular" Verbs (Actually Regular in Imparfait!)**

**Avoir** (stem: av-): j'avais, tu avais, il avait, nous avions, vous aviez, ils avaient

**Faire** (stem: fais-): je faisais, tu faisais, il faisait, nous faisions, vous faisiez, ils faisaient

**Aller** (stem: all-): j'allais, tu allais, il allait, nous allions, vous alliez, ils allaient

**Vouloir** (stem: voul-): je voulais, nous voulions

**Pouvoir** (stem: pouv-): je pouvais, nous pouvions

**Prendre** (stem: pren-): je prenais, nous prenions

---

**4. When to Use Imparfait - Four Main Uses**

**Usage #1: Descriptions (setting the scene)**
- Physical: La maison était grande.
- Mental/Emotional: J'étais content.
- Weather: Il faisait beau.
- Location: Nous étions à Paris.

**Usage #2: Repeated/Habitual Actions**
- Time markers: tous les jours, chaque semaine, toujours, souvent, d'habitude, autrefois
- Example: Je jouais au tennis tous les samedis.
- Translation clue: "used to" or "would" (habitual)

**Usage #3: Ongoing/Progressive Actions**
- Translation: "was/were + -ing"
- Example: Je regardais la télé. (I was watching TV.)
- Often interrupted by passé composé: Je lisais quand tu as appelé.

**Usage #4: Age, Time, Background Info**
- Age: J'avais dix ans.
- Time: Il était midi.
- Background: Il y avait beaucoup de monde.

---

**5. Special Fixed Patterns with Imparfait**

**Weather (ALWAYS imparfait):**
- Il faisait beau/chaud/froid/mauvais
- Il pleuvait / Il neigeait
- Le soleil brillait
- Il y avait du vent/des nuages/du soleil

**Age (ALWAYS avoir in imparfait):**
- J'avais X ans
- Quel âge avais-tu?

**Time (ALWAYS être in imparfait):**
- Il était X heures
- C'était le matin/soir
- C'était lundi

**Il y avait (there was/were):**
- Il y avait + noun (singular or plural)
- Il y avait beaucoup de monde
- Il n'y avait personne

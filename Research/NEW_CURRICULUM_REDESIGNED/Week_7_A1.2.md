### GRAMMAR SECTION (10 min)

#### Explanation

**1. Why the Futur Proche Matters**

The **futur proche** (near future) is THE most common way to talk about future events in everyday French conversation. While French has a formal future tense (futur simple - you'll learn later), native speakers overwhelmingly prefer futur proche for talking about plans, intentions, and things that will happen soon. It's equivalent to English "I'm going to do something" - simple, intuitive, and essential for daily communication!

**2. The Simple Formula: ALLER (present) + Infinitive**

The futur proche is incredibly easy to form - it's even simpler than passé composé!

**Formula:** **ALLER (present tense) + Infinitive verb**

**Example with "manger" (to eat):**
- Je **vais** manger (I'm going to eat / I will eat)
- Auxiliary: "vais" (present tense of aller)
- Main verb: "manger" (infinitive - unchanged!)

**Full conjugation with "manger":**
- Je **vais** manger (I'm going to eat)
- Tu **vas** manger (You're going to eat - singular/informal)
- Il/Elle/On **va** manger (He/She/One is going to eat)
- Nous **allons** manger (We're going to eat)
- Vous **allez** manger (You're going to eat - plural/formal)
- Ils/Elles **vont** manger (They're going to eat)

**Structure breakdown:**
1. **Subject** (Je, Tu, Il...)
2. **ALLER** (conjugated in present: vais, vas, va, allons, allez, vont)
3. **Infinitive verb** (manger, partir, faire, être - never changes!)

**Key advantage:** The main verb stays in infinitive form - no past participles to memorize, no agreement rules!

**3. Using Futur Proche with All Verb Types**

**With -ER verbs:**
- Parler (to speak) → Je **vais parler** à Paul. (I'm going to speak to Paul.)
- Regarder (to watch) → Tu **vas regarder** le film? (Are you going to watch the movie?)
- Travailler (to work) → Elle **va travailler** demain. (She's going to work tomorrow.)

**With -IR verbs:**
- Finir (to finish) → Nous **allons finir** le projet. (We're going to finish the project.)
- Choisir (to choose) → Vous **allez choisir** le menu? (Are you going to choose the menu?)
- Partir (to leave) → Ils **vont partir** à 8h. (They're going to leave at 8am.)

**With -RE verbs:**
- Vendre (to sell) → Je **vais vendre** ma voiture. (I'm going to sell my car.)
- Attendre (to wait) → Tu **vas attendre** ici? (Are you going to wait here?)
- Répondre (to answer) → Il **va répondre** demain. (He's going to answer tomorrow.)

**With irregular verbs:**
- Faire (to do/make) → Je **vais faire** mes devoirs. (I'm going to do my homework.)
- Être (to be) → Tu **vas être** en retard! (You're going to be late!)
- Avoir (to have) → Elle **va avoir** 20 ans. (She's going to be 20 years old.)
- Aller (to go) → Nous **allons aller** à Paris. (We're going to go to Paris.) *Note: aller appears twice!*

**4. Real-World Usage & Examples**

**Scene 1: Making Plans**
- A: Qu'est-ce que tu vas faire ce soir? (What are you going to do tonight?)
- B: Je vais regarder un film à la maison. (I'm going to watch a movie at home.)
- A: Tu vas sortir demain? (Are you going to go out tomorrow?)
- B: Oui, je vais aller au restaurant avec des amis. (Yes, I'm going to go to the restaurant with friends.)

**Scene 2: Discussing Tomorrow**
- A: Qu'est-ce que vous allez faire demain? (What are you going to do tomorrow?)
- B: Nous allons visiter le musée. (We're going to visit the museum.)
- A: Vous allez partir à quelle heure? (What time are you going to leave?)
- B: On va partir à 9h. (We're going to leave at 9am.)

**Scene 3: Weather Predictions**
- A: Il va pleuvoir demain? (Is it going to rain tomorrow?)
- B: Oui, il va pleuvoir toute la journée. (Yes, it's going to rain all day.)
- A: Qu'est-ce que tu vas faire? (What are you going to do?)
- B: Je vais rester à la maison. (I'm going to stay home.)

**Scene 4: Office/School Plans**
- A: Tu vas finir le projet aujourd'hui? (Are you going to finish the project today?)
- B: Non, je vais finir demain. (No, I'm going to finish tomorrow.)
- A: Le professeur va être content? (Is the teacher going to be happy?)
- B: J'espère! (I hope so!)

**Negation in Futur Proche:**
- **Pattern:** ne + aller + pas + infinitive
- Je **ne vais pas** manger ce soir. (I'm not going to eat tonight.)
- Tu **ne vas pas** sortir? (Aren't you going to go out?)
- Il **ne va pas** travailler demain. (He's not going to work tomorrow.)
- Nous **n'allons pas** partir. (We're not going to leave.)

**Questions in Futur Proche:**
- **Intonation:** Tu vas manger? ↗ (Are you going to eat?)
- **Est-ce que:** Est-ce que tu vas manger? (Are you going to eat?)
- **Inversion:** Vas-tu manger? (Are you going to eat? - formal)
- **Qu'est-ce que:** Qu'est-ce que tu vas faire? (What are you going to do?)

**5. Common Errors & Practice Tips**

**Common mistakes to avoid:**
- ❌ Je vais mangé (past participle) → ✅ Je vais manger (infinitive!)
- ❌ Je vais mange (present conjugation) → ✅ Je vais manger (infinitive!)
- ❌ Je vais à manger (adding preposition) → ✅ Je vais manger
- ❌ Il va travaille (conjugated) → ✅ Il va travailler (infinitive!)
- ❌ Nous allons partis (past participle) → ✅ Nous allons partir (infinitive!)

**Memory tips:**
- **ALLER = "going to" literally!** You're already using "aller" (to go) - just add the action!
- **Infinitive = unchanged verb:** manger, finir, vendre - exactly as in dictionary
- **No agreement rules:** Unlike passé composé with être, futur proche never changes the main verb
- Think: "I **am going** (vais) **to eat** (manger)" → Je vais manger

**Time expressions that signal futur proche:**
- Demain (tomorrow)
- Ce soir (tonight)
- La semaine prochaine (next week)
- Bientôt (soon)
- Dans 5 minutes (in 5 minutes)
- Plus tard (later)

**Practice strategy:**
1. Master present tense of aller (vais, vas, va, allons, allez, vont)
2. Keep the main verb in infinitive form (never conjugate it!)
3. Add time expressions: demain, ce soir, bientôt
4. Practice negation: Je ne vais pas + infinitive

**Comparison to English:**
- "I'm going to eat" = Je vais manger ✅
- "I will eat" = Je vais manger ✅ (both translate the same way!)
- French uses futur proche for both meanings

**Next step:** Tomorrow we'll practice using futur proche in various contexts - plans, intentions, predictions!

## DAY 32 - A1.2.32: Futur Proche Practice (Plans & Intentions)

### Metadata
- **CEFR Level:** A1.2
- **Grammar Topic:** Futur proche in context, expressing plans and intentions
- **Vocabulary Domain:** Weekly plans, intentions, scheduled activities
- **Content Identifiers:** futur_proche, plans, intentions, scheduling
- **Speaking Tier:** 2 (Guided conversation)
- **Duration:** 30 minutes
- **Day Number:** 32

---

### GRAMMAR SECTION (10 min)

#### Explanation

**1. Expanding Your Future-Talking Skills**

Now that you understand the basic formula of futur proche, it's time to use it naturally in real-life contexts. Today we'll focus on expressing different types of future actions: **definite plans** ("I'm going to the dentist at 3pm"), **intentions** ("I'm going to start exercising"), **predictions** ("It's going to be difficult"), and **scheduled events** ("The movie is going to start at 8pm"). These nuances will make your French sound more natural and sophisticated!

**2. Context 1: Making Definite Plans**

When you have specific plans already arranged, futur proche is perfect:

**Personal schedules:**
- Je vais aller chez le dentiste demain à 15h. (I'm going to go to the dentist tomorrow at 3pm.)
- Tu vas rencontrer Paul ce soir? (Are you going to meet Paul tonight?)
- Elle va prendre le train à 8h30. (She's going to take the train at 8:30am.)
- Nous allons dîner au restaurant samedi. (We're going to have dinner at the restaurant Saturday.)

**Work/School schedules:**
- Je vais avoir une réunion à 10h. (I'm going to have a meeting at 10am.)
- Tu vas présenter ton projet demain? (Are you going to present your project tomorrow?)
- Le professeur va expliquer la leçon. (The teacher is going to explain the lesson.)
- Nous allons commencer le cours à 9h. (We're going to start the class at 9am.)

**Travel plans:**
- Je vais partir en vacances la semaine prochaine. (I'm going to leave on vacation next week.)
- Vous allez voyager en train ou en avion? (Are you going to travel by train or plane?)
- Ils vont visiter trois pays. (They're going to visit three countries.)
- On va passer deux semaines en France. (We're going to spend two weeks in France.)

**3. Context 2: Expressing Intentions (Not Definite Yet)**

Futur proche also expresses what you *intend* or want to do, even if not firmly scheduled:

**Personal goals:**
- Je vais commencer à faire du sport. (I'm going to start exercising.)
- Tu vas apprendre le français? (Are you going to learn French?)
- Elle va arrêter de fumer. (She's going to stop smoking.)
- Nous allons manger plus sainement. (We're going to eat more healthily.)

**Decisions & resolutions:**
- Je vais chercher un nouveau travail. (I'm going to look for a new job.)
- Tu vas acheter une nouvelle voiture? (Are you going to buy a new car?)
- Il va déménager à Paris. (He's going to move to Paris.)
- Ils vont économiser de l'argent. (They're going to save money.)

**Promises:**
- Je vais t'appeler demain, promis! (I'm going to call you tomorrow, I promise!)
- Tu vas m'aider? (Are you going to help me?)
- Il va venir, ne t'inquiète pas. (He's going to come, don't worry.)
- On va réussir! (We're going to succeed!)

**4. Context 3: Making Predictions**

Use futur proche to predict what will happen (especially imminent events):

**Weather predictions:**
- Il va pleuvoir cet après-midi. (It's going to rain this afternoon.)
- Il va neiger demain. (It's going to snow tomorrow.)
- Il va faire beau ce week-end. (It's going to be nice this weekend.)
- Le temps va changer. (The weather is going to change.)

**Logical predictions:**
- Tu vas être en retard! (You're going to be late!)
- Elle va réussir son examen, j'en suis sûr. (She's going to pass her exam, I'm sure.)
- Ils vont gagner le match. (They're going to win the match.)
- Le film va être intéressant. (The movie is going to be interesting.)

**Warning/Consequence predictions:**
- Attention! Tu vas tomber! (Watch out! You're going to fall!)
- Il va avoir des problèmes. (He's going to have problems.)
- Si tu continues, tu vas être malade. (If you continue, you're going to be sick.)
- Vous allez rater le train! (You're going to miss the train!)

**5. Combining Futur Proche with Other Tenses**

**Past + Future combinations:**
- Hier, j'ai travaillé, mais demain je vais me reposer. (Yesterday I worked, but tomorrow I'm going to rest.)
- Tu as fini tes devoirs? Maintenant tu vas pouvoir sortir. (Did you finish your homework? Now you're going to be able to go out.)
- Elle est allée au marché et elle va préparer le dîner. (She went to the market and she's going to prepare dinner.)

**Present + Future combinations:**
- Je suis fatigué, je vais dormir tôt ce soir. (I'm tired, I'm going to sleep early tonight.)
- Il pleut maintenant, mais il va faire beau demain. (It's raining now, but it's going to be nice tomorrow.)
- Tu as faim? Je vais préparer le repas. (Are you hungry? I'm going to prepare the meal.)

**Useful expressions with futur proche:**
- Je vais voir (I'll see / I'm going to see)
- On va voir (We'll see)
- Ça va être... (It's going to be...)
- Qu'est-ce qui va se passer? (What's going to happen?)
- Comment ça va se passer? (How is it going to happen?)

**Common expressions:**
| French | English | Example |
|--------|---------|---------|
| Je vais essayer | I'm going to try | Je vais essayer de venir. |
| Tu vas voir | You'll see | Tu vas voir, c'est facile! |
| On va s'amuser | We're going to have fun | On va s'amuser ce soir! |
| Ça va aller | It's going to be okay | Ne t'inquiète pas, ça va aller. |
| Qu'est-ce qu'on va faire? | What are we going to do? | Qu'est-ce qu'on va faire demain? |

**Practice dialogue combining contexts:**
```
A: Qu'est-ce que tu vas faire ce week-end?
   (What are you going to do this weekend?)

B: Samedi, je vais aller au marché le matin, et l'après-midi je vais visiter le musée.
   (Saturday, I'm going to go to the market in the morning, and in the afternoon I'm going to visit the museum.)
   
A: Il va faire beau?
   (Is it going to be nice?)
   
B: Oui, d'après la météo, il va faire 25 degrés. Et toi?
   (Yes, according to the weather forecast, it's going to be 25 degrees. And you?)
   
A: Moi, je vais rester à la maison. Je vais commencer à lire un nouveau livre.
   (Me, I'm going to stay home. I'm going to start reading a new book.)
   
B: Quel livre?
   (Which book?)
   
A: Un roman français. Ça va être difficile, mais je vais essayer!
   (A French novel. It's going to be difficult, but I'm going to try!)
```

## DAY 33 - A1.2.33: Time Expressions for Future

### Metadata
- **CEFR Level:** A1.2
- **Grammar Topic:** Comprehensive future time expressions, temporal adverbs
- **Vocabulary Domain:** Time indicators, calendar, scheduling
- **Content Identifiers:** time_expressions, future_time, temporal_adverbs
- **Speaking Tier:** 2 (Guided conversation)
- **Duration:** 30 minutes
- **Day Number:** 33

---

### GRAMMAR SECTION (10 min)

#### Explanation

**1. The Importance of Time Markers**

When talking about the future, time expressions are essential to specify *when* something will happen. Without them, your listener won't know if you mean "in five minutes" or "next year"! French has a rich system of temporal markers that work perfectly with futur proche. Mastering these will make your future-tense conversations precise and natural.

**2. Immediate Future (Very Soon)**

**Within minutes/hours:**
- **Dans + time period** (in/within):
  - Dans 5 minutes (in 5 minutes)
  - Dans une heure (in an hour)
  - Dans 10 secondes (in 10 seconds)
  - Example: Le film va commencer dans 5 minutes. (The movie is going to start in 5 minutes.)

- **Bientôt** (soon):
  - Il va arriver bientôt. (He's going to arrive soon.)
  - On va manger bientôt. (We're going to eat soon.)

- **Tout de suite** (right away/immediately):
  - Je vais venir tout de suite! (I'm going to come right away!)
  - Il va répondre tout de suite. (He's going to answer immediately.)

- **Immédiatement** (immediately):
  - Nous allons partir immédiatement. (We're going to leave immediately.)

**Same day:**
- **Ce matin** (this morning) - *if still morning when speaking*
- **Cet après-midi** (this afternoon)
- **Ce soir** (tonight/this evening)
- **Aujourd'hui** (today)
- **Plus tard** (later):
  - Je vais t'appeler plus tard. (I'm going to call you later.)
  - On va voir plus tard. (We'll see later.)

**3. Near Future (Days & Weeks)**

**Tomorrow & beyond:**
- **Demain** (tomorrow):
  - Demain matin (tomorrow morning)
  - Demain après-midi (tomorrow afternoon)
  - Demain soir (tomorrow evening)
  - Example: Je vais travailler demain. (I'm going to work tomorrow.)

- **Après-demain** (day after tomorrow):
  - On va partir après-demain. (We're going to leave the day after tomorrow.)

**This/Next week:**
- **Cette semaine** (this week):
  - Je vais finir le projet cette semaine. (I'm going to finish the project this week.)

- **La semaine prochaine** (next week):
  - Nous allons partir la semaine prochaine. (We're going to leave next week.)

- **Dans une semaine** (in a week):
  - Elle va revenir dans une semaine. (She's going to come back in a week.)

**Days of the week:**
- **Lundi/Mardi/Mercredi/Jeudi/Vendredi/Samedi/Dimanche**
  - Lundi prochain (next Monday)
  - Samedi prochain (next Saturday)
  - Example: Je vais voir le dentiste vendredi. (I'm going to see the dentist Friday.)

**4. Mid-Range Future (Months)**

**This/Next month:**
- **Ce mois-ci** (this month):
  - Nous allons déménager ce mois-ci. (We're going to move this month.)

- **Le mois prochain** (next month):
  - Je vais commencer mon nouveau travail le mois prochain. (I'm going to start my new job next month.)

- **Dans un mois** (in a month):
  - Tu vas finir dans un mois? (Are you going to finish in a month?)

**Seasons:**
- **Cet été** (this summer)
- **Cet automne** (this fall)
- **Cet hiver** (this winter)
- **Ce printemps** (this spring)
- **L'été prochain** (next summer)
- **L'hiver prochain** (next winter)
  - Example: Nous allons voyager en Italie cet été. (We're going to travel to Italy this summer.)

**5. Long-Range Future & Advanced Time Expressions**

**Years:**
- **Cette année** (this year):
  - Je vais apprendre le français cette année. (I'm going to learn French this year.)

- **L'année prochaine** (next year):
  - Elle va habiter à Paris l'année prochaine. (She's going to live in Paris next year.)

- **Dans deux ans** (in two years):
  - Ils vont avoir un bébé dans deux ans. (They're going to have a baby in two years.)

**Duration expressions with "pendant" (for/during):**
- Je vais rester pendant une semaine. (I'm going to stay for a week.)
- Nous allons étudier pendant deux heures. (We're going to study for two hours.)

**Complete time expression chart:**

|-----------|------------|---------|

## DAY 34 - A1.2.34: Futur Proche vs. Present Tense

### Metadata
- **CEFR Level:** A1.2
- **Grammar Topic:** Contrasting futur proche with present tense, habitual vs. future actions
- **Vocabulary Domain:** Daily routines, habits, future plans
- **Content Identifiers:** futur_proche_vs_present, habits, future_actions, contrast
- **Speaking Tier:** 2 (Guided conversation)
- **Duration:** 30 minutes
- **Day Number:** 34

---

### GRAMMAR SECTION (10 min)

#### Explanation

**1. Understanding the Key Difference**

Now that you're comfortable with futur proche, it's crucial to distinguish when to use it versus the **present tense**. The difference is subtle but important: **present tense** describes habitual actions, general truths, and things happening right now, while **futur proche** describes specific future actions. Sometimes the context is the only clue! Mastering this distinction will make your French sound natural and precise.

**2. Present Tense: Habits, Routines, and General Truths**

**Habitual actions (things you do regularly):**
- Je travaille tous les jours. (I work every day.) - *present tense*
- Tu manges au restaurant le samedi. (You eat at the restaurant on Saturdays.) - *present tense*
- Elle va au cinéma chaque semaine. (She goes to the movies every week.) - *present tense*
- Nous étudions le lundi. (We study on Mondays.) - *present tense*

**Key indicators of habitual present:**
- **Tous les jours / chaque jour** (every day)
- **Toutes les semaines** (every week)
- **Le lundi / le samedi** (on Mondays / on Saturdays - *habitual*)
- **Souvent** (often)
- **Toujours** (always)
- **D'habitude** (usually)
- **Généralement** (generally)

**Examples:**
- Je vais au travail en métro tous les jours. (I go to work by metro every day.)
- Elle fait du sport trois fois par semaine. (She does sports three times a week.)
- Nous mangeons ensemble le dimanche. (We eat together on Sundays.)

**General truths and facts:**
- La Terre tourne autour du Soleil. (The Earth revolves around the Sun.) - *present*
- Paris est la capitale de la France. (Paris is the capital of France.) - *present*
- Les chats aiment dormir. (Cats like to sleep.) - *present*

**3. Futur Proche: Specific Future Actions**

**One-time future events:**
- Je vais travailler demain. (I'm going to work tomorrow.) - *futur proche*
- Tu vas manger au restaurant ce soir. (You're going to eat at the restaurant tonight.) - *futur proche*
- Elle va aller au cinéma samedi. (She's going to go to the movies Saturday.) - *futur proche*
- Nous allons étudier lundi prochain. (We're going to study next Monday.) - *futur proche*

**Key indicators of futur proche:**
- **Demain** (tomorrow)
- **Ce soir** (tonight/this evening)
- **La semaine prochaine** (next week)
- **Samedi prochain** (next Saturday - *specific*)
- **Dans X temps** (in X time)
- **Bientôt** (soon)

**Examples:**
- Je vais aller au travail en taxi demain. (I'm going to go to work by taxi tomorrow.) - *specific occasion*
- Elle va faire du sport samedi matin. (She's going to do sports Saturday morning.) - *one time*
- Nous allons manger ensemble dimanche prochain. (We're going to eat together next Sunday.) - *specific date*

**4. Direct Comparisons: Same Context, Different Meanings**

**Habitual (present) vs. Future (futur proche):**

| Present (Habit) | Futur Proche (Specific Future) |
|----------------|-------------------------------|
| Je mange au restaurant **le samedi**. (I eat at the restaurant **on Saturdays**.) - *every Saturday* | Je vais manger au restaurant **samedi**. (I'm going to eat at the restaurant **Saturday**.) - *this Saturday* |
| Elle travaille **tous les jours**. (She works **every day**.) - *routine* | Elle va travailler **demain**. (She's going to work **tomorrow**.) - *one specific day* |
| Nous allons au cinéma **chaque semaine**. (We go to the movies **every week**.) - *habitual* | Nous allons aller au cinéma **la semaine prochaine**. (We're going to go to the movies **next week**.) - *one specific week* |
| Tu fais du sport **le lundi et le mercredi**. (You do sports **on Mondays and Wednesdays**.) - *routine* | Tu vas faire du sport **lundi prochain**. (You're going to do sports **next Monday**.) - *one Monday* |
| Je prends le bus **tous les matins**. (I take the bus **every morning**.) - *habit* | Je vais prendre le bus **demain matin**. (I'm going to take the bus **tomorrow morning**.) - *one time* |

**Dialogue contrasting both:**
```
A: Tu manges souvent au restaurant?
   (Do you eat at the restaurant often?) - asking about habits
   
B: Oui, je mange au restaurant tous les vendredis.
   (Yes, I eat at the restaurant every Friday.) - present for habit
   
A: Et ce vendredi? Tu vas manger au restaurant aussi?
   (And this Friday? Are you going to eat at the restaurant too?) - asking about specific future
   
B: Non, ce vendredi je vais manger à la maison. Je vais préparer un repas spécial.
   (No, this Friday I'm going to eat at home. I'm going to prepare a special meal.) - futur proche for specific plan
```

**5. Common Situations Where Both Tenses Appear Together**

**Explaining routine vs. exception:**
- D'habitude, je **prends** le train, mais demain je **vais prendre** la voiture. (Usually I take the train, but tomorrow I'm going to take the car.)
- Généralement, elle **travaille** à la maison, mais la semaine prochaine elle **va travailler** au bureau. (Generally she works at home, but next week she's going to work at the office.)
- Normalement, nous **mangeons** à 19h, mais ce soir nous **allons manger** à 20h. (Normally we eat at 7pm, but tonight we're going to eat at 8pm.)

**Describing schedule changes:**
- Le lundi je **travaille** de 9h à 17h. (On Mondays I work from 9am to 5pm.) - *habit*
- Mais lundi prochain, je **vais travailler** de 10h à 18h. (But next Monday, I'm going to work from 10am to 6pm.) - *specific change*

**Present continuous (right now) vs. Future:**
- Maintenant je **mange**, mais dans une heure je **vais travailler**. (Now I'm eating, but in an hour I'm going to work.)
- Il **parle** au téléphone maintenant, mais après il **va partir**. (He's speaking on the phone now, but afterwards he's going to leave.)

**Practice sentences - identify tense:**
1. Je vais au travail en métro tous les jours. → **PRESENT** (tous les jours = habit)
2. Je vais aller au travail en taxi demain. → **FUTUR PROCHE** (demain = specific future)
3. Nous mangeons ensemble le dimanche. → **PRESENT** (le dimanche = every Sunday)
4. Nous allons manger ensemble dimanche. → **FUTUR PROCHE** (dimanche = this Sunday)
5. Elle fait du yoga chaque matin. → **PRESENT** (chaque matin = habit)
6. Elle va faire du yoga demain matin. → **FUTUR PROCHE** (demain matin = specific)

**Key takeaway:** Pay attention to time expressions! They're your best clue for choosing the correct tense.

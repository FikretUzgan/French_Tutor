### Metadata

---

### GRAMMAR SECTION (10 min)

#### Explanation

**1. What is the Passé Simple?**
The **Passé Simple** is a literary past tense. You will **read** it in books, history texts, and fairy tales, but you will almost **never speak** it (unless you are giving a very formal speech). It replaces the Passé Composé in writing to describe completed actions.

- Spoken: "Il **a parlé**." (He spoke.)
- Written: "Il **parla**." (He spoke.)

**2. Formation: -ER Verbs**
Remove *-er* and add: **-ai, -as, -a, -âmes, -âtes, -èrent**.

- **Parler (to speak):**
  - Je parl**ai**
  - Tu parl**as**
  - Il/Elle parl**a** (Most common!)
  - Nous parl**âmes**
  - Vous parl**âtes**
  - Ils/Elles parl**èrent**

**3. Formation: -IR & -RE Verbs**
Remove *-ir/-re* and add: **-is, -is, -it, -îmes, -îtes, -irent**.

- **Finir (to finish):**
  - Je fin**is**
  - Tu fin**is**
  - Il fin**it** (Looks like Present!)
  - Nous fin**îmes**
  - Vous fin**îtes**
  - Ils fin**irent**

**4. When to Use It?**
Strictly for **narrations**. It moves the story forward.
"Il **entra**, il **regarda** la salle et il **vit** le roi." (He entered, looked at the room, and saw the king.)

**Cultural Note:**
Don't use this in conversation! If you say "Je parlai" to a French person, they will think you are a walking 19th-century novel. Stick to Passé Composé for speech.

### GRAMMAR SECTION (10 min)

#### Explanation

**1. The "U" Family (Ending in -us, -us, -ut)**
Many irregular verbs use a "U" sound in Passé Simple.

- **Être (to be) -> Fus** (Je fus, tu fus, il **fut**, nous fûmes, vous fûtes, ils **furent**)
- **Avoir (to have) -> Eus** (J'eus, tu eus, il **eut**, ils eurent) [Pronounced "u"]
- **Pouvoir -> Put**
- **Vouloir -> Voulut**
- **Savoir -> Sut**
- **Devoir -> Dut**
- **Lire -> Lut**
- **Boire -> But**
- **Courir -> Courut**
- **Connaître -> Connut**

**2. The "I" Family (Ending in -is, -is, -it)**
Some follow the -IR pattern even if they are irregular.

- **Faire -> Fit** (Il fit, ils firent)
- **Dire -> Dit** (Il dit, ils dirent)
- **Mettre -> Mit** (Il mit, ils mirent)
- **Prendre -> Prit** (Il prit, ils prirent)
- **Voir -> Vit** (Il vit, ils virent)

**3. The "IN" Family (Venir/Tenir)**
These are unique.

- **Venir (to come) -> Vins** (Je vins, il **vint**, ils **vinrent**)
- **Tenir (to hold) -> Tins** (Je tins, il **tint**, ils **tinrent**)

**4. Reading Strategy**
When you see words like "fut", "eut", "fit", "vint" in a text - recognize them as the past tense actions of major verbs.

## DAY 83 - B1.1.83: Plus-que-parfait (Past Perfect - Formation)

### Metadata

- **CEFR Level:** B1.1
- **Grammar Topic:** The "Past of the Past"
- **Vocabulary Domain:** Time / Sequence
- **Content Identifiers:** plus_que_parfait, grammar_formation, past_tense_advanced
- **Speaking Tier:** 3
- **Duration:** 30 minutes
- **Day Number:** 83

---

### GRAMMAR SECTION (10 min)

#### Explanation

**1. What is Plus-que-parfait?**
It corresponds to the English **"Had done"**. It describes an action that happened **before** another past action.

- "When I arrived (past), he **had already left** (past perfect)."
- "Quand je suis arrivé, il **était déjà parti**."

**2. Formation Formula**
**Auxiliary (Avoir/Être in IMPARFAIT)** + **Past Participle**

**3. Choosing the Auxiliary**
Same rules as Passé Composé!

- Most verbs use **Avoir** (imparfait: j'avais, tu avais...)
- DR MRS VANDERTRAMP + Reflexives use **Être** (imparfait: j'étais, tu étais...)

**4. Examples**

- **Manger (Avoir):**
  - J'**avais** mangé (I had eaten)
  - Tu **avais** mangé
  - Il **avait** mangé

- **Partir (Être):**
  - J'**étais** parti(e) (I had left)
  - Il **était** parti
  - Nous **étions** parti(e)s

**5. Negation**
Sandwich the auxiliary: "Je **n'**avais **pas** mangé." (I hadn't eaten.)

**1. The Timeline of the Past**
Imagine a timeline going backwards from NOW.

1. **NOW** (Présent)
2. **PAST** (Passé Composé / Imparfait) -> "Hier, j'ai mangé."
3. **WAY BACK** (Plus-que-parfait) -> "Avant ça, j'**avais** cuisiné."

**2. Combining Tenses**
We use PQP to explain the **cause** or **condition** of a past event.

- Event 2 (Result - PC): "Je n'ai pas eu faim..." (I wasn't hungry...)
- Event 1 (Cause - PQP): "...parce que j'**avais** trop **mangé**." (...because I **had eaten** too much.)

**3. "Si" Clauses with Plus-que-parfait (Hypothetical Past)**
We will study this fully in Conditionnel Passé, but know this structure:

- **Si + Plus-que-parfait** expresses regret.
- "Si j'**avais su**!" (If I **had known**!)

**4. Key Triggers**
Look for these words that signal PQP:

- **Parce que** (Because -> explaining earlier cause)
- **Déjà** (Already)
- **La veille** (The day before)

### GRAMMAR REVIEW (15 min)

**1. Passé Simple (Reading Only)**

- Use in literature/history.
- **-ER:** -ai, -as, -a, -âmes, -âtes, -èrent (Il parla)
- **-IR/-RE:** -is, -is, -it, -îmes, -îtes, -irent (Il finit)
- **Irregular U:** Il fut (être), il eut (avoir), il put (pouvoir), il sut (savoir).
- **Irregular I:** Il fit (faire), il vit (voir), il prit (prendre).
- **Irregular IN:** Il vint (venir).

**2. Plus-que-parfait (Had done)**

- Use to express "past of the past".
- **Formation:** Imparfait of Avoir/Être + Past Participle.
- **Example:** J'**avais** mangé. Elle **était** partie.
- **Key usage:** Explaining the cause of a past event. "J'étais malade car j'avais mangé..."

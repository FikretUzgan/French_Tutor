### GRAMMAR SECTION (10 min)

#### Explanation

**1. What is Conditionnel PrÃ©sent?**

The **conditionnel prÃ©sent** is a mood used to express:

- **Politeness** ("I would like...")
- **Wishes/Desires** ("I would love to...")
- **Hypothetical situations** ("It would be nice...")
- **Advice** ("You should...")

It is equivalent to the English "would" + verb.

**2. Formation: A Simple Formula**

The conditionnel simple is formed by combining the **Futur Simple STEM** with the **Imparfait ENDINGS**.

**Formula:** `Futur Stem` + `Imparfait Endings`

**Reminders:**

- **Futur Stem:** Usually the infinitive (parler-, finir-) or irregular stem (ser-, aur-, ir-).
- **Imparfait Endings:** -ais, -ais, -ait, -ions, -iez, -aient.

**3. Regular Verbs (manger, finir, vendre)**

| Personne | Ending | Manger (to eat) | Finir (to finish) | Vendre (to sell) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Je | -ais | Je manger**ais** | Je finir**ais** | Je vendr**ais** |
| Tu | -ais | Tu manger**ais** | Tu finir**ais** | Tu vendr**ais** |
| Il/Elle/On | -ait | Il manger**ait** | Il finir**ait** | Il vendr**ait** |
| Nous | -ions | Nous manger**ions** | Nous finir**ions** | Nous vendr**ions** |
| Vous | -iez | Vous manger**iez** | Vous finir**iez** | Vous vendr**iez** |
| Ils/Elles | -aient | Ils manger**aient** | Ils finir**aient** | Ils vendr**aient** |

**4. Irregular Verbs (Same stems as Futur Simple)**

The stems are exactly the same as the Futur Simple you learned last week.

- **ÃŠtre:** ser- (Je serais - I would be)
- **Avoir:** aur- (J'aurais - I would have)
- **Aller:** ir- (J'irais - I would go)
- **Faire:** fer- (Je ferais - I would do)
- **Pouvoir:** pourr- (Je pourrais - I could/would be able to)
- **Vouloir:** voudr- (Je voudrais - I would like)
- **Devoir:** devr- (Je devrais - I should)

**5. Pronunciation Note**

The endings -ais, -ais, -ait, and -aient are all pronounced **"eh"** (like 'bed').
Only -ions and -iez sound different.
Note that the 'r' of the future stem must be clearly heard before the ending.

**Cultural Note:**
Using the conditional is essential for "la politesse" in France. Saying "Je veux" (I want) can sound rude. Always prefer "Je voudrais" (I would like).

**C) Aimer (To like) -> J'aimerais**

**Cultural Note:**

### GRAMMAR SECTION (10 min)

#### Explanation

**1. The "Si" (If) Formulas**

There are two main "Si" formulas for A2 level. You know the first one. Today we learn the second one.

**Formula 1 (Likely / Real):** Review
`Si` + `PrÃ©sent` + `Futur (Simple or Proche)`

- "Si j'**ai** le temps, je **viendrai**." (If I **have** time, I **will come**.) -> This is likely to happen.

**Formula 2 (Hypothetical / Unreal / Imagination):** NEW!
`Si` + `Imparfait` + `Conditionnel`

- "Si j'**avais** le temps, je **viendrais**." (If I **had** time, I **would come**.) -> But I don't have time, so I won't come.
- "Si j'**Ã©tais** riche, j'**achÃ¨terais** un chÃ¢teau." (If I **were** rich, I **would buy** a castle.)

**2. How to Construct It**

1. Start with **"Si"**.
2. Verb after "si" MUST be in **Imparfait**. (NEVER Conditional after Si!)
3. The other verb (consequence) is in **Conditionnel**.

**Rule:** **"Si" eats the "R".** (Because conditional has an 'r' sound, but imparfait doesn't. You never hear the 'R' immediately after 'Si').

- WRONG: Si j'aurais... (If I would have...) -> Strictly forbidden!
- RIGHT: Si j'avais... (If I had...)

**3. Changing the Order**
You can flip the sentence:

- "J'**achÃ¨terais** un chÃ¢teau si j'**Ã©tais** riche."
- Meaning is the same.

**4. "Si j'Ã©tais toi" (If I were you)**

- "Si j'Ã©tais toi, je ferais du sport."
- Common way to give advice.

**Cultural Note:**
Mixing up the tenses in Si-clauses is a very common mistake even for French children. Remember: **Si + Imparfait = Conditionnel**.

- **Advice:** "Tu devrais..."
- **Hypothetical/Imaginary:** "Ce serait super."

**3. Common Errors Reminder**

## DAY 1 - A1.1.1: Verb Être (I am, You are) + Greetings

### Metadata
- **CEFR Level:** A1.1
- **Grammar Topic:** Verb être - je suis, tu es, il/elle est
- **Vocabulary Domain:** Greetings and Basic Phrases
- **Content Identifiers:** conjugation, fill_blank, listening, dialogue
- **Speaking Tier:** 1 (Script-based repetition)
- **Duration:** 30 minutes
- **Day Number:** 1

---

### GRAMMAR SECTION (10 min)

#### Explanation

**1. Definition: What is Être?**

The verb **être** means "to be" in English. It is THE most important verb in French because it helps you introduce yourself, describe things, and talk about where you are. Just like "I am," "you are," "he is" in English, French uses different forms: je suis, tu es, il est. This is called **conjugation**—changing the verb to match who is speaking.

**2. Formation & Basic Conjugation - Être (Present Tense)**

| Pronoun | Être Form | English | Example |
|---------|-----------|---------|---------|
| je | suis | I am | Je suis Marie (I am Marie) |
| tu | es | you are (informal) | Tu es français (You are French) |
| il | est | he is | Il est étudiant (He is a student) |
| elle | est | she is | Elle est professeur (She is a teacher) |
| on | est | we/one (informal) | On est content (We are happy) |

**Today's focus:** Only je, tu, il, elle (the basics for personal introduction)

**3. Common Patterns & Use Cases**

- **Introducing yourself:** "Je suis [name]" → "Je suis Pierre" (I am Pierre)
- **Nationality:** "Je suis français/française" (I am French - masc/fem)
- **Profession:** "Je suis étudiant/étudiante" (I am a student)
- **State/Feeling:** "Je suis content/contente" (I am happy)
- **Location (coming soon):** "Je suis à Paris" (I am in Paris) - introduced Day 3

**4. Real-World Dialogue Examples**

**Scene 1: First Meeting**
- A: "Bonjour! Je suis Marie. Et toi?" (Hello! I am Marie. And you?)
- B: "Bonjour! Je suis Pierre." (Hello! I am Pierre.)
- A: "Tu es français?" (Are you French?)
- B: "Oui, je suis français." (Yes, I am French.)

**Scene 2: Classroom Introduction**
- Teacher: "Bonjour! Je suis Madame Dupont." (Hello! I am Mrs. Dupont.)
- Student: "Bonjour, Madame. Je suis étudiant." (Hello, Ma'am. I am a student.)

**5. Common Errors & Next Steps**

- ❌ "Je être Pierre" → ✅ "Je suis Pierre" (Must conjugate the verb, don't use infinitive)
- ❌ "Tu suis français" → ✅ "Tu es français" (Each pronoun has its own form)
- ❌ "Elle suis contente" → ✅ "Elle est contente" (elle = est, not suis)
- **Next step:** Tomorrow we add nous, vous, ils, elles (plural forms) and learn avoir (to have)

### CULTURAL NOTE

**French Greetings:**
In France, "Bonjour" is used during the day (morning/afternoon) when meeting someone. It's polite and essential! "Salut" is informal (for friends). When you meet someone for the first time, you say "Bonjour! Je suis [name]" and often shake hands. The French value politeness, so always start with "Bonjour!"

## DAY 2 - A1.1.2: Verb Être (Plural Forms) + Basic Introductions

### Metadata
- **CEFR Level:** A1.1
- **Grammar Topic:** Verb être - nous sommes, vous êtes, ils/elles sont
- **Vocabulary Domain:** Introductions, Plural Pronouns
- **Content Identifiers:** conjugation, fill_blank, listening, dialogue
- **Speaking Tier:** 1 (Script-based with minimal variation)
- **Duration:** 30 minutes
- **Day Number:** 2

---

### GRAMMAR SECTION (10 min)

#### Explanation

**1. Être - Complete Conjugation (All Forms)**

Yesterday we learned je suis, tu es, il/elle est. Today we complete the conjugation with plural forms: nous, vous, ils, elles. These are used when talking about groups or addressing multiple people formally.

**2. Full Conjugation Table - Être (Present Tense)**

| Pronoun | Être Form | English | Example |
|---------|-----------|---------|---------|
| je | suis | I am | Je suis Pierre |
| tu | es | you are (informal singular) | Tu es français |
| il | est | he is | Il est professeur |
| elle | est | she is | Elle est étudiante |
| nous | sommes | we are | Nous sommes content**s** |
| vous | êtes | you are (formal/plural) | Vous êtes français |
| ils | sont | they are (masc/mixed) | Ils sont étudiants |
| elles | sont | they are (feminine) | Elles sont françaises |

**Key difference:** Nous/vous/ils/elles are for groups or formal address

**3. Common Patterns & Use Cases**

- **Talking about groups:** "Nous sommes étudiants" (We are students)
- **Formal address:** "Vous êtes Madame Dupont?" (Are you Mrs. Dupont? - polite)
- **Plural groups:** "Ils sont français" (They are French - masculine or mixed group)
- **Feminine groups:** "Elles sont étudiantes" (They are students - all feminine)
- **Politeness:** Always use "vous" with strangers, teachers, older people

**4. Real-World Dialogue Examples**

**Scene 1: Meeting in a Café**
- A: "Bonjour! Vous êtes français?" (Hello! Are you French? - formal/polite)
- B: "Oui, nous sommes français. Et vous?" (Yes, we are French. And you?)
- A: "Nous sommes américains." (We are American.)

**Scene 2: Classroom**
- Teacher: "Vous êtes étudiants?" (Are you students? - addressing class)
- Students: "Oui, nous sommes étudiants." (Yes, we are students.)

**Scene 3: Talking About Friends**
- A: "Pierre et Marie sont ici?" (Are Pierre and Marie here?)
- B: "Oui, ils sont ici." (Yes, they are here.)

**5. Common Errors & Next Steps**

- ❌ "Nous est étudiants" → ✅ "Nous sommes étudiants" (nous = sommes, always)
- ❌ "Vous sont français" → ✅ "Vous êtes français" (vous = êtes)
- ❌ "Ils es français" → ✅ "Ils sont français" (ils/elles = sont)
- **Next step:** Tomorrow we learn avoir (to have) - another essential verb!

### CULTURAL NOTE

**Tu vs. Vous:**
In French, "tu" is informal (friends, family, children) and "vous" is formal (strangers, teachers, bosses, elderly people). When meeting someone new, always use "vous" until they say "On peut se tutoyer?" (Can we use 'tu'?). It's a sign of respect!

## DAY 3 - A1.1.3: Verb Avoir (To Have) + Basic Possessions

### Metadata
- **CEFR Level:** A1.1
- **Grammar Topic:** Verb avoir - present tense conjugation
- **Vocabulary Domain:** Basic possessions, age
- **Content Identifiers:** conjugation, fill_blank, listening, dialogue
- **Speaking Tier:** 1 (Script-based with guided variation)
- **Duration:** 30 minutes
- **Day Number:** 3

---

### GRAMMAR SECTION (10 min)

#### Explanation

**1. Definition: What is Avoir?**

The verb **avoir** means "to have" in English. It is the second most important verb in French (after être). We use avoir to talk about possessions (I have a book), age (I am 25 years old → J'ai 25 ans), and many expressions. Like être, avoir changes for each pronoun.

**2. Full Conjugation Table - Avoir (Present Tense)**

| Pronoun | Avoir Form | English | Example |
|---------|------------|---------|---------|
| j' (je) | ai | I have | J'ai un livre (I have a book) |
| tu | as | you have | Tu as un chat (You have a cat) |
| il | a | he has | Il a 20 ans (He is 20 years old) |
| elle | a | she has | Elle a un ami (She has a friend) |
| nous | avons | we have | Nous avons un chien (We have a dog) |
| vous | avez | you have (formal/plural) | Vous avez une voiture (You have a car) |
| ils | ont | they have (masc/mixed) | Ils ont des livres (They have books) |
| elles | ont | they have (feminine) | Elles ont des amis (They have friends) |

**Note:** Before a vowel, "je" becomes "j'" → j'ai (not je ai)

**3. Common Patterns & Use Cases**

- **Age:** "J'ai 25 ans" (I am 25 years old - literally "I have 25 years")
- **Possessions:** "J'ai un livre" (I have a book)
- **Family:** "J'ai deux sœurs" (I have two sisters)
- **Feelings (expressions):** "J'ai faim" (I am hungry - literally "I have hunger")
- **Questions:** "Tu as un chat?" (Do you have a cat?)

**4. Real-World Dialogue Examples**

**Scene 1: Talking About Age**
- A: "Tu as quel âge?" (How old are you? - literally "You have what age?")
- B: "J'ai 20 ans. Et toi?" (I am 20 years old. And you?)
- A: "J'ai 22 ans." (I am 22.)

**Scene 2: Possessions**
- A: "Tu as un livre?" (Do you have a book?)
- B: "Oui, j'ai un livre français." (Yes, I have a French book.)

**Scene 3: Family**
- A: "Vous avez des enfants?" (Do you have children? - formal)
- B: "Oui, j'ai deux enfants." (Yes, I have two children.)

**5. Common Errors & Next Steps**

- ❌ "Je avoir un livre" → ✅ "J'ai un livre" (must conjugate)
- ❌ "Tu ai 20 ans" → ✅ "Tu as 20 ans" (tu = as, not ai)
- ❌ "Il ont un chat" → ✅ "Il a un chat" (il = a, ils = ont)
- ❌ "Je ai un ami" → ✅ "J'ai un ami" (je → j' before vowel)
- **Next step:** Tomorrow we learn regular -ER verbs (parler, habiter, etc.)

## DAY 4 - A1.1.4: Regular -ER Verbs (Parler, Habiter, Aimer)

### Metadata
- **CEFR Level:** A1.1
- **Grammar Topic:** Regular -ER verb conjugation (present tense)
- **Vocabulary Domain:** Actions, daily activities
- **Content Identifiers:** conjugation, fill_blank, listening, dialogue
- **Speaking Tier:** 1 (Script-based with more freedom)
- **Duration:** 30 minutes
- **Day Number:** 4

---

### GRAMMAR SECTION (10 min)

#### Explanation

**1. What are -ER Verbs?**

Most French verbs end in **-ER** in their infinitive form (the base form, like "to speak" in English). These are called **regular -ER verbs** because they all follow the same conjugation pattern. Examples: parler (to speak), habiter (to live), aimer (to like/love). Once you learn the pattern, you can conjugate hundreds of verbs!

**2. How to Conjugate Regular -ER Verbs**

**Steps:**
1. Take the infinitive: **parl**er
2. Remove -er: **parl** (this is the stem)
3. Add the endings: -e, -es, -e, -ons, -ez, -ent

**Example: Parler (to speak)**

| Pronoun | Stem + Ending | English |
|---------|---------------|---------|
| je | parle | I speak |
| tu | parles | you speak |
| il/elle | parle | he/she speaks |
| nous | parlons | we speak |
| vous | parlez | you speak (formal/plural) |
| ils/elles | parlent | they speak |

**Pattern for ALL -ER verbs:** -e, -es, -e, -ons, -ez, -ent

**3. Common -ER Verbs & Examples**

| Infinitive | Stem | je form | English |
|------------|------|---------|---------|
| parler | parl- | je parle | I speak |
| habiter | habit- | j'habite | I live |
| aimer | aim- | j'aime | I like/love |
| étudier | étudi- | j'étudie | I study |
| travailler | travaill- | je travaille | I work |

**Examples in sentences:**
- "Je parle français." (I speak French.)
- "Tu habites à Paris?" (Do you live in Paris?)
- "Il aime le café." (He likes coffee.)
- "Nous étudions tous les jours." (We study every day.)

**4. Real-World Dialogue**

**Scene 1: Getting to Know Someone**
- A: "Tu parles français?" (Do you speak French?)
- B: "Oui, je parle français. Et toi?" (Yes, I speak French. And you?)
- A: "Je parle un peu." (I speak a little.)

**Scene 2: Where You Live**
- A: "Vous habitez où?" (Where do you live? - formal)
- B: "J'habite à Paris. Et vous?" (I live in Paris. And you?)
- A: "J'habite à Lyon." (I live in Lyon.)

**Scene 3: Likes and Hobbies**
- A: "Tu aimes le café?" (Do you like coffee?)
- B: "Oui, j'aime beaucoup le café!" (Yes, I really like coffee!)

**5. Common Errors & Next Steps**

- ❌ "Je parler français" → ✅ "Je parle français" (must remove -er and add -e)
- ❌ "Tu parle français" → ✅ "Tu parles français" (tu = -es ending)
- ❌ "Nous parle" → ✅ "Nous parlons" (nous = -ons)
- ❌ "Ils parlez" → ✅ "Ils parlent" (ils = -ent, not -ez)
- **Next step:** Tomorrow we learn articles (le, la, les) and gender agreement

## DAY 5 - A1.1.5: Articles (Le, La, Les, Un, Une, Des) + Gender

### Metadata
- **CEFR Level:** A1.1
- **Grammar Topic:** Definite and indefinite articles, noun gender
- **Vocabulary Domain:** Common nouns, objects
- **Content Identifiers:** grammar, fill_blank, listening, multiple_choice
- **Speaking Tier:** 1 (Script with slight variation)
- **Duration:** 30 minutes
- **Day Number:** 5

---

### GRAMMAR SECTION (10 min)

#### Explanation

**1. What are Articles?**

In French, every noun has a **gender** (masculine or feminine) and requires an **article** (like "the" or "a" in English). There are two types: **definite articles** (le, la, les = the) and **indefinite articles** (un, une, des = a/an/some). You must always use an article with nouns in French.

**2. Definite Articles (The)**

| Gender | Singular | Plural | Example |
|--------|----------|--------|---------|
| Masculine | le | les | le livre (the book) |
| Feminine | la | les | la table (the table) |
| Vowel starts* | l' | les | l'ami (the friend - masc) |

*Before a vowel or silent H, use **l'** instead of le/la

- "Le café est bon." (The coffee is good.)
- "La maison est grande." (The house is big.)
- "Les livres sont ici." (The books are here.)

**3. Indefinite Articles (A / An / Some)**

| Gender | Singular | Plural | Example |
|--------|----------|--------|---------|
| Masculine | un | des | un livre (a book) |
| Feminine | une | des | une table (a table) |

- "J'ai un livre." (I have a book.)
- "Tu as une voiture." (You have a car.)
- "Nous avons des amis." (We have friends/some friends.)

**4. How to Know Gender?**

Unfortunately, there's no perfect rule! You must **memorize** the gender with each noun. However, some patterns help:

**Often Masculine:**
- Words ending in: -age, -ment, -eau, -phone, -scope
- Examples: le fromage (cheese), le moment, le bureau

**Often Feminine:**
- Words ending in: -tion, -sion, -té, -ette, -ence
- Examples: la nation, la mission, la liberté

**Best strategy:** Always learn nouns with their article: "un livre" not just "livre"

**5. Real-World Examples**

- "J'aime **le** café." (I like coffee - in general)
- "Je veux **un** café." (I want a coffee - one coffee)
- "**La** maison est grande." (The house is big.)
- "J'ai **une** voiture." (I have a car.)
- "**Les** étudiants parlent français." (The students speak French.)

**Common Errors:**
- ❌ "J'ai livre" → ✅ "J'ai un livre" (must have article)
- ❌ "Le table" → ✅ "La table" (table is feminine)
- ❌ "Les livre" → ✅ "Les livres" (plural needs -s on noun too)

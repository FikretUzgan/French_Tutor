### GRAMMAR SECTION (10 min)

#### Explanation (5-paragraph teacher format)

**1. What are -IR Verbs?**

The second family of regular verbs in French ends in **-IR** in their infinitive form. Like -ER verbs, they follow a predictable pattern once you learn it. Common -IR verbs include: **finir** (to finish), **choisir** (to choose), **réussir** (to succeed), **grandir** (to grow). These verbs describe actions, achievements, and transformations. Learning this pattern gives you access to dozens of useful verbs!

**2. How to Conjugate Regular -IR Verbs**

**Steps:**
1. Take the infinitive: **fin**ir
2. Remove -ir: **fin** (this is the stem)
3. Add the endings: -is, -is, -it, -issons, -issez, -issent

**Example: Finir (to finish)**

| Pronoun | Stem + Ending | Pronunciation | English |
|---------|---------------|---------------|---------|
| je | finis | fee-nee | I finish |
| tu | finis | fee-nee | you finish |
| il/elle | finit | fee-nee | he/she finishes |
| nous | finissons | fee-nee-son | we finish |
| vous | finissez | fee-nee-say | you finish (formal/plural) |
| ils/elles | finissent | fee-nees | they finish |

**Key pattern:** Notice the **-iss-** appears in nous, vous, ils/elles forms!

**3. Common Regular -IR Verbs & Examples**

| Infinitive | Stem | je form | English | Example Sentence |
|------------|------|---------|---------|------------------|
| finir | fin- | je finis | I finish | Je finis mon travail. (I finish my work.) |
| choisir | chois- | je choisis | I choose | Je choisis le café. (I choose the coffee.) |
| réussir | réuss- | je réussis | I succeed | Je réussis l'examen. (I pass the exam.) |
| grandir | grand- | je grandis | I grow | L'enfant grandit vite. (The child grows fast.) |
| remplir | rempl- | je remplis | I fill | Je remplis le formulaire. (I fill out the form.) |

**Examples in realistic scenarios:**
- **Restaurant:** "Vous choisissez le menu?" (Are you choosing the menu? - waiter asking)
- **Work:** "Je finis à 17h." (I finish at 5 PM.)
- **School:** "Tu réussis tes examens?" (Do you pass your exams?)
- **Daily life:** "Nous finissons le dîner." (We finish dinner.)

**4. Real-World Dialogue Examples**

**Scene 1: At a Restaurant**
- Waiter: "Vous choisissez le plat du jour?" (Are you choosing the daily special?)
- Customer: "Oui, je choisis le poulet, s'il vous plaît." (Yes, I choose the chicken, please.)
- Waiter: "Très bien!"

**Scene 2: At Work**
- A: "À quelle heure tu finis le travail?" (What time do you finish work?)
- B: "Je finis à 18h. Et toi?" (I finish at 6 PM. And you?)
- A: "Je finis à 17h." (I finish at 5 PM.)

**Scene 3: School Success**
- A: "Tu réussis tes examens?" (Do you pass your exams?)
- B: "Oui, je réussis tous mes examens!" (Yes, I pass all my exams!)

**5. Common Errors & Next Steps**

- ❌ "Je finir le travail" → ✅ "Je finis le travail" (must conjugate, not use infinitive)
- ❌ "Tu finit" → ✅ "Tu finis" (tu = -is, not -it)
- ❌ "Nous finons" → ✅ "Nous finissons" (must add -iss- before ending)
- ❌ "Ils finisent" → ✅ "Ils finissent" (-issent, not -isent)
- **Next step:** Tomorrow we learn -RE verbs (the third verb family!)

### Metadata
- **CEFR Level:** A1.1

- **Vocabulary Domain:** Actions, waiting, selling, responding
- **Content Identifiers:** conjugation, fill_blank, listening, dialogue
- **Speaking Tier:** 1 (Script-based with variation)
- **Duration:** 30 minutes
- **Day Number:** 7

---

### GRAMMAR SECTION (10 min)

#### Explanation (5-paragraph teacher format)

**1. What are -RE Verbs?**

The third and final family of regular French verbs ends in **-RE** in their infinitive form. These verbs are less common than -ER and -IR, but still essential. Common -RE verbs include: **vendre** (to sell), **attendre** (to wait), **répondre** (to answer/reply), **entendre** (to hear), **descendre** (to go down). Once you master this pattern, you've learned all three regular French verb families!

**2. How to Conjugate Regular -RE Verbs**

**Steps:**
1. Take the infinitive: **vend**re
2. Remove -re: **vend** (this is the stem)
3. Add the endings: -s, -s, -, -ons, -ez, -ent

**Important:** Notice that il/elle/on has **NO ENDING** (just the stem)!

**Example: Vendre (to sell)**

| Pronoun | Stem + Ending | Pronunciation | English |
|---------|---------------|---------------|---------|
| je | vends | vahn | I sell |
| tu | vends | vahn | you sell |
| il/elle | vend | vahn | he/she sells |
| nous | vendons | vahn-don | we sell |
| vous | vendez | vahn-day | you sell |
| ils/elles | vendent | vahnd | they sell |

**Pattern for ALL -RE verbs:** -s, -s, ∅ (nothing), -ons, -ez, -ent

**3. Common Regular -RE Verbs & Examples**

| Infinitive | Stem | je form | il/elle form | English |
|------------|------|---------|--------------|---------|
| vendre | vend- | je vends | il vend | I sell / he sells |
| attendre | attend- | j'attends | il attend | I wait / he waits |
| répondre | répond- | je réponds | il répond | I answer / he answers |
| entendre | entend- | j'entends | il entend | I hear / he hears |
| descendre | descend- | je descends | il descend | I go down / he goes down |

**Examples in realistic scenarios:**
- **Shopping:** "Il vend des livres." (He sells books.)
- **Transportation:** "J'attends le bus." (I wait for the bus.)
- **Communication:** "Tu réponds au message?" (Do you reply to the message?)
- **Everyday:** "Vous entendez la musique?" (Do you hear the music?)

**4. Real-World Dialogue Examples**

**Scene 1: At a Market**
- Customer: "Vous vendez des fruits?" (Do you sell fruits?)
- Vendor: "Oui, je vends des pommes et des oranges." (Yes, I sell apples and oranges.)

**Scene 2: Waiting for the Bus**
- A: "Tu attends le bus?" (Are you waiting for the bus?)
- B: "Oui, j'attends depuis 10 minutes." (Yes, I've been waiting for 10 minutes.)
- A: "Moi aussi!"

**Scene 3: Phone Call**
- A: "Tu réponds au téléphone?" (Do you answer the phone?)
- B: "Oui, je réponds maintenant." (Yes, I answer now.)

**5. Common Errors & Next Steps**

- ❌ "Je vendre" → ✅ "Je vends" (must conjugate)
- ❌ "Il vends" → ✅ "Il vend" (il/elle = NO ENDING, just stem)
- ❌ "Nous vendez" → ✅ "Nous vendons" (nous = -ons)
- ❌ "Tu vend" → ✅ "Tu vends" (tu needs -s)
- **Key:** Remember il/elle has NO ENDING in -RE verbs!
- **Next step:** Tomorrow we learn negation (ne...pas) to say what you DON'T do!

## DAY 8 - A1.1.8: Negation with Ne...Pas

### Metadata
- **CEFR Level:** A1.1
- **Grammar Topic:** Negative sentences (ne...pas structure)
- **Vocabulary Domain:** Negative expressions, refusals
- **Content Identifiers:** grammar, fill_blank, listening, dialogue, negation
- **Speaking Tier:** 1 (Script-based practicing refusals)
- **Duration:** 30 minutes
- **Day Number:** 8

---

### GRAMMAR SECTION (10 min)

#### Explanation (5-paragraph teacher format)

**1. What is Negation in French?**

To say you DON'T do something in French, you use **ne...pas** around the verb. Unlike English where you add "don't" or "doesn't" before the verb, French wraps the verb with two parts: **ne** (before the verb) and **pas** (after the verb). Example: "Je parle" (I speak) → "Je ne parle pas" (I don't speak). This is the most common way to make negative sentences in French.

**2. How to Form Negative Sentences - Ne...Pas**

**Formula:** Subject + ne + verb + pas

| Affirmative | Negative | English |
|-------------|----------|---------|
| Je parle français. | Je ne parle pas français. | I don't speak French. |
| Tu as un chat. | Tu n'as pas de chat. | You don't have a cat. |
| Il habite à Paris. | Il n'habite pas à Paris. | He doesn't live in Paris. |
| Nous aimons le café. | Nous n'aimons pas le café. | We don't like coffee. |
| Vous finissez. | Vous ne finissez pas. | You don't finish. |
| Ils vendent. | Ils ne vendent pas. | They don't sell. |

**Important:** Before a vowel, "ne" becomes **n'** → "Je n'ai pas" (not "Je ne ai pas")

**3. Special Rule: Negation with Avoir (de, not un/une/des)**

When avoir is negative, **un/une/des** changes to **de** (or **d'** before a vowel):

| Affirmative | Negative |
|-------------|----------|
| J'ai un chat. | Je n'ai pas **de** chat. (not "un chat") |
| Tu as une voiture. | Tu n'as pas **de** voiture. |
| Nous avons des livres. | Nous n'avons pas **de** livres. |

**Why?** In negative sentences with avoir, French uses "de" to mean "any" - "I don't have any cat."

**4. Real-World Dialogue Examples**

**Scene 1: At a Café - Preferences**
- A: "Tu aimes le café?" (Do you like coffee?)
- B: "Non, je n'aime pas le café. Je préfère le thé." (No, I don't like coffee. I prefer tea.)

**Scene 2: Language Skills**
- A: "Tu parles espagnol?" (Do you speak Spanish?)
- B: "Non, je ne parle pas espagnol. Je parle français et anglais." (No, I don't speak Spanish. I speak French and English.)

**Scene 3: Housing**
- A: "Vous habitez à Lyon?" (Do you live in Lyon?)
- B: "Non, je n'habite pas à Lyon. J'habite à Paris." (No, I don't live in Lyon. I live in Paris.)

**Scene 4: Possessions**
- A: "Tu as un chien?" (Do you have a dog?)
- B: "Non, je n'ai pas de chien. J'ai un chat." (No, I don't have a dog. I have a cat.)

**5. Common Errors & Next Steps**

- ❌ "Je pas parle français" → ✅ "Je ne parle pas français" (need both ne and pas)
- ❌ "Je ne parle français pas" → ✅ "Je ne parle pas français" (pas goes after verb)
- ❌ "Je n'ai pas un chat" → ✅ "Je n'ai pas de chat" (use de, not un/une/des)
- ❌ "Je ne ai pas" → ✅ "Je n'ai pas" (ne → n' before vowel)
- **Spoken French note:** In casual conversation, French people often drop the "ne" and just say "Je parle pas," but in written and formal French, ALWAYS use both!

## DAY 9 - A1.1.9: Asking Questions (Est-ce que, Inversion, Intonation)

### Metadata
- **CEFR Level:** A1.1

- **Vocabulary Domain:** Question words, curiosity, information gathering
- **Content Identifiers:** grammar, questions, listening, dialogue, intonation
- **Speaking Tier:** 1 (Script-based with question practice)
- **Duration:** 30 minutes
- **Day Number:** 9

---

### GRAMMAR SECTION (10 min)

#### Explanation (5-paragraph teacher format)

**1. Three Ways to Ask Questions in French**

In French, there are **three different ways** to ask the same question. Each has a different level of formality:
1. **Intonation** (casual - just raise your voice at the end)
2. **Est-ce que** (neutral - most common)
3. **Inversion** (formal - flip subject and verb)

All three mean the exact same thing! Example: "Do you speak French?"
- Intonation: "Tu parles français?" ↗ (voice goes up)
- Est-ce que: "Est-ce que tu parles français?"
- Inversion: "Parles-tu français?"

**2. Method 1: Intonation (Simplest - Just Add ↗)**

**How it works:** Say the statement normally, but raise your voice at the end (like in English).

| Statement | Question (Intonation) | English |
|-----------|----------------------|---------|
| Tu parles français. | Tu parles français? ↗ | Do you speak French? |
| Vous habitez à Paris. | Vous habitez à Paris? ↗ | Do you live in Paris? |
| Il aime le café. | Il aime le café? ↗ | Does he like coffee? |
| C'est bon. | C'est bon? ↗ | Is it good? |

**When to use:** Casual conversation with friends, family. Most common in spoken French!

**3. Method 2: Est-ce que (Most Common in All Situations)**

**How it works:** Add "Est-ce que" at the beginning of the statement.

**Formula:** Est-ce que + subject + verb?

| Statement | Question (Est-ce que) | English |
|-----------|----------------------|---------|
| Tu parles français. | Est-ce que tu parles français? | Do you speak French? |
| Vous habitez à Paris. | Est-ce qu'vous habitez à Paris? | Do you live in Paris? |
| Il aime le café. | Est-ce qu'il aime le café? | Does he like coffee? |

**Note:** Before a vowel, "que" becomes "qu'" → "Est-ce qu'il..."

**When to use:** Any situation! This is the safest, most neutral way to ask questions. Works in both formal and informal contexts.

**4. Method 3: Inversion (Formal - Written/Professional)**

**How it works:** Flip the subject pronoun and verb, connect with a hyphen.

**Formula:** Verb + hyphen + subject pronoun?

| Statement | Question (Inversion) | English |
|-----------|---------------------|---------|
| Tu parles français. | Parles-tu français? | Do you speak French? |
| Vous habitez à Paris. | Habitez-vous à Paris? | Do you live in Paris? |
| Ils aiment le café. | Aiment-ils le café? | Do they like coffee? |

**Special case:** If the verb ends in a vowel and the subject is il/elle/on, add **-t-** between them:
- "Il habite" → "Habite-**t**-il?" (Does he live?)
- "Elle aime" → "Aime-**t**-elle?" (Does she like?)

**When to use:** Formal writing, literature, professional settings, or when addressing strangers very politely.

**5. Real-World Dialogue Examples**

**Scene 1: Casual Friends (Intonation)**
- A: "Tu viens ce soir?" ↗ (Are you coming tonight?)
- B: "Oui, je viens!"

**Scene 2: New Acquaintance (Est-ce que)**
- A: "Est-ce que vous parlez anglais?" (Do you speak English?)
- B: "Oui, je parle un peu."

**Scene 3: Formal Setting (Inversion)**
- A: "Habitez-vous à Paris, Monsieur?" (Do you live in Paris, sir?)
- B: "Oui, j'habite à Paris."

**Common Errors & Next Steps:**
- ❌ "Tu parles français?" (flat tone - no rising intonation = statement, not question)
- ✅ "Tu parles français?" ↗ (rising tone = question)
- ❌ "Est-que tu parles?" → ✅ "Est-ce que tu parles?"
- ❌ "Parle-tu?" (missing -s) → ✅ "Parles-tu?"
- **Next step:** Tomorrow we learn numbers 1-20 to talk about age, time, and quantities!

### GRAMMAR SECTION (10 min)

#### Explanation

**1. Why Possessive Adjectives Matter**

In French, you can't simply say "my book" or "his car" - you must make the possessive adjective **agree with the noun it modifies**, not the owner! This is the key difference from English. If the object you own is feminine, you use the feminine possessive form. If it's plural, you use the plural form. This lesson will teach you how to naturally express ownership, relationships, and belonging in French - essential for describing your life, family, and possessions!

**2. The Complete Possessive System**

French possessive adjectives change based on **THREE factors:**
1. **Who owns it** (I, you, he/she, we, you all, they)
2. **Gender of the thing owned** (masculine/feminine)
3. **Number of things owned** (singular/plural)

**Full table:**

| Owner | Masculine Singular | Feminine Singular | Plural (all genders) |
|-------|-------------------|-------------------|----------------------|
| **Je** (I) | mon (my) | ma (my) | mes (my) |
| **Tu** (you) | ton (your) | ta (your) | tes (your) |
| **Il/Elle** (he/she) | son (his/her) | sa (his/her) | ses (his/her) |
| **Nous** (we) | notre (our) | notre (our) | nos (our) |
| **Vous** (you formal/plural) | votre (your) | votre (your) | vos (your) |
| **Ils/Elles** (they) | leur (their) | leur (their) | leurs (their) |

**Key patterns:**
- **Mon/ton/son group:** All change for gender AND number (mon/ma/mes, ton/ta/tes, son/sa/ses)
- **Notre/votre group:** Only change for number (notre/nos, votre/vos)
- **Leur group:** Only changes for number (leur/leurs)

#### Explanation

**1. What Are Demonstrative Adjectives?**

Demonstrative adjectives are words like "this," "that," "these," and "those" in English. They help you **point out** or **identify** specific things. In French, you use **ce, cet, cette, ces** depending on the gender and number of the noun. **2. The Four Forms of Demonstrative Adjectives**

French demonstrative adjectives change based on:
1. **Gender** of the noun (masculine/feminine)
2. **Number** of the noun (singular/plural)
3. **Starting letter** of the noun (vowel or consonant)

**Complete table:**

| Gender & Number | Form | When to Use | Examples |
|----------------|------|-------------|----------|
| **Masculine Singular (consonant)** | ce | Before masculine nouns starting with consonant | ce livre, ce chat, ce restaurant |

- **Ce** vs **cet**: Both mean "this/that" (masculine), but **cet** prevents awkward pronunciation before vowels

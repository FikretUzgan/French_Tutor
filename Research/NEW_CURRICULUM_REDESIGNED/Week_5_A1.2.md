### GRAMMAR SECTION (10 min)

#### Explanation (5-paragraph teacher format)

**1. Why the Passé Composé Matters**

The **passé composé** is THE most important past tense in spoken French. It's used to talk about completed actions in the past—things you did yesterday, last week, or at any specific moment. While French has several past tenses (imparfait, plus-que-parfait), the passé composé is your essential tool for 90% of everyday past-tense conversations. Think of it as the French equivalent of English "I ate," "I have eaten," or "I did eat" - all translate to the same form in French!

**2. The Two-Part Formula: Avoir + Past Participle**

The passé composé is a **compound tense** (two parts):

**Formula:** **Avoir (present tense) + Past Participle**

**Example with "manger" (to eat):**
- J'ai mangé (I ate / I have eaten)
- Auxiliary: "ai" (present tense of avoir)
- Past participle: "mangé" (from manger)

**Full conjugation:**
- Je **ai** mangé → J'**ai** mangé (I ate)
- Tu **as** mangé (You ate - singular/informal)
- Il/Elle/On **a** mangé (He/She/One ate)
- Nous **avons** mangé (We ate)
- Vous **avez** mangé (You ate - plural/formal)
- Ils/Elles **ont** mangé (They ate)

**Structure breakdown:**
1. **Subject** (Je, Tu, Il...)
2. **Avoir** (conjugated in present: ai, as, a, avons, avez, ont)
3. **Past Participle** (mangé, fini, parlé, choisi...)

**3. Forming Regular Past Participles**

**For regular verbs, the past participle follows predictable patterns:**

| Verb Type | Infinitive Ending | Past Participle Rule | Example |
|-----------|-------------------|---------------------|---------|
| **-ER verbs** | -er | Drop -er, add **-é** | parler → parlé |
| | | | manger → mangé |
| | | | regarder → regardé |
| **-IR verbs** | -ir | Drop -ir, add **-i** | finir → fini |
| | | | choisir → choisi |
| | | | réussir → réussi |
| **-RE verbs** | -re | Drop -re, add **-u** | vendre → vendu |
| | | | attendre → attendu |
| | | | répondre → répondu |

**Examples in sentences:**
- Parler (to speak) → J'ai **parlé** français. (I spoke French.)
- Finir (to finish) → Tu as **fini** tes devoirs. (You finished your homework.)
- Vendre (to sell) → Il a **vendu** sa voiture. (He sold his car.)
- Manger (to eat) → Nous avons **mangé** au restaurant. (We ate at the restaurant.)
- Choisir (to choose) → Vous avez **choisi** le menu. (You chose the menu.)
- Attendre (to wait) → Ils ont **attendu** le bus. (They waited for the bus.)

**4. Real-World Usage & Examples**

**Scene 1: Talking About Yesterday**
- A: Qu'est-ce que tu as fait hier? (What did you do yesterday?)
- B: J'ai regardé un film et j'ai mangé une pizza. (I watched a movie and I ate a pizza.)
- A: Tu as aimé le film? (Did you like the movie?)
- B: Oui, j'ai adoré! (Yes, I loved it!)

**Scene 2: At Work/School**
- A: Tu as fini le projet? (Did you finish the project?)
- B: Oui, j'ai fini hier soir. (Yes, I finished yesterday evening.)
- A: Tu as parlé au professeur? (Did you speak to the teacher?)
- B: Non, je n'ai pas parlé. (No, I didn't speak.)

**Scene 3: Shopping Experience**
- A: Vous avez vendu beaucoup de choses? (Did you sell many things?)
- B: Oui, nous avons vendu tous les livres. (Yes, we sold all the books.)
- A: Les clients ont attendu longtemps? (Did the customers wait long?)
- B: Non, ils ont attendu cinq minutes seulement. (No, they only waited five minutes.)

**Negation in Passé Composé:**
- **Pattern:** ne + avoir + pas + past participle
- Je **n'ai pas** mangé. (I didn't eat / I haven't eaten.)
- Tu **n'as pas** fini. (You didn't finish.)
- Il **n'a pas** parlé. (He didn't speak.)
- Nous **n'avons pas** vendu. (We didn't sell.)

**5. Common Errors & Practice Tips**

**Common mistakes to avoid:**
- ❌ Je mangé (missing auxiliary avoir) → ✅ J'ai mangé
- ❌ J'ai manger (infinitive instead of past participle) → ✅ J'ai mangé
- ❌ Je suis mangé (wrong auxiliary - être vs avoir) → ✅ J'ai mangé (most verbs use avoir!)
- ❌ Tu a parlé (wrong avoir form) → ✅ Tu as parlé
- ❌ J'ai ne pas mangé (negation in wrong position) → ✅ Je n'ai pas mangé

**Memory tips:**
- **-ER verbs = -é sound (like "ay"):** parlé, mangé, regardé
- **-IR verbs = -i sound (like "ee"):** fini, choisi
- **-RE verbs = -u sound (like "oo"):** vendu, attendu
- **Negation sandwich:** ne + avoir + pas wraps around the auxiliary

**Practice strategy:**
1. Master present tense of avoir first (ai, as, a, avons, avez, ont)
2. Learn past participle patterns (-é, -i, -u)
3. Combine: J'ai + mangé = J'ai mangé
4. Add negation: Je + n'ai pas + mangé = Je n'ai pas mangé

**5. Practice Patterns & Common Contexts**

**Restaurant & Food Context:**
- Commander (to order) → J'ai **commandé** un steak. (I ordered a steak.)
- Manger (to eat) → Nous avons **mangé** au restaurant. (We ate at the restaurant.)

- Travailler (to work) → J'ai **travaillé** toute la journée. (I worked all day.)

- Finir (to finish) → Elle a **fini** son projet. (She finished her project.)
- Regarder (to watch) → Nous avons **regardé** un film. (We watched a movie.)

**Scenario: At the Restaurant - Describe Your Meal**

**Guided Conversation Framework:**
```

```

**Vocabulary Support (Restaurant Theme):**

- **Desserts:** une glace (ice cream), un gâteau (cake), une tarte (pie)
- **Actions:** commandé (ordered), mangé (ate), choisi (chose), aimé (liked), attendu (waited), demandé (asked for)
- **Adjectives:** délicieux/délicieuse (delicious), bon/bonne (good), excellent(e) (excellent)

**Sample Student Response:**
```

   (Yesterday, I ate at the restaurant "Le Petit Bistro".)

3. Oui, j'ai choisi une glace au chocolat comme dessert.
   (Yes, I chose chocolate ice cream as dessert.)

   (Yes, I loved the meal! It was delicious.)

   (No, we didn't wait long - only 10 minutes.)
```

### Metadata

- **Vocabulary Domain:** Daily activities, beverages, actions

- **Speaking Tier:** 2 (Guided conversation)

---

### GRAMMAR SECTION (10 min)

#### Explanation (5-paragraph teacher format)

**1. The Reality of Irregular Verbs**

While regular verbs follow predictable patterns (-é, -i, -u), some of the MOST COMMON French verbs have irregular past participles that don't follow any rule. The good news? There are only about 20-30 truly essential irregular past participles to memorize, and they're the verbs you'll use most often. Today we'll master the "Big 6" that appear in almost every conversation about the past!

**2. The Essential Irregular Past Participles**

**These MUST be memorized - they don't follow patterns:**

| Infinitive | Past Participle | Meaning | Example |
|------------|-----------------|---------|---------|
| **avoir** | **eu** (NOT "avé") | to have | J'ai **eu** un problème. (I had a problem.) |
| **être** | **été** (NOT "êté") | to be | Il a **été** malade. (He was sick.) |
| **faire** | **fait** (NOT "faisé") | to do/make | Tu as **fait** tes devoirs? (Did you do your homework?) |
| **prendre** | **pris** (NOT "prendu") | to take | Elle a **pris** le bus. (She took the bus.) |
| **voir** | **vu** (NOT "voiru") | to see | Nous avons **vu** un film. (We saw a movie.) |
| **boire** | **bu** (NOT "boiru") | to drink | Vous avez **bu** du café? (Did you drink coffee?) |

**Why irregular?** These verbs evolved from Latin with sound changes over centuries. Their past participles come from older forms that don't match modern patterns.

**3. Detailed Breakdown with Full Conjugations**

**Avoir (to have) → eu**

Full conjugation in passé composé:
- J'ai **eu** (I had)
- Tu as **eu** (You had)
- Il/Elle/On a **eu** (He/She/One had)
- Nous avons **eu** (We had)
- Vous avez **eu** (You had - formal/plural)
- Ils/Elles ont **eu** (They had)

**Common uses:**
- J'ai eu un problème. (I had a problem.)
- Tu as eu de la chance! (You were lucky! - literally "had luck")
- Il a eu 20 ans hier. (He turned 20 yesterday.)
- Nous avons eu un examen. (We had an exam.)

**Être (to be) → été**

Full conjugation:
- J'ai **été** (I was / I have been)
- Tu as **été** (You were)
- Il/Elle a **été** (He/She was)
- Nous avons **été** (We were)
- Vous avez **été** (You were)
- Ils/Elles ont **été** (They were)

**Note:** Uses AVOIR as auxiliary, even though it means "to be"!

**Common uses:**
- J'ai été malade hier. (I was sick yesterday.)
- Tu as été en France? (Have you been to France?)
- Il a été très gentil. (He was very kind.)
- Nous avons été surpris. (We were surprised.)

**Faire (to do/make) → fait**

Full conjugation:
- J'ai **fait** (I did / I made)
- Tu as **fait** (You did)
- Il/Elle a **fait** (He/She did)
- Nous avons **fait** (We did)
- Vous avez **fait** (You did)
- Ils/Elles ont **fait** (They did)

**Common uses:**
- J'ai fait mes devoirs. (I did my homework.)
- Tu as fait du sport? (Did you do sports?)
- Il a fait un gâteau. (He made a cake.)
- Qu'est-ce que tu as fait hier? (What did you do yesterday?)
- Il a fait beau. (The weather was nice. - idiomatic)

**Prendre (to take) → pris**

Full conjugation:
- J'ai **pris** (I took)
- Tu as **pris** (You took)
- Il/Elle a **pris** (He/She took)
- Nous avons **pris** (We took)
- Vous avez **pris** (You took)
- Ils/Elles ont **pris** (They took)

**Common uses:**
- J'ai pris le bus. (I took the bus.)
- Tu as pris un café? (Did you have a coffee?)
- Elle a pris une photo. (She took a photo.)
- Nous avons pris le petit-déjeuner. (We had breakfast.)

**Related verbs (same pattern):**
- Comprendre → compris (understood)
- Apprendre → appris (learned)

**Voir (to see) → vu**

Full conjugation:
- J'ai **vu** (I saw)
- Tu as **vu** (You saw)
- Il/Elle a **vu** (He/She saw)
- Nous avons **vu** (We saw)
- Vous avez **vu** (You saw)
- Ils/Elles ont **vu** (They saw)

**Common uses:**
- J'ai vu un film. (I saw a movie.)
- Tu as vu Marie? (Did you see Marie?)
- Il a vu un accident. (He saw an accident.)
- Nous avons vu la Tour Eiffel. (We saw the Eiffel Tower.)

**Boire (to drink) → bu**

Full conjugation:
- J'ai **bu** (I drank)
- Tu as **bu** (You drank)
- Il/Elle a **bu** (He/She drank)
- Nous avons **bu** (We drank)
- Vous avez **bu** (You drank)
- Ils/Elles ont **bu** (They drank)

**Common uses:**
- J'ai bu un café. (I drank a coffee.)
- Tu as bu de l'eau? (Did you drink water?)
- Elle a bu trop de vin. (She drank too much wine.)
- Nous avons bu du jus d'orange. (We drank orange juice.)

**4. Real-World Dialogue Examples**

**Scene 1: Discussing the Day**
- A: Qu'est-ce que tu as fait hier? (What did you do yesterday?)
- B: J'ai vu un film et j'ai bu un café avec Marie. (I saw a movie and I drank coffee with Marie.)
- A: Tu as eu le temps de faire tes devoirs? (Did you have time to do your homework?)
- B: Oui, j'ai fait mes devoirs le soir. (Yes, I did my homework in the evening.)

**Scene 2: Weekend Stories**
- A: Comment a été ton week-end? (How was your weekend?)
- B: Il a été super! J'ai pris le train pour Paris. (It was great! I took the train to Paris.)
- A: Tu as vu des musées? (Did you see museums?)
- B: Oui, j'ai vu le Louvre et j'ai pris beaucoup de photos. (Yes, I saw the Louvre and I took lots of photos.)

**Scene 3: Problem Discussion**
- A: Tu as eu un problème avec le projet? (Did you have a problem with the project?)
- B: Oui, j'ai eu un bug dans le code. (Yes, I had a bug in the code.)
- A: Tu as fait quoi? (What did you do?)
- B: J'ai pris le temps de réviser tout le code. (I took the time to review all the code.)

**5. Common Errors & Memory Strategies**

**Common mistakes:**
- ❌ J'ai voir un film → ✅ J'ai vu un film
- ❌ Tu as faité tes devoirs → ✅ Tu as fait tes devoirs
- ❌ Il a prendu le bus → ✅ Il a pris le bus
- ❌ Nous avons boiru du café → ✅ Nous avons bu du café
- ❌ J'ai avé un problème → ✅ J'ai eu un problème
- ❌ Elle est été malade (wrong auxiliary) → ✅ Elle a été malade

**Memory tricks:**
- **AVOIR → EU:** Think "uh-oh, I had a problem" (eu sounds like "uh")
- **ÊTRE → ÉTÉ:** Think "été" (summer) - I was there in summer
- **FAIRE → FAIT:** "Facts" - I made/did facts
- **PRENDRE → PRIS:** "Prize" - I took a prize
- **VOIR → VU:** "View" - I saw the view
- **BOIRE → BU:** "Boo!" - I drank the boo(ze)

**Practice pattern:**
1. Say infinitive: "Avoir"
2. Say past participle: "eu"
3. Make sentence: "J'ai eu un problème"
4. Repeat for all 6 verbs

**Flashcard drill:**
- Front: "Faire"
- Back: "Fait - J'ai fait mes devoirs"

### GRAMMAR SECTION (10 min)

#### Explanation (5-paragraph teacher format)

**1. Expanding Your Irregular Verb Arsenal**

Yesterday you mastered the "Big 6" irregular past participles. Today we'll add 8 MORE essential irregular verbs that appear constantly in everyday French. These include communication verbs (dire, écrire, lire), placement verbs (mettre), and modal verbs (pouvoir, vouloir, savoir, devoir). Combined with yesterday's verbs, you'll have the foundation to discuss almost any past action!

**2. Communication & Writing Verbs**

**Dire (to say/tell) → dit**

- J'ai **dit** la vérité. (I told the truth.)
- Tu as **dit** bonjour? (Did you say hello?)
- Il a **dit** qu'il est malade. (He said he's sick.)
- Qu'est-ce que vous avez **dit**? (What did you say?)

**Écrire (to write) → écrit**

- J'ai **écrit** une lettre. (I wrote a letter.)
- Tu as **écrit** un email? (Did you write an email?)
- Elle a **écrit** un message. (She wrote a message.)
- Nous avons **écrit** nos noms. (We wrote our names.)

**Lire (to read) → lu**

- J'ai **lu** un livre intéressant. (I read an interesting book.)
- Tu as **lu** le journal? (Did you read the newspaper?)
- Il a **lu** l'article. (He read the article.)
- Vous avez **lu** les instructions? (Did you read the instructions?)

**3. Placement & Action Verb**

**Mettre (to put/place) → mis**

- J'ai **mis** le livre sur la table. (I put the book on the table.)
- Tu as **mis** ton manteau? (Did you put on your coat?)
- Elle a **mis** la télé. (She turned on the TV.)
- Nous avons **mis** une heure pour arriver. (We took an hour to arrive. - idiomatic)

**Related verb:**
- Promettre (to promise) → promis: J'ai promis de venir. (I promised to come.)

**4. Modal Verbs (Can, Want, Know, Must)**

**Pouvoir (to be able to/can) → pu**

- J'ai **pu** finir le projet. (I was able to finish the project.)
- Tu as **pu** venir? (Were you able to come?)
- Il n'a pas **pu** répondre. (He couldn't answer.)
- Nous avons **pu** voir le film. (We were able to see the movie.)

**Vouloir (to want) → voulu**

- J'ai **voulu** partir tôt. (I wanted to leave early.)
- Tu as **voulu** manger au restaurant? (Did you want to eat at the restaurant?)
- Elle a **voulu** acheter une voiture. (She wanted to buy a car.)
- Ils n'ont pas **voulu** attendre. (They didn't want to wait.)

**Savoir (to know) → su**

- J'ai **su** la réponse. (I knew the answer.)
- Tu as **su** qu'il est parti? (Did you know he left?)
- Il a **su** résoudre le problème. (He knew how to solve the problem.)
- Nous avons **su** la vérité. (We learned/found out the truth.)

**Note:** "Savoir" in passé composé often means "found out" or "learned"

**Devoir (to have to/must) → dû** *(with circumflex accent)*

- J'ai **dû** partir tôt. (I had to leave early.)
- Tu as **dû** travailler hier? (Did you have to work yesterday?)
- Elle a **dû** attendre longtemps. (She had to wait a long time.)
- Nous avons **dû** annuler. (We had to cancel.)

**Note:** Devoir also means "to owe" - J'ai dû 20 euros. (I owed 20 euros.)

**5. Complete List & Memory Strategies**

**Full Summary Table:**

| Infinitive | Past Participle | Type | Mnemonic |
|------------|-----------------|------|----------|
| **dire** | dit | Communication | "Deet" - I said it |
| **écrire** | écrit | Communication | Same as "dit" pattern |
| **lire** | lu | Communication | "Loo" - I read through |
| **mettre** | mis | Action | "Meese" - I placed |
| **pouvoir** | pu | Modal | "Poo" - I could |
| **vouloir** | voulu | Modal | "Voo-loo" - I wanted to |
| **savoir** | su | Modal | "Soo" - I knew |
| **devoir** | dû | Modal | "Doo" - I had to do |

**Pattern Recognition:**
- **-IRE verbs often → -IT or -U:**
  - Dire → dit
  - Écrire → écrit
  - Lire → lu
- **Modal verbs → Short forms:**
  - Pouvoir → pu
  - Savoir → su
  - Devoir → dû
  - Vouloir → voulu (exception - longer)

**Dialogue Example Using All New Verbs:**
```
A: Qu'est-ce que tu as fait hier soir?
   (What did you do yesterday evening?)
   
B: J'ai lu un livre et j'ai écrit un email à ma mère.

A: Tu as pu finir tes devoirs?

B: Non, je n'ai pas pu. J'ai dû travailler tard.

A: Qu'est-ce que ton patron a dit?
   (What did your boss say?)
   
B: Il a dit que j'ai bien travaillé. J'ai mis beaucoup d'efforts.
   (He said I worked well. I put in a lot of effort.)
```

- ❌ J'ai dire → ✅ J'ai dit
- ❌ Tu as écris → ✅ Tu as écrit (no "s")
- ❌ Il a lisé → ✅ Il a lu
- ❌ Nous avons mettis → ✅ Nous avons mis
- ❌ J'ai devé → ✅ J'ai dû (circumflex!)

**1. Passé Composé Structure**
- **Formula:** Avoir (present) + Past Participle
- **Usage:** Completed actions in the past
- **Negation:** ne + avoir + pas + past participle

**2. Regular Past Participles**

|-----------|---------|----------|

| **-RE** | -re → **-u** | vendre → vendu, attendre → attendu, répondre → répondu |

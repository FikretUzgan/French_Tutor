### GRAMMAR SECTION (10 min)

#### Explanation

**1. Why Questions Matter in French**

Asking questions is essential for everyday communication. In French, there are **three main ways** to ask the same question, ranging from informal (spoken) to formal (written). Understanding all three methods gives you flexibility—you'll sound natural in casual conversations and professional in formal contexts. Today we'll master: **intonation questions, est-ce que questions, and inversion questions**.

**2. Method 1: Intonation (Informal - Most Common in Speech)**

The easiest way to ask a question is simply to **raise your voice** at the end of a statement. This is the most common method in everyday spoken French.

**Pattern:** Statement + ↗ (rising intonation)

**Examples:**
- Tu parles français? ↗ (You speak French?)
- Il est étudiant? ↗ (He is a student?)
- Vous habitez à Paris? ↗ (You live in Paris?)
- C'est ton livre? ↗ (Is it/This is your book?)

**When to use:** Casual conversations, among friends, informal settings

**3. Method 2: Est-ce que (Neutral - Works Everywhere)**

Add **"Est-ce que"** (literally "Is it that") at the beginning of any statement to turn it into a question. This works in both formal and informal contexts.

**Pattern:** Est-ce que + statement?

**Examples:**
- Est-ce que tu parles français? (Do you speak French?)
- Est-ce qu'il est étudiant? (Is he a student?)
- Est-ce que vous habitez à Paris? (Do you live in Paris?)
- Est-ce que c'est ton livre? (Is this your book?)

**Note:** "Est-ce que" becomes "Est-ce qu'" before a vowel (elision)

**When to use:** Universal method - works in all situations, written and spoken

**4. Method 3: Inversion (Formal - Written/Professional)**

**Invert** (swap) the subject pronoun and verb, connecting them with a hyphen. This is the most formal method and common in written French.

**Pattern:** Verb + hyphen + subject pronoun?

**Examples:**
- Parles-tu français? (Do you speak French?)
- Est-il étudiant? (Is he a student?)
- Habitez-vous à Paris? (Do you live in Paris?)
- Est-ce ton livre? (Is this your book?) *Note: "c'est" doesn't invert well

**Special rules:**
- Add **-t-** between two vowels for pronunciation: A-t-il un livre? (Does he have a book?)
- With je, inversion is rare except with common verbs: Suis-je...? Ai-je...? Puis-je...?

**When to use:** Formal writing, professional contexts, literature

**Comparison Chart:**

| Style | Example | Usage |
|-------|---------|-------|
| **Intonation** | Tu parles français? ↗ | Informal/Spoken |
| **Est-ce que** | Est-ce que tu parles français? | Universal |
| **Inversion** | Parles-tu français? | Formal/Written |

**5. Question Words (Interrogatives)**

**Common question words:**

| French | English | Example |
|--------|---------|---------|
| **Qui?** | Who? | Qui est-ce? (Who is it?) |
| **Quoi?** / **Que?** | What? | Qu'est-ce que c'est? (What is it?) |
| **Où?** | Where? | Où es-tu? (Where are you?) |
| **Quand?** | When? | Quand arrives-tu? (When do you arrive?) |
| **Comment?** | How? | Comment ça va? (How are you?) |
| **Pourquoi?** | Why? | Pourquoi tu étudies? (Why do you study?) |
| **Combien?** | How many/much? | Combien ça coûte? (How much does it cost?) |
| **Quel(le)?** | Which/What? | Quelle heure est-il? (What time is it?) |

**Using question words with est-ce que:**
- Où est-ce que tu habites? (Where do you live?)
- Quand est-ce que tu arrives? (When do you arrive?)
- Pourquoi est-ce que tu étudies le français? (Why do you study French?)

**Real-World Dialogue Examples**

**Scene 1: Meeting Someone New**
- A: Comment tu t'appelles? ↗ (What's your name? - intonation)
- B: Je m'appelle Marie. Et toi?
- A: Moi, c'est Paul. Où est-ce que tu habites? (Where do you live? - est-ce que)
- B: J'habite à Paris. Et toi?
- A: J'habite à Lyon.

**Scene 2: At a Café (More Formal)**
- Waiter: Que désirez-vous? (What would you like? - inversion, formal)
- Customer: Est-ce que vous avez du café? (Do you have coffee? - est-ce que)
- Waiter: Oui. Voulez-vous un croissant aussi? (Do you want a croissant too? - inversion)
- Customer: Non, merci. Combien ça coûte? (How much does it cost?)

**Scene 3: Planning with Friends**
- A: On va au cinéma? ↗ (Are we going to the cinema? - intonation)
- B: Quand? (When?)
- A: Ce soir. Tu viens? ↗ (Tonight. Are you coming?)
- B: Pourquoi pas! À quelle heure? (Why not! At what time?)
- A: À 20h.

**Common Errors & Tips:**

**Common errors:**
- ❌ "Tu parles français?" (written) → ✅ "Parles-tu français?" or "Est-ce que tu parles français?" (formal writing)
- ❌ "Est-ce que il est..." → ✅ "Est-ce qu'il est..." (elision before vowel)
- ❌ "A-il un livre?" → ✅ "A-t-il un livre?" (need -t- between vowels)
- ❌ "Qu'est-ce que est-ce?" → ✅ "Qu'est-ce que c'est?" (What is it?)

**Practice tip:** Start with intonation for speaking, master est-ce que for safety, learn inversion for reading comprehension!

- **Speaking Tier:** 1 (Script-based with negation practice)
- **Duration:** 30 minutes
- **Day Number:** 17

---

### GRAMMAR SECTION (10 min)

#### Explanation

**1. Beyond "Ne...Pas" - Why Complex Negation Matters**

You already know **ne...pas** (not), but French has several other negative expressions that add nuance: **never, nothing, no one, no more**. These work similarly to ne...pas (surrounding the verb) but change the meaning. Mastering these gives you precise expression: the difference between "I don't eat" and "I never eat" or "I eat nothing" is huge!

**2. The Four Main Complex Negations**

**Structure:** All follow the same pattern as ne...pas → **ne + verb + negative word**

| Negation | English | Example | Literal Translation |
|----------|---------|---------|---------------------|
| **ne...plus** | no more / no longer / not anymore | Je ne fume plus. | I don't smoke anymore. |
| **ne...jamais** | never | Je ne fume jamais. | I never smoke. |
| **ne...rien** | nothing / not anything | Je ne vois rien. | I see nothing / I don't see anything. |
| **ne...personne** | no one / nobody / not anyone | Je ne vois personne. | I see no one / I don't see anyone. |

**Position:** Same as ne...pas - "ne" before verb, second part after verb

**3. Detailed Breakdown & Examples**

**Ne...plus (no more / not anymore):**
- Used when something **was true before, but isn't now**
- J'habite à Paris → Je n'habite plus à Paris. (I no longer live in Paris.)
- Il travaille ici → Il ne travaille plus ici. (He doesn't work here anymore.)
- Nous avons du pain → Nous n'avons plus de pain. (We don't have bread anymore / We're out of bread.)

**Ne...jamais (never):**
- Used for **actions that NEVER happen**
- Opposite of "toujours" (always) or "souvent" (often)
- Je fume → Je ne fume jamais. (I never smoke.)
- Elle va au cinéma → Elle ne va jamais au cinéma. (She never goes to the cinema.)
- Tu manges de la viande → Tu ne manges jamais de viande. (You never eat meat.)

**Ne...rien (nothing):**
- Used when **nothing** is done/seen/heard
- Je vois quelque chose → Je ne vois rien. (I see nothing / I don't see anything.)
- Il fait quelque chose → Il ne fait rien. (He does nothing / He's not doing anything.)
- Nous mangeons → Nous ne mangeons rien. (We eat nothing / We're not eating anything.)

**Ne...personne (no one / nobody):**
- Used when **no person** is involved
- Je vois quelqu'un → Je ne vois personne. (I see no one / I don't see anyone.)
- Il parle à quelqu'un → Il ne parle à personne. (He speaks to no one / He's not talking to anyone.)
- **Special:** "Personne" can come after object: Je ne connais personne. (I know no one.)

**4. Real-World Dialogue Examples**

**Scene 1: At a Restaurant**
- Waiter: Vous désirez quelque chose? (Do you want something?)
- Customer: Non, merci. Je ne veux rien. (No, thanks. I don't want anything.)
- Waiter: Vous ne mangez jamais ici? (You never eat here?)
- Customer: Si, mais aujourd'hui, non. (Yes I do, but not today.)

**Scene 2: Talking About Habits**
- A: Tu fumes? (Do you smoke?)
- B: Non, je ne fume plus. J'ai arrêté. (No, I don't smoke anymore. I quit.)
- A: C'est bien! Moi, je ne fume jamais. (That's good! I never smoke.)

**Scene 3: Looking for Someone**
- A: Tu vois Marie? (Do you see Marie?)
- B: Non, je ne vois personne. (No, I don't see anyone.)
- A: Elle n'est jamais au bureau? (She's never at the office?)
- B: Si, mais pas aujourd'hui. (Yes she is, but not today.)

**Scene 4: Shopping**
- A: Qu'est-ce que tu achètes? (What are you buying?)
- B: Rien. Je ne veux rien. (Nothing. I don't want anything.)
- A: Tu n'achètes jamais de vêtements? (You never buy clothes?)
- B: Pas souvent. (Not often.)

**5. Important Grammar Notes**

**Omitting "Pas" - Never combine:**
- ❌ "Je ne fume jamais pas" → ✅ "Je ne fume jamais" (jamais replaces pas)
- ❌ "Je ne vois rien pas" → ✅ "Je ne vois rien" (rien replaces pas)
- **Rule:** Use ONLY ONE negative word (plus/jamais/rien/personne) - don't add "pas"

**"De" after negation (partitive article):**
- J'ai du pain → Je n'ai plus **de** pain. (not "du")
- Il mange de la viande → Il ne mange jamais **de** viande. (not "de la")
- **Exception:** rien/personne don't always trigger this

**Difference between ne...plus and ne...jamais:**
- **Ne...plus:** Used to do it, stopped → Je ne fume plus. (I quit smoking)
- **Ne...jamais:** Never did it, never will → Je ne fume jamais. (I've never smoked / I don't smoke - ever)

**Common errors:**
- ❌ "Je ne jamais fume" → ✅ "Je ne fume jamais" (jamais comes after verb)
- ❌ "Je ne vois pas personne" → ✅ "Je ne vois personne" (omit "pas")
- ❌ "Je n'ai plus du pain" → ✅ "Je n'ai plus de pain" (use "de" after negation)

## DAY 18 - A1.1.18: Imperatives & Commands

### Metadata
- **CEFR Level:** A1.1
- **Grammar Topic:** Imperative mood (commands, requests, instructions)
- **Vocabulary Domain:** Action verbs, polite requests, directions
- **Content Identifiers:** imperative, commands, conjugation, fill_blank
- **Speaking Tier:** 1→2 (Transition to guided conversation)
- **Duration:** 30 minutes
- **Day Number:** 18

---

### GRAMMAR SECTION (10 min)

#### Explanation

**1. What is the Imperative Mood?**

The **imperative** is used to give **commands, instructions, suggestions, or requests**. In French, it's simpler than you think—you use only **three forms** based on who you're talking to: **tu** (you - informal), **nous** (we/let's), and **vous** (you - formal/plural). There's no subject pronoun—jump straight to the action! Examples: "**Parle!**" (Speak!), "**Parlons!**" (Let's speak!), "**Parlez!**" (Speak! - formal)

**2. How to Form the Imperative**

**Simple rule:** Use the present tense verb form, but **drop the subject pronoun**.

**Regular -ER verbs (parler - to speak):**

| Pronoun | Present Tense | Imperative | English |
|---------|---------------|------------|---------|
| tu | tu parles | Parle! | Speak! (informal) |

### GRAMMAR SECTION (10 min)

#### Explanation

**1. Why Time Expressions Matter**

Talking about **when** you do things is essential for daily life: appointments, work schedules, meeting friends, daily routines. In French, you need to know how to **tell time, name days/months, and use frequency adverbs** (always, sometimes, never). Today we'll master: "Quelle heure est-il?" (What time is it?), days of the week, and how to describe your routine.

**2. Telling Time in French**

**Asking time:**
- Quelle heure est-il? (What time is it?)
- Il est quelle heure? (What time is it? - informal)
- Tu as l'heure? (Do you have the time?)

**Telling time - Basic structure:**

| Time | French | Pronunciation |
|------|--------|---------------|
| 1:00 | Il est une heure | eel ay oon uhr |
| 2:00 | Il est deux heures | eel ay duh zuhr |
| 3:00 | Il est trois heures | eel ay trwa zuhr |
| 12:00 (noon) | Il est midi | eel ay mee-dee |
| 12:00 (midnight) | Il est minuit | eel ay mee-nwee |

**Important:** Use "**Il est**" + time (not "C'est")

**Minutes:**
- **:15** → et quart (quarter past) - Il est deux heures **et quart** (2:15)
- **:30** → et demie (half past) - Il est trois heures **et demie** (3:30)
- **:45** → moins le quart (quarter to) - Il est quatre heures **moins le quart** (3:45)
- **Other minutes (1-29):** Il est deux heures **cinq** (2:05), Il est trois heures **vingt** (3:20)
- **Minutes (31-59):** Use "moins" (minus) - Il est quatre heures **moins dix** (3:50 - lit. 4 minus 10)

**Examples:**
- Il est huit heures. (It's 8:00.)
- Il est neuf heures et quart. (It's 9:15.)
- Il est dix heures et demie. (It's 10:30.)
- Il est onze heures moins le quart. (It's 10:45.)
- Il est midi et demi. (It's 12:30 PM.) *Note: "demi" not "demie" with midi/minuit
- Il est minuit. (It's midnight.)

**24-hour clock (official time - trains, TV, schedules):**
- Il est treize heures. (It's 13:00 / 1:00 PM.)
- Il est vingt heures trente. (It's 20:30 / 8:30 PM.)
- Il est dix-sept heures quinze. (It's 17:15 / 5:15 PM.)

**3. Days of the Week & Months**

**Days of the week (les jours de la semaine):**

| French | English | Pronunciation |
|--------|---------|---------------|
| lundi | Monday | luhn-dee |
| mardi | Tuesday | mar-dee |
| mercredi | Wednesday | mehr-kruh-dee |
| jeudi | Thursday | zhuh-dee |
| vendredi | Friday | vahn-druh-dee |
| samedi | Saturday | sam-dee |
| dimanche | Sunday | dee-mahnsh |

**Important notes:**
- Days are **masculine**: le lundi (Monday), le mardi (Tuesday)
- No capital letters (unlike English): monday → lundi
- "On Monday" (specific) = lundi (just the word, no article)
- "On Mondays" (habitual) = le lundi (with article)

**Examples:**
- Aujourd'hui, c'est lundi. (Today is Monday.)
- Je travaille lundi. (I work on Monday - this coming Monday.)
- Je travaille le lundi. (I work on Mondays - every Monday.)
- Le cours est mercredi. (The class is on Wednesday.)

**Months (we'll learn these more in Month 2, but here are basics):**
- janvier (January), février (February), mars (March), avril (April)
- Also lowercase: en janvier (in January)

**4. Frequency Adverbs - How Often?**

**Common frequency expressions:**

| French | English | Position |
|--------|---------|----------|
| toujours | always | after verb |
| souvent | often | after verb |
| parfois | sometimes | after verb |
| rarement | rarely | after verb |
| jamais (with ne) | never | after verb |
| tous les jours | every day | end of sentence |
| chaque jour | each day | end of sentence |

**Examples:**
- Je travaille **toujours** le lundi. (I **always** work on Mondays.)
- Il va **souvent** au café. (He **often** goes to the café.)
- Nous mangeons **parfois** au restaurant. (We **sometimes** eat at the restaurant.)
- Elle ne dort **jamais** tard. (She **never** sleeps late.)
- Je fais du sport **tous les jours**. (I exercise **every day**.)

**Daily routine vocabulary:**
- se réveiller (to wake up)
- se lever (to get up)
- prendre le petit-déjeuner (to have breakfast)
- aller au travail (to go to work)
- déjeuner (to have lunch)
- rentrer (to come home)
- dîner (to have dinner)
- se coucher (to go to bed)

**5. Real-World Dialogue Examples**

**Scene 1: Asking About Time**
- A: Quelle heure est-il? (What time is it?)
- B: Il est trois heures et demie. (It's 3:30.)
- A: Déjà? (Already?) Je suis en retard! (I'm late!)
- B: À quelle heure est ton rendez-vous? (What time is your appointment?)
- A: À quatre heures moins le quart. (At 3:45.)

**Scene 2: Describing Routine**
- A: À quelle heure tu te lèves? (What time do you get up?)
- B: Je me lève à sept heures. (I get up at 7:00.)
- A: Tous les jours? (Every day?)
- B: Non, le lundi et le mercredi. Le weekend, je me lève à dix heures. (No, on Mondays and Wednesdays. On weekends, I get up at 10:00.)

**Scene 3: Making Plans**
- A: On se voit quand? (When do we meet?)
- B: Jeudi à huit heures? (Thursday at 8:00?)
- A: Le matin ou le soir? (Morning or evening?)
- B: Le soir. Au restaurant. (Evening. At the restaurant.)
- A: D'accord, à jeudi! (Okay, see you Thursday!)

**Scene 4: Talking About Habits**
- A: Tu fais du sport? (Do you exercise?)
- B: Oui, je vais souvent à la salle de sport. (Yes, I often go to the gym.)
- A: Combien de fois par semaine? (How many times per week?)
- B: Trois fois. Le lundi, mercredi et vendredi. (Three times. Monday, Wednesday, and Friday.)

**Common errors:**
- ❌ "C'est trois heures" → ✅ "Il est trois heures" (use "Il est" for time)
- ❌ "Il est trois heures et demi" → ✅ "Il est trois heures et demie" (demie agrees with "heure" - feminine)
- ❌ "Lundi" (capitalized) → ✅ "lundi" (lowercase in French)
- ❌ "dans lundi" → ✅ "lundi" or "le lundi" (no "in" needed)

### MONTH 1 COMPREHENSIVE EXAM (DELF A1 Mock Format)

**Duration:** 60-90 minutes total  
**Pass Threshold:** 50/100 points  
**Format:** 4 sections (Listening, Reading, Writing, Speaking)

---

#### SECTION 1: LISTENING COMPREHENSION (25 points, 25-30 min)

**Exercise 1: Short Dialogues (10 points)**

Listen to 5 short conversations and answer multiple-choice questions.

**Example:**
- **Audio:** "Bonjour! Quelle heure est-il? - Il est trois heures et demie."
- **Question:** Quelle heure est-il?
  - a) 3:00
  - b) 3:15
  - c) 3:30 ✅
  - d) 3:45
- **Points:** 2

*[5 dialogues × 2 points each]*

**Exercise 2: Longer Conversation (10 points)**

Listen to a conversation about someone's daily routine and answer True/False questions.

**Example Audio:**
"Je m'appelle Marie. Je me lève à sept heures tous les jours. Je prends le petit-déjeuner à huit heures. Je travaille le lundi, mercredi et vendredi. Le weekend, je ne travaille jamais."

**Questions:**
1. Marie se lève à huit heures. (True/False) → **False** (2 pts)
2. Marie travaille trois jours par semaine. (True/False) → **True** (2 pts)
3. Marie travaille le weekend. (True/False) → **False** (2 pts)
4. Marie prend le petit-déjeuner. (True/False) → **True** (2 pts)
5. Marie ne travaille jamais le mardi. (True/False) → **True** (2 pts)

**Exercise 3: Public Announcements (5 points)**

Listen to announcements and identify information (time, place, activity).

**Example Audio:**
"Le train pour Paris part à quatorze heures trente, quai numéro trois."

**Questions:**
- Quelle heure? → 14h30 (1 pt)
- Pour où? → Paris (1 pt)
- Quel quai? → 3 (1 pt)

*[Multiple announcements for 5 points total]*

---

#### SECTION 2: READING COMPREHENSION (25 points, 30 min)

**Exercise 1: Short Texts - Matching (8 points)**

Match 4 short descriptions to appropriate categories/images.

**Example:**
"Je suis étudiant. J'habite à Paris. J'étudie le français et l'anglais."

**Match to:**
- a) Student ✅
- b) Doctor
- c) Teacher
- d) Worker

*[4 texts × 2 points each]*

**Exercise 2: Practical Documents (10 points)**

Read a schedule/menu/sign and answer questions.

**Example Document: Café Menu**
```
CAFÉ DE PARIS
Ouvert tous les jours
Lundi-Vendredi: 7h00 - 20h00
Samedi-Dimanche: 9h00 - 18h00

MENU:
Café: 2€
Croissant: 1,50€
Sandwich: 5€
```

**Questions:**
1. À quelle heure le café ouvre le lundi? (7h00) - 2 pts
2. Combien coûte un café? (2€) - 2 pts
3. Le café est ouvert le dimanche? (Oui) - 2 pts
4. À quelle heure le café ferme le samedi? (18h00) - 2 pts
5. Qu'est-ce qui coûte 1,50€? (Un croissant) - 2 pts

**Exercise 3: Personal Message/Email (7 points)**

Read a short email and answer comprehension questions.

**Example Email:**
```
Salut Sophie!

Comment ça va? Moi, ça va bien. Je travaille beaucoup cette semaine.
On se voit samedi? Je propose le café à 15h00. Tu es libre?

Bisous,
Marie
```

**Questions:**
1. Qui écrit? (Marie) - 1 pt
2. À qui? (Sophie) - 1 pt
3. Quel jour est la proposition? (Samedi) - 2 pts
4. À quelle heure? (15h00) - 2 pts
5. Où? (Au café) - 1 pt

---

#### SECTION 3: WRITTEN PRODUCTION (25 points, 30 min)

**Exercise 1: Form Completion (10 points)**

Fill out a personal information form.

**Example Form:**
```
NOM: _______________
PRÉNOM: _______________
ÂGE: _______________
NATIONALITÉ: _______________
PROFESSION: _______________
ADRESSE: _______________
TÉLÉPHONE: _______________
```

**Grading:**
- Correct format (2 pts)
- Accurate information (4 pts)
- Spelling (2 pts)
- Legibility (2 pts)

**Exercise 2: Short Message (15 points)**

Write a 40-60 word message in one of these scenarios:
- Invite a friend to coffee (suggest day, time, place)
- Describe your daily routine
- Describe your room/house

**Example Response:**
"Salut Marc! Tu es libre samedi? Je propose d'aller au café à 14h00. Le café est près de la gare. On peut parler et prendre un croissant. Tu viens? Réponds-moi! À bientôt, Paul"

**Grading Rubric:**
- Task completion (5 pts) - Did you answer all parts?
- Vocabulary appropriateness (4 pts)
- Grammar accuracy (3 pts)
- Spelling (2 pts)
- Coherence (1 pt)

---

#### SECTION 4: SPEAKING PRODUCTION (25 points, 15-20 min)

**Part 1: Guided Interview (10 points, 5 min)**

Answer questions about yourself.

**Example Questions:**
- Comment tu t'appelles?
- Où est-ce que tu habites?
- Quelle est ta nationalité?
- Qu'est-ce que tu fais comme travail/études?
- Tu parles quelles langues?
- Quel âge as-tu?

**Grading:**
- Comprehension of questions (3 pts)
- Accuracy of answers (4 pts)
- Pronunciation (2 pts)
- Fluency (1 pt)

**Part 2: Interactive Dialogue (10 points, 5-7 min)**

Exchange information in a scenario (at café, making plans, shopping).

**Example Scenario: At a Café**
- Student plays customer
- Examiner plays waiter
- Student must: greet, order items, ask price, ask time, pay

**Grading:**
- Task completion (4 pts)
- Appropriate vocabulary (3 pts)
- Grammar accuracy (2 pts)
- Interaction ability (1 pt)

**Part 3: Monologue (5 points, 3-5 min)**

Speak for 1-2 minutes on a topic:
- Your daily routine
- Your family/friends
- Your hobbies
- Your town/city

**Grading:**
- Content (2 pts)
- Vocabulary range (1 pt)
- Grammar (1 pt)
- Pronunciation/fluency (1 pt)

### Metadata
- **CEFR Level:** A1.1

- **Vocabulary Domain:** Objects, colors, basic items

- **Duration:** 30 minutes
- **Day Number:** 11

---

### GRAMMAR SECTION (10 min)

#### Explanation (5-paragraph teacher format)

**1. Why Does French Have Gender?**

Every French noun is either **masculine** (masculin) or **feminine** (féminin). This isn't about biology—it's a grammatical category. A table (**la table**) is feminine, but a desk (**le bureau**) is masculine. This gender determines which article (le/la), adjective form, and pronouns you use. Unlike English (where "the" works for everything), French requires you to learn the gender of each noun. Don't worry—there are patterns to help!

**2. How to Recognize Gender: Articles**

The article tells you the gender:

| Article | Gender | Example | English |
|---------|--------|---------|---------|
| **le** | Masculine | le café | the café |
| **la** | Feminine | la table | the table |
| **l'** | Both (before vowel/h) | l'ami (M), l'amie (F) | the friend |
| **un** | Masculine | un livre | a book |
| **une** | Feminine | une chaise | a chair |

**Key tip:** Memorize nouns WITH their article: "le café" not just "café", "la table" not just "table".

**3. Common Gender Patterns & Endings**

**Typically Masculine (-age, -eau, -ment, -eur):**
- **-age:** le fromage (cheese), le garage (garage), le voyage (trip)
- **-eau:** le bureau (desk), le château (castle), le cadeau (gift)
- **-ment:** le gouvernement (government), le moment (moment)
- **-eur:** le professeur (teacher - M), le danseur (dancer - M)

**Typically Feminine (-tion, -sion, -té, -ette, -esse):**
- **-tion:** la nation (nation), la station (station), la question (question)
- **-sion:** la passion (passion), la mission (mission)
- **-té:** la liberté (freedom), la santé (health), la beauté (beauty)
- **-ette:** la baguette (baguette), la serviette (napkin), la tablette (tablet)
- **-esse:** la princesse (princess), la vitesse (speed)

**Exceptions exist! Always check.**

**4. Real-World Dialogue Examples**

**Scene 1: Shopping**
- A: "Je cherche **un livre** de français." (I'm looking for a French book. - M)
- B: "Voici **une table** avec des livres." (Here's a table with books. - F)
- A: "Merci! Et **le café**?" (Thanks! And the café? - M)
- B: "**Le café** est à gauche." (The café is on the left.)

**Scene 2: Describing Objects**
- A: "C'est **un stylo** ou **une gomme**?" (Is it a pen or an eraser?)
- B: "C'est **un stylo** bleu." (It's a blue pen. - M)
- A: "Et ça, c'est quoi?" (And that, what is it?)
- B: "C'est **une chaise**." (It's a chair. - F)

**Scene 3: At the Office**
- A: "Où est **le bureau**?" (Where is the desk? - M)
- B: "**Le bureau** est ici. Et **la lampe**?" (The desk is here. And the lamp? - F)
- A: "**La lampe** est sur **la table**." (The lamp is on the table. - F)

**5. How to Learn Gender Efficiently**

- **Visual trick:** Imagine masculine nouns in BLUE, feminine in RED (colors help memory!)
- **Flashcards:** Write "le café" not "café" - learn with the article
- **Grouping:** Group by ending pattern (-tion words = feminine 95% of time)
- **Practice:** Repeat "le/un" or "la/une" when learning new words

**Common errors:**
- ❌ "La stylo" → ✅ "Le stylo" (pen is masculine)
- ❌ "Un table" → ✅ "Une table" (table is feminine)
- ❌ "Le question" → ✅ "La question" (-tion = feminine)

## DAY 12 - A1.1.12: Number (Singular & Plural)

### Metadata
- **CEFR Level:** A1.1
- **Grammar Topic:** Plural formation for nouns and articles
- **Vocabulary Domain:** Objects, quantities, colors
- **Content Identifiers:** plural_rules, fill_blank, listening, multiple_choice
- **Speaking Tier:** 1 (Script-based with quantity practice)
- **Duration:** 30 minutes
- **Day Number:** 12

---

### GRAMMAR SECTION (10 min)

#### Explanation (5-paragraph teacher format)

**1. What is Number in French?**

In French, nouns can be **singular** (one item) or **plural** (more than one). Just like in English, we add markers to show plural, but French has specific rules. The article changes, and usually we add **-s** to the noun (though you don't pronounce it!). Plural affects articles, adjectives, and verb agreement. Example: "**un livre**" (a book) → "**des livres**" (some books).

**2. How Articles Change in Plural**

| Singular | Plural | Example Singular | Example Plural |
|----------|--------|------------------|----------------|
| le | les | le stylo (the pen) | les stylos (the pens) |
| la | les | la table (the table) | les tables (the tables) |
| l' | les | l'ami (the friend) | les amis (the friends) |
| un | des | un livre (a book) | des livres (books/some books) |
| une | des | une chaise (a chair) | des chaises (chairs/some chairs) |

**Key pattern:** Both masculine AND feminine use **"les"** in plural! Gender doesn't matter for "the" in plural.

**3. Plural Formation Rules**

**Rule 1: Most nouns → Add -s (not pronounced!)**
- un chat → des chat**s** (a cat → cats)
- une table → des table**s** (a table → tables)
- le stylo → les stylo**s** (the pen → the pens)

**Rule 2: Nouns ending in -s, -x, -z → NO CHANGE**
- un pays → des pays (a country → countries) - already has -s
- une voix → des voix (a voice → voices) - already has -x
- un nez → des nez (a nose → noses) - already has -z

**Rule 3: Nouns ending in -eau, -au, -eu → Add -x**
- un bureau → des bureau**x** (a desk → desks)
- un château → des château**x** (a castle → castles)
- un cheveu → des cheveu**x** (a hair → hairs)

**Rule 4: Some -al → -aux**
- un animal → des anim**aux** (an animal → animals)
- un journal → des journ**aux** (a newspaper → newspapers)

**Irregular plurals:**
- un œil → des yeux (an eye → eyes)
- monsieur → messieurs (sir → sirs)
- madame → mesdames (madam → madams)

**4. Real-World Dialogue Examples**

**Scene 1: Shopping for School Supplies**
- A: "J'ai besoin de **stylos**." (I need pens. - plural)
- B: "Combien de stylos?" (How many pens?)
- A: "Trois **stylos** bleus, s'il vous plaît." (Three blue pens, please.)

**Scene 2: Describing a Classroom**
- A: "Il y a combien de **tables**?" (How many tables are there?)
- B: "Il y a dix **tables** et vingt **chaises**." (There are 10 tables and 20 chairs.)
- A: "Et **les livres**?" (And the books?)
- B: "**Les livres** sont sur **les tables**." (The books are on the tables.)

**Scene 3: At Home**
- A: "Où sont **les clés**?" (Where are the keys? - plural)
- B: "**Les clés** sont sur **le bureau**." (The keys are on the desk.)
- A: "Et **les chaises**?" (And the chairs?)
- B: "**Les chaises** sont dans la cuisine." (The chairs are in the kitchen.)

**5. Common Errors & Practice Tips**

**Common errors:**
- ❌ "Les stylo" → ✅ "Les stylos" (must add -s even if not pronounced)
- ❌ "Des bureau" → ✅ "Des bureaux" (-eau → -eaux in plural)
- ❌ "Un animaux" → ✅ "Un animal" (animaux is plural only)
- ❌ "Le livres" → ✅ "Les livres" (plural article = les, not le)

**Practice tip:** Count objects aloud: "un stylo, deux stylos, trois stylos" to reinforce plural forms!

## DAY 13 - A1.1.13: Article Agreement (Gender + Number)

### Metadata
- **CEFR Level:** A1.1
- **Grammar Topic:** Complete article agreement (definite, indefinite, with gender and number)
- **Vocabulary Domain:** Objects, descriptions, colors
- **Content Identifiers:** article_agreement, fill_blank, listening, multiple_choice
- **Speaking Tier:** 1 (Script-based with article practice)
- **Duration:** 30 minutes
- **Day Number:** 13

---

### GRAMMAR SECTION (10 min)

#### Explanation (5-paragraph teacher format)

**1. What is Article Agreement?**

In French, articles must **agree** with the noun they accompany in BOTH gender (M/F) and number (S/P). This means you need to choose the correct form based on TWO factors. For example: "le stylo" (M, S) but "les stylos" (M, P) and "la table" (F, S) but "les tables" (F, P). This agreement is fundamental to French grammar—get this right, and everything else becomes easier!

**2. Complete Article Chart**

**Definite Articles (the):**

| | Masculine | Feminine | Vowel/h |
|---------|-----------|----------|---------|
| **Singular** | le | la | l' |
| **Plural** | les | les | les |

**Examples:**
- le stylo (the pen - M, S) → les stylos (the pens - M, P)
- la table (the table - F, S) → les tables (the tables - F, P)
- l'ami (the friend - M, S) → les amis (the friends - M, P)
- l'école (the school - F, S) → les écoles (the schools - F, P)

**Indefinite Articles (a/an, some):**

| | Masculine | Feminine |
|---------|-----------|----------|
| **Singular** | un | une |
| **Plural** | des | des |

**Examples:**
- un livre (a book - M, S) → des livres (books - M, P)
- une chaise (a chair - F, S) → des chaises (chairs - F, P)

**3. How to Choose the Correct Article (Decision Tree)**

**Step 1:** Is the noun specific/known or general/unknown?
- Specific → Use definite (le/la/les)
- General → Use indefinite (un/une/des)

**Step 2:** What is the gender?
- Masculine → le/un
- Feminine → la/une

**Step 3:** Is it singular or plural?
- Singular → le/la/un/une
- Plural → les/des

**Step 4:** Does it start with a vowel or silent h?
- Yes + Singular → l' (both genders)
- No → Use regular form

**Examples in context:**
- "Je cherche **le livre**." (I'm looking for **the book** - specific, M, S)
- "Je veux **un café**." (I want **a coffee** - general, M, S)
- "**Les tables** sont grandes." (**The tables** are big - specific, P)
- "Il y a **des chaises**." (There are **chairs** - general, P)

**4. Real-World Dialogue Examples**

**Scene 1: In a Bookstore**
- A: "Excusez-moi, où sont **les livres** de français?" (Excuse me, where are the French books? - specific, P)
- B: "**Les livres** sont là-bas." (The books are over there.)
- A: "Je cherche **un livre** pour débutants." (I'm looking for a book for beginners. - general, M, S)
- B: "Voici **des livres** parfaits!" (Here are some perfect books! - general, P)

**Scene 2: At a Café**
- A: "Bonjour! **Un café** et **une eau**, s'il vous plaît." (Hello! A coffee and a water, please. - general)
- Waiter: "**Le café** est chaud." (The coffee is hot. - specific)
- A: "Merci! Où sont **les toilettes**?" (Thanks! Where are the restrooms? - specific, P)
- Waiter: "**Les toilettes** sont à droite." (The restrooms are on the right.)

**Scene 3: Describing a Room**
- A: "Dans ma chambre, il y a **un lit**, **une table** et **des chaises**." (In my room, there is a bed, a table, and chairs.)
- B: "**Le lit** est grand?" (Is the bed big? - specific)
- A: "Oui, **le lit** est très grand. Et **la table** est petite." (Yes, the bed is very big. And the table is small.)

**5. Common Errors & Mastery Tips**

**Common errors:**
- ❌ "Un table" → ✅ "Une table" (table is feminine)
- ❌ "La stylos" → ✅ "Les stylos" (plural = les, not la)
- ❌ "Le ami" → ✅ "L'ami" (vowel = l' not le)
- ❌ "Des bureau" → ✅ "Des bureaux" (plural of bureau = bureaux)

- **Duration:** 30 minutes
- **Day Number:** 14

---

### GRAMMAR SECTION (10 min)

#### Explanation (5-paragraph teacher format)

**1. What is Adjective Agreement?**

In French, adjectives must **agree** with the noun they describe in BOTH gender (M/F) and number (S/P). Unlike English (where "blue" stays "blue" for anything), French adjectives change form. Example: "un stylo **bleu**" (M, S) but "une table **bleue**" (F, S) and "des stylos **bleus**" (M, P) and "des tables **bleues**" (F, P). Master this, and your French will sound natural!

**2. How Adjectives Change Forms**

**Rule 1: Masculine → Feminine (add -e)**
- petit → petit**e** (small)
- grand → grand**e** (big)
- français → français**e** (French)

**Rule 2: Singular → Plural (add -s)**
- petit → petit**s** (M plural)
- petite → petite**s** (F plural)

**Complete Chart Example: petit (small)**

| | Masculine | Feminine |
|---------|-----------|----------|
| **Singular** | petit | petite |
| **Plural** | petits | petites |

**Examples:**
- un **petit** livre (a small book - M, S)
- une **petite** table (a small table - F, S)
- des **petits** livres (small books - M, P)
- des **petites** tables (small tables - F, P)

**Special cases:**
- Already ends in -e → NO CHANGE for feminine: rouge (M) = rouge (F)
- Already ends in -s or -x → NO CHANGE for M plural: français (M, S) = français (M, P)

**3. Adjective Placement: Before or After the Noun?**

**MOST adjectives come AFTER the noun:**
- Colors: un stylo **bleu** (a blue pen)
- Nationality: un restaurant **français** (a French restaurant)
- Long descriptions: une voiture **confortable** (a comfortable car)

**Some common adjectives come BEFORE the noun (BANGS rule):**
- **B**eauty: beau/belle (beautiful), joli/jolie (pretty)
- **A**ge: jeune (young), vieux/vieille (old), nouveau/nouvelle (new)
- **N**umber: un, deux, plusieurs (several)
- **G**oodness: bon/bonne (good), mauvais/mauvaise (bad)
- **S**ize: grand/grande (big), petit/petite (small), gros/grosse (fat)

**Examples:**
- BEFORE: un **petit** chat (a small cat) - size
- BEFORE: un **beau** livre (a beautiful book) - beauty
- AFTER: un chat **noir** (a black cat) - color
- AFTER: une table **française** (a French table) - nationality

**Both possible (meaning change):**
- un homme **grand** (a tall man) vs. un **grand** homme (a great man)
- ma voiture **propre** (my clean car) vs. ma **propre** voiture (my own car)

**4. Real-World Dialogue Examples**

**Scene 1: Shopping for Furniture**
- A: "Je cherche une **petite table blanche**." (I'm looking for a small white table. - BEFORE + AFTER)
- B: "Voici une **jolie table blanche**." (Here's a pretty white table. - BEFORE + AFTER)
- A: "Parfait! Et une **grande chaise rouge**?" (Perfect! And a big red chair?)
- B: "Nous avons des **grandes chaises rouges**." (We have big red chairs. - P)

**Scene 2: Describing People**
- A: "C'est ton ami?" (Is that your friend?)
- B: "Oui, c'est un **jeune homme sympathique**." (Yes, he's a nice young man. - BEFORE + AFTER)
- A: "Et elle?" (And her?)
- B: "C'est une **belle femme française**." (She's a beautiful French woman. - BEFORE + AFTER)

**Scene 3: At a Bookstore**
- A: "Tu as un **bon livre**?" (Do you have a good book? - BEFORE)
- B: "Oui, j'ai un **nouveau livre intéressant**." (Yes, I have a new interesting book. - BEFORE + AFTER)
- A: "C'est un livre **français**?" (Is it a French book? - AFTER)
- B: "Oui, un **grand livre français**." (Yes, a great French book. - BEFORE + AFTER)

**5. Common Errors & Mastery Strategy**

**Common errors:**
- ❌ "Une petit table" → ✅ "Une petite table" (feminine = -e)
- ❌ "Des grande chaises" → ✅ "Des grandes chaises" (plural = -s)
- ❌ "Un bleu stylo" → ✅ "Un stylo bleu" (color comes AFTER)
- ❌ "Une table petit" → ✅ "Une petite table" (size comes BEFORE)

**Mastery strategy:**
1. **Learn BANGS adjectives** (these come BEFORE) - all others go AFTER
2. **Always think:** Gender? Number? Position?
3. **Practice formula:** Article + BANGS adj + NOUN + other adj
   - Example: une **petite** table **rouge** (a small red table)
4. **Color code practice:** Write BEFORE adjectives in green, AFTER in orange

### SPEAKING EXERCISE (10 min) - Week 3 Comprehensive Practice

#### Format: Tier 1 (Script-based with cumulative variation)

**Scenario: Describing Your Room (Multi-turn dialogue)**

**Script Provided (Student practices):**
```
A: Comment est ta chambre? (How is your room?)
B: Ma chambre est grande et jolie. (My room is big and pretty.)
A: Qu'est-ce qu'il y a dans ta chambre? (What's in your room?)
B: Il y a un lit, une table, et des chaises. (There's a bed, a table, and chairs.)
A: Comment est la table? (How is the table?)
B: C'est une petite table blanche. (It's a small white table.)
A: Et les chaises? (And the chairs?)
B: Les chaises sont grandes et rouges. (The chairs are big and red.)
```

**Variation Prompts (Student substitutes):**
1. Change colors: "bleu", "vert", "noir", "jaune"
2. Change sizes: "grand/grande", "petit/petite"
3. Add BANGS adjectives: "beau/belle", "nouveau/nouvelle", "bon/bonne"
4. Describe different objects: "bureau", "fenêtre", "porte", "voiture"

**Guided Personalization:**
- Describe your real room with 5 objects (use correct article + 1-2 adjectives each)
- Example: "Dans ma chambre, il y a un grand lit blanc, une petite table rouge, des jolies chaises bleues, une belle fenêtre, et un nouveau bureau noir."

**Recording & Evaluation:**
- Student records full room description (8-10 sentences)
- Auto-evaluation checklist:
  - ✅ Used correct articles (le/la/les/un/une/des)?
  - ✅ Adjectives agree in gender/number?
  - ✅ BANGS adjectives placed BEFORE noun?
  - ✅ Color adjectives placed AFTER noun?
- Feedback: Comprehensive grammar check + pronunciation

### Metadata

---

### GRAMMAR SECTION (10 min)

#### Explanation

**1. Two Past Tenses, Two Different Jobs**

French uses **two main past tenses** for storytelling: **passé composé (PC)** and **imparfait**. Think of **passé composé** as **events** (finished actions, specific moments), and **imparfait** as **background** (ongoing actions, habits, descriptions). When you tell a story, you usually **set the scene in imparfait** and then **mark key actions in passé composé**.

**2. The Core Rule: Events vs. Background**

- **Passé composé = completed, specific actions**
  - One action finished in the past
  - A moment that moved the story forward
  - Example: **J'ai acheté** un billet. (I bought a ticket.)

- **Imparfait = ongoing or habitual past**
  - Repeated habits
  - Descriptions and feelings
  - Background actions
  - Example: **J'attendais** le train. (I was waiting for the train.)

**3. Use Time Markers to Choose the Tense**

**Passé composé markers:**
- hier (yesterday), tout à coup (suddenly), une fois (once), ce matin (this morning), à 8h (at 8)

**Imparfait markers:**
- toujours (always), souvent (often), tous les jours (every day), pendant que (while), quand j'étais (when I was)

**4. Scene vs. Action Example**

**Scene (imparfait):**
- Il **faisait** froid et il **pleuvait**. (It was cold and raining.)
- Nous **marchions** dans la rue. (We were walking in the street.)

**Action (passé composé):**
- Soudain, un taxi **s'est arrêté**. (Suddenly, a taxi stopped.)
- Je **suis monté** dedans. (I got in.)

**5. Your Story Toolkit (Mini Template)**

Use this pattern when you speak:
- **Imparfait** to set the scene
- **Passé composé** for what happened

Example:
- **Pendant que** je **regardais** la vitrine, un ami **m'a appelé**.
  (While I was looking at the shop window, a friend called me.)

**Cultural Note:**
French storytelling relies on this contrast. In films and novels, the background is almost always imparfait, while important events are in passé composé. This gives stories rhythm and clarity.

## DAY 47 - A2.1.47: Completed Actions vs. Ongoing Background

### Metadata
- **CEFR Level:** A2.1
- **Grammar Topic:** PC vs. imparfait - completed actions vs. ongoing/background
- **Vocabulary Domain:** Travel actions, movement, background descriptions
- **Content Identifiers:** pc_vs_imparfait, completed_action, background, contrast
- **Speaking Tier:** 2 (Guided conversation)
- **Duration:** 30 minutes
- **Day Number:** 47

---

### GRAMMAR SECTION (10 min)

#### Explanation

**1. Completed Action = Passé Composé**

Use **passé composé** for actions that **start and end** in the past. These are the main events in a story.
- Je **suis arrivé** à 18h. (I arrived at 6.)
- Nous **avons visité** le musée. (We visited the museum.)

**2. Ongoing Background = Imparfait**

Use **imparfait** for actions that were **in progress** or **set the scene**.
- Il **faisait** beau. (The weather was nice.)
- Nous **marchions** le long de la Seine. (We were walking along the Seine.)

**3. How to Combine Them**

A typical story sentence looks like this:
- **Imparfait + quand + passé composé**
- Je **parlais** avec un ami quand le train **est arrivé**.

**4. Time Expressions to Help You**

**Passé composé:**
- hier, tout à coup, soudain, à 8h, une fois

**Imparfait:**
- pendant que, toujours, souvent, d'habitude, chaque semaine

**5. Real-World Travel Example**

- Il **faisait** chaud et nous **marchions** vers la gare.
- Soudain, un bus **est passé** très vite.
- Nous **avons traversé** la rue et nous **sommes montés** dedans.

**Cultural Note:**
French news reports and personal stories often alternate these tenses. Background is imparfait, the headline action is passé composé.

## DAY 48 - A2.1.48: Interruptions (One Action Interrupts Another)

### Metadata
- **CEFR Level:** A2.1
- **Grammar Topic:** PC vs. imparfait - interruption pattern (ongoing action + event)
- **Vocabulary Domain:** Unexpected events, reactions
- **Content Identifiers:** interruption, pc_vs_imparfait, quand, tout_a_coup
- **Speaking Tier:** 2 (Guided conversation)
- **Duration:** 30 minutes
- **Day Number:** 48

---

### GRAMMAR SECTION (10 min)

#### Explanation

**1. The Interruption Pattern**

A classic French past structure is:
- **Imparfait (ongoing action) + quand + passé composé (interruption)**

Example:
- Je **lisais** quand la lumière **s'est éteinte**.
  (I was reading when the light went out.)

**2. Why This Pattern Works**

The ongoing action was in progress (imparfait). The sudden event interrupted it (passé composé). This helps listeners picture the timeline.

**3. Common Interruption Markers**

- **quand** (when)
- **tout à coup** (all of a sudden)
- **soudain** (suddenly)
- **pendant que** (while)

**4. Examples with Real-Life Situations**

- Je **dormais** quand le téléphone **a sonné**.
- Nous **mangions** quand le serveur **est arrivé**.
- Elle **parlait** quand le bus **est parti**.

**5. Making It Natural**

Add details in imparfait to set the scene, then add the interruption in PC.
- Il **faisait** nuit et nous **marchions** quand une voiture **s'est arrêtée**.

**Cultural Note:**
French speakers often use this pattern in everyday conversation, especially when recounting surprises or problems.

## DAY 49 - A2.1.49: Narrative Building (Telling Complete Stories)

### Metadata
- **CEFR Level:** A2.1
- **Grammar Topic:** Narrative building with PC + imparfait
- **Vocabulary Domain:** Sequencing, storytelling connectors
- **Content Identifiers:** narration, pc_vs_imparfait, connectors, storytelling
- **Speaking Tier:** 2→3 (Guided to freer)
- **Duration:** 30 minutes
- **Day Number:** 49

---

### GRAMMAR SECTION (10 min)

#### Explanation

**1. Storytelling = Mixing Both Tenses**

When you tell a full story, you **need both tenses**. Imparfait sets the scene and describes the background. Passé composé moves the story forward with actions and events.

**2. Story Structure**

- **Setting (imparfait):** Where, when, atmosphere
- **Actions (passé composé):** What happened
- **Reaction (passé composé or imparfait):** Feelings or consequences

**3. Helpful Connectors**

- **D'abord** (first)
- **Ensuite** (then)
- **Puis** (then)
- **Enfin** (finally)

These connectors often introduce **passé composé** actions, while descriptions in between stay in **imparfait**.

**4. Example Story**

- Il **faisait** nuit et nous **marchions** dans la rue.
- **D'abord**, un chien **a aboyé**.
- **Ensuite**, une voiture **s'est arrêtée**.
- **Enfin**, nous **sommes rentrés** à la maison.

**5. Fluency Tip**

Keep your story clear: use 2-3 imparfait sentences for the background, then 2-3 passé composé sentences for events.

**Cultural Note:**
French speakers often start stories with "Il était une fois..." (imparfait), then move to actions in passé composé to keep the listener engaged.

## DAY 50 - A2.1.50: Week 10 Review & Assessment

### Metadata

- **Vocabulary Domain:** Mixed review, narration and travel

- **Speaking Tier:** 2→3 (Guided to freer)
- **Duration:** 30 minutes
- **Day Number:** 50

---

### GRAMMAR SECTION (10 min)

#### Explanation

**1. Review: The Two-Past System**

- **Imparfait:** background, habits, descriptions, ongoing actions
- **Passé composé:** completed actions, specific events

**2. Quick Decision Guide**

Ask yourself:
- Was it an ongoing action or a habit? → **Imparfait**
- Was it a finished event? → **Passé composé**

**3. Common Patterns**

- **Imparfait + quand + PC**
- **Pendant que + imparfait**, then **PC**
- **Connectors** (d'abord, ensuite, enfin) + **PC**

**4. Mini Review Example**

- Il **faisait** beau et nous **marchions**.
- Tout à coup, un événement **a changé** la situation.
- Ensuite, nous **sommes rentrés**.

**5. Confidence Tip**

If you are unsure, start with **imparfait** for the scene, then add **passé composé** for the event. This is the most natural structure for French narratives.

**Cultural Note:**
French teachers often say: "Imparfait = decor, passé composé = action." Keep that in mind.

### GRAMMAR SECTION (10 min)

#### Explanation

**1. Direct vs. Indirect Speech**

- **Direct:** Paul dit : "Je suis fatiguÃ©." (Paul says: "I am tired.")
- **Indirect:** Paul dit **qu'il est** fatiguÃ©. (Paul says **that he is** tired.)

**2. The Connector "QUE"**
Use **que** (that) to connect the introduction verb and the statement.

- **Que** becomes **qu'** before a vowel.
- "Il dit **que** je suis gentil."
- "Il dit **qu'il** aime Paris."

**3. Key Changes**
When moving from Direct to Indirect, you must change:

- **Pronouns:** "Je" becomes "Il/Elle". "Mon" becomes "Son".
- **Verbs:** Match the new subject.

**Example:**

- Direct: Marie dit : "**J'**aime **mon** chien."
- Indirect: Marie dit **qu'elle** aime **son** chien.

**4. Reporting Verbs**

- **Dire** (to say) -> Il dit que...
- **Affirmer** (to affirm) -> Il affirme que...
- **Demander** (to ask) -> Il demande si... (if yes/no question)
- **RÃ©pondre** (to answer) -> Il rÃ©pond que...

**Cultural Note:**
In spoken French, "que" is essential. In English, we often drop "that" ("He says he is tired"), but in French, you CANNOT drop "que". You MUST say "Il dit **que**..."

### GRAMMAR SECTION (10 min)

#### Explanation

**1. Reporting Yes/No Questions ("Si")**
When reporting a question that starts with "Est-ce que" or is a Yes/No question, use **SI** (if).

- Direct: Il demande : "Est-ce que tu viens?" (Are you coming?)
- Indirect: Il demande **si** tu viens. (He asks **if** you are coming.)

**2. Reporting "What" Questions ("Ce que")**
When the questions starts with "Qu'est-ce que" (What), use **CE QUE**.

- Direct: Il demande : "Qu'est-ce que tu fais?" (What are you doing?)
- Indirect: Il demande **ce que** tu fais. (He asks **what** you are doing.)

**3. Reporting Commands ("De + Infinitive")**
When reporting an order/imperative, use **DE** + **Infinitive**.

- Direct: Il dit : "Mange ta soupe!" (Eat your soup!)
- Indirect: Il me dit **de manger** ma soupe. (He tells me **to eat** my soup.)
- Direct: "Ne pars pas!" (Don't leave!)
- Indirect: Il me dit **de ne pas partir**. (He tells me **not to leave**.)

**4. Summary Table**

| Direct Type | Connectord | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Statement | QUE | Il dit **que** c'est bon. |
| Yes/No Question | SI | Il demande **si** c'est bon. |
| "What" Question | CE QUE | Il demande **ce que** c'est. |
| Command | DE + Inf | Il dit **de** manger. |

**Cultural Note:**
"Ce que" is tricky for learners. English speakers want to say "Il demande quoi tu fais" -> This is very incorrect. Always "Il demande **ce que** tu fais."

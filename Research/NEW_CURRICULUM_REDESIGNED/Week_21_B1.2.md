## DAY 101 - B1.2.101: La Voix Passive (Formation)

### Metadata

- **CEFR Level:** B1.2
- **Grammar Topic:** Passive voice formation (subject becomes receiver of the action)
- **Vocabulary Domain:** News, media, events
- **Content Identifiers:** voix_passive, passive_voice, etre_past_participle, grammar_advanced
- **Speaking Tier:** 3
- **Duration:** 30 minutes
- **Day Number:** 101

---

### GRAMMAR SECTION (10 min)

#### Explanation

**1. Active vs. Passive**
In an **active** sentence, the subject does the action.
In a **passive** sentence, the subject receives the action.

- **Active:** Le chat mange la souris. (The cat eats the mouse.)
- **Passive:** La souris **est mangée** par le chat. (The mouse **is eaten** by the cat.)

**2. Formation**
**Subject + ÊTRE (conjugated) + Past Participle + PAR + Agent**

The key: **ÊTRE** carries the tense, and the **past participle agrees** with the subject (gender + number).

| Tense | Active | Passive |
| --- | --- | --- |
| **Présent** | Le chef prépare le repas. | Le repas **est préparé** par le chef. |
| **Passé composé** | Le chef a préparé le repas. | Le repas **a été préparé** par le chef. |
| **Imparfait** | Le chef préparait le repas. | Le repas **était préparé** par le chef. |
| **Futur simple** | Le chef préparera le repas. | Le repas **sera préparé** par le chef. |
| **Plus-que-parfait** | Le chef avait préparé le repas. | Le repas **avait été préparé** par le chef. |

**3. Agreement Rule**
The past participle **ALWAYS agrees** with the subject in passive voice:

- Le livre **est lu**. (The book is read. — Masculine singular)
- La lettre **est lue**. (The letter is read. — Feminine singular)
- Les livres **sont lus**. (The books are read. — Masculine plural)
- Les lettres **sont lues**. (The letters are read. — Feminine plural)

**4. PAR vs. DE**
Usually **par** (by) introduces the agent. But with verbs of emotion/state, use **de**:

- Il est aimé **de** tous. (He is loved **by** all.)
- Elle est connue **de** tout le monde. (She is known **by** everyone.)
- La ville est entourée **de** montagnes. (The city is surrounded **by** mountains.)

**Cultural Note:**
French uses the passive voice less often than English. In many cases, French prefers the active voice with "on" (one/we/they): "On a volé mon vélo." instead of "Mon vélo a été volé." Both are correct, but the active "on" construction is much more natural in spoken French.

## DAY 102 - B1.2.102: La Voix Passive (Pratique & Alternatives)

### Metadata

- **CEFR Level:** B1.2
- **Grammar Topic:** Passive voice alternatives (ON, se faire, reflexive passive)
- **Vocabulary Domain:** Media, everyday events, processes
- **Content Identifiers:** voix_passive_alternatives, on_construction, se_faire, grammar_advanced
- **Speaking Tier:** 3
- **Duration:** 30 minutes
- **Day Number:** 102

---

### GRAMMAR SECTION (10 min)

#### Explanation

**1. Why Avoid the Passive?**
French often prefers alternatives to the passive voice. The passive sounds formal/written. In speech, French people use these alternatives:

**2. Alternative 1: ON (Most common!)**

- Passive: "Le français **est parlé** au Canada."
- Active with ON: "**On parle** français au Canada." ← More natural!
- Passive: "Mon vélo **a été volé**."
- Active with ON: "**On a volé** mon vélo." ← Much more natural!

**3. Alternative 2: SE FAIRE + Infinitif (To get something done to you)**
Used when the subject is affected (often negatively):

- "Il **s'est fait voler** son portefeuille." (He **got his wallet stolen**.)
- "Elle **s'est fait renvoyer**." (She **got fired**.)
- "Je me suis fait couper les cheveux." (I got my hair cut — neutral.)

**4. Alternative 3: Reflexive Passive (Se + Verb)**
Used for general processes, rules, or customs:

- "Le vin **se boit** frais." (Wine **is drunk** chilled — it's the custom.)
- "Ça **ne se dit pas.**" (That **is not said** — it's not done.)
- "Ce plat **se mange** froid." (This dish **is eaten** cold.)
- "Le français **se parle** dans 29 pays." (French **is spoken** in 29 countries.)

**5. When TO USE true passive:**

- Formal writing, news reports, scientific texts.
- When the agent (par...) is important to mention.
- "Ce tableau a été peint **par Picasso**." (The agent matters!)

**Cultural Note:**
If you ever watch French news, you'll hear a mix of passive ("a été arrêté par la police") and active with ON ("on a retrouvé le suspect"). In casual conversation, almost nobody uses the full passive construction — they use ON or se faire.

## DAY 103 - B1.2.103: Le Participe Présent

### Metadata

- **CEFR Level:** B1.2
- **Grammar Topic:** Present participle formation and usage
- **Vocabulary Domain:** Descriptions, simultaneous actions
- **Content Identifiers:** participe_present, ant_form, descriptive, grammar_advanced
- **Speaking Tier:** 3
- **Duration:** 30 minutes
- **Day Number:** 103

---

### GRAMMAR SECTION (10 min)

#### Explanation

**1. What is the Participe Présent?**
It's the French equivalent of the English "-ing" form used as an **adjective or descriptive clause**.
Formation: Take the **nous** form of the present tense → remove **-ons** → add **-ant**.

| Verb | Nous form | Participe Présent |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Parler | Parlons | **Parlant** |
| Finir | Finissons | **Finissant** |
| Prendre | Prenons | **Prenant** |
| Manger | Mangeons | **Mangeant** |
| Voir | Voyons | **Voyant** |

**2. Three Irregulars**

- **Être** → **Étant** (Not "somant"!)
- **Avoir** → **Ayant** (Not "avonant"!)
- **Savoir** → **Sachant** (Not "savonant"!)

**3. Usage 1: Replacing a Relative Clause**
The participe présent can replace **qui + verb**:

- "Les personnes **qui parlent** français..." → "Les personnes **parlant** français..."
- "Un homme **qui porte** un chapeau..." → "Un homme **portant** un chapeau..."

**4. Usage 2: Expressing Cause/Reason**
It can explain WHY:

- "**Étant** malade, je suis resté chez moi." (**Being** sick, I stayed home.)
- "**N'ayant** pas d'argent, il n'a pas mangé." (**Not having** money, he didn't eat.)

**5. Participe Présent vs. Adjective**
⚠️ When the -ant word is used as an **adjective**, it AGREES (gender + number). When it's a **participe présent** (verbal action), it DOES NOT agree.

- Adjective: "Une histoire **intéressante**." (An interesting story — agrees!)
- Participe présent: "Une histoire **intéressant** tout le monde." (A story interesting everyone — no agreement!)

**Cultural Note:**
The participe présent is more common in written French (literature, administrative texts, journalism). In spoken French, people usually prefer relative clauses ("les gens qui parlent" instead of "les gens parlant"). But understanding it is essential for reading French texts.

### Metadata

- **CEFR Level:** B1.2

- **Vocabulary Domain:** Daily actions, multitasking, manner
- **Content Identifiers:** gerondif, en_ant, simultaneous_actions, manner, grammar_advanced
- **Speaking Tier:** 3
- **Duration:** 30 minutes
- **Day Number:** 104

---

### GRAMMAR SECTION (10 min)

#### Explanation

**1. What is the Gérondif?**
The gérondif is **EN + Participe Présent** (-ant form). It expresses:

- **Simultaneous actions** (while doing X, Y happens)
- **Manner/How** (by doing X)
- **Condition** (if you do X)

**2. Formation**
**EN** + Participe Présent (nous-stem + ant)

- EN parlant (while/by speaking)
- EN mangeant (while/by eating)
- EN finissant (while/by finishing)
- EN étant (while/by being)
- EN ayant (while/by having)
- EN sachant (while/by knowing)

**3. Usage 1: Simultaneity (While)**

- "Il chante **en se douchant**." (He sings **while showering**.)
- "Elle écoute la radio **en conduisant**." (She listens to the radio **while driving**.)

**4. Usage 2: Manner/Means (By)**

- "J'ai appris le français **en regardant** des films." (I learned French **by watching** movies.)
- "On progresse **en pratiquant**." (You progress **by practicing**.)

**5. Usage 3: Condition (If/When)**

- "**En travaillant** dur, tu réussiras." (**If you work** / **By working** hard, you'll succeed.)
- "**En partant** tôt, tu éviteras les bouchons." (**If you leave** / **By leaving** early, you'll avoid traffic.)

**6. TOUT EN + -ant (Emphasis / Contrast)**
Adding **TOUT** emphasizes the simultaneity, or creates a contrast:

- "Il sourit **tout en pleurant**." (He smiles **while** (at the same time) **crying**.)
- "Elle travaille **tout en écoutant** de la musique." (She works **while** listening to music.)

**7. Critical Rule: Same Subject!**
⚠️ The subject of the gérondif MUST be the same as the subject of the main verb.

- ✅ "**En arrivant**, **j'**ai vu Marie." (When **I** arrived, **I** saw Marie. — Same subject!)
- ❌ "**En arrivant**, Marie **m'**a vu." (This implies Marie was the one arriving, but the sentence is ambiguous.)

**Cultural Note:**
The gérondif "en + -ant" is extremely useful in conversation. It's one of the easiest ways to add complexity and nuance to your sentences. Master this construction and you'll sound much more natural in French!

---

### GRAMMAR REVIEW (15 min)

**1. Voix Passive:**

- Formation: ÊTRE (conjugated) + Past Participle (+ par/de + agent).
- Agreement: PP agrees with subject (gender + number).
- All tenses: est fait, a été fait, sera fait, était fait, avait été fait.
- PAR (most verbs) vs. DE (emotion/state: aimé de, connu de, entouré de).

**2. Alternatives to Passive:**

- **ON** (most common): "On a volé mon vélo."
- **SE FAIRE** (personal experience): "Je me suis fait voler."
- **Reflexive passive** (customs): "Le vin se boit frais."

**3. Participe Présent:**

- Formation: nous-stem + **-ant**.
- Irregulars: **Étant, Ayant, Sachant**.
- Replaces "qui + verb": "Les gens parlant français" = "Les gens qui parlent français."
- Expresses cause: "Étant malade, je suis resté."
- **No agreement** when verbal (PP). Agreement when adjective.

**4. Gérondif:**

- Formation: **EN** + participe présent.
- Simultaneity: "Il mange en regardant la télé." (while)
- Manner: "J'apprends en lisant." (by)
- Condition: "En partant tôt, tu éviteras le trafic." (if/by)
- **TOUT EN**: emphasis/contrast: "Il sourit tout en pleurant."
- Critical rule: **Same subject** for both verbs.

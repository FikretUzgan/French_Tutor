## DAY 91 - B1.1.91: Pronoms Relatifs Composés (Lequel)

### Metadata

- **CEFR Level:** B1.1
- **Grammar Topic:** Relative pronouns after prepositions (in which, with which)
- **Vocabulary Domain:** Locations and Spatial relations
- **Content Identifiers:** lequel_laquelle, relative_pronouns_complex, prepositions
- **Speaking Tier:** 3
- **Duration:** 30 minutes
- **Day Number:** 91

---

### GRAMMAR SECTION (10 min)

#### Explanation

**1. The Problem**
You know **qui** (subject), **que** (object), and **où** (place/time). But what about "The pen **with which** I write"?

- You cannot say "avec qui" (pen is not a person).
- You cannot say "avec que" (grammar error).

**2. The Solution: Lequel**
Use **Lequel** (and its forms) after a preposition (avec, sans, pour, sur, sous, dans...).
It must agree in gender and number with the noun it replaces.

| | Masculine | Feminine |
|---|---|---|
| **Singular** | Lequel | Laquelle |
| **Plural** | Lesquels | Lesquelles |

**3. Examples**

- "Le stylo **avec lequel** j'écris." (The pen **with which** I write.)
- "La table **sur laquelle** je suis assis." (The table **on which** I am sitting.)
- "Les amis **pour lesquels** je cuisine." (The friends **for whom** I cook - *Qui* is also okay for people, but *Lequels* is precise).

**4. Exceptions**

- If the preposition is simple "de", use **dont** (Week 20).
- If the preposition is "à", "lequel" contracts (Day 92 preview).

## DAY 92 - B1.1.92: Auquel & Duquel (Contractions)

### Metadata

- **CEFR Level:** B1.1
- **Grammar Topic:** Contractions of À/De + Lequel
- **Vocabulary Domain:** Verbs with indirect objects
- **Content Identifiers:** auquel, duquel, relative_pronouns_contracted, grammar_advanced
- **Speaking Tier:** 3
- **Duration:** 30 minutes
- **Day Number:** 92

---

### GRAMMAR SECTION (10 min)

#### Explanation

**1. The Contractions with À**
When the preposition is **À**, it contracts with Le/Les forms of Lequel.

- À + Lequel = **Auquel**
- À + Lesquels = **Auxquels**
- À + Lesquelles = **Auxquelles**
- À + Laquelle = **À laquelle** (No contraction!)

*Usage:* With verbs like "penser à", "répondre à", "s'intéresser à".
"Le film **auquel** je pense." (The movie I am thinking about.)

**2. The Contractions with DE**
When the preposition is a complex one ending in **DE** (près de, à côté de, à cause de).

- De + Lequel = **Duquel**
- De + Lesquels = **Desquels**
- De + Lesquelles = **Desquelles**
- De + Laquelle = **De laquelle** (No contraction!)

*Note:* For simple "de" (possession), we use **Dont** (Week 20). **Duquel** is used mainly with locational phrases like "près de".
"L'arbre **près duquel** je suis." (The tree near which I am.)

## DAY 93 - B1.1.93: Pronoms Démonstratifs (Celui/Celle)

### Metadata

- **CEFR Level:** B1.1
- **Grammar Topic:** Demonstrative Pronouns (The one, those)
- **Vocabulary Domain:** Selection and Preference
- **Content Identifiers:** demonstrative_pronouns, celui_celle, celui_ci_celui_la
- **Speaking Tier:** 3
- **Duration:** 30 minutes
- **Day Number:** 93

---

### GRAMMAR SECTION (10 min)

#### Explanation

**1. What are they?**
They replace a noun to avoid repetition.
English: "I like **the one** on the left." / "I like **those** who are red."
French: "J'aime **celui** de gauche."

**2. Forms**

| | Masculine | Feminine |
|---|---|---|
| **Singular** | Celui (the one) | Celle (the one) |
| **Plural** | Ceux (the ones/those) | Celles (the ones/those) |

**3. Usage 1: With "de" (Possession/Origin)**

- "My car and **Paul's**." -> "Ma voiture et **celle de** Paul." (Literally: the one of Paul).

**4. Usage 2: With Relative Pronoun**

- "The one who is..." -> "**Celui qui** est..."
- "Those that I see..." -> "**Ceux que** je vois..."

**5. Usage 3: Suffixes -ci / -là (This one / That one)**

- **Celui-ci** (This one here).
- **Celui-là** (That one there).
- Used to distinguish between two specific items explicitly.

## DAY 94 - B1.1.94: La Mise en Relief (C'est...qui/que)

### Metadata

- **CEFR Level:** B1.1
- **Grammar Topic:** Highlighting specific parts of a sentence
- **Vocabulary Domain:** Emphasis
- **Content Identifiers:** mise_en_relief, c_est_qui, c_est_que, emphasis
- **Speaking Tier:** 3
- **Duration:** 30 minutes
- **Day Number:** 94

---

### GRAMMAR SECTION (10 min)

#### Explanation

**1. What is Mise en Relief?**
It means "putting in relief" or **emphasizing**.
Standard: "Paul a mangé le gâteau."
Emphasized (subject): "It is Paul who ate the cake." -> "**C'est** Paul **qui** a mangé le gâteau."
Emphasized (object): "It is the cake that Paul ate." -> "**C'est** le gâteau **que** Paul a mangé."

**2. Structure C'est... QUI**
Highlights the **Subject**.

- "Marie a gagné." -> "C'est **Marie qui** a gagné."
- "Je suis chef." -> "C'est **moi qui** suis chef." (Note: verb matches 'moi'/'je' -> suis!)

**3. Structure C'est... QUE**
Highlights the **Object** or **Time/Place**.

- "J'aime Paris." -> "C'est **Paris que** j'aime."
- "Il part demain." -> "C'est **demain qu'**il part."

**4. Ce qui / Ce que**
When the highlighted thing is vague ("What").

- "What interests me is football." -> "**Ce qui** m'intéresse, c'est le foot."
- "What I want is water." -> "**Ce que** je veux, c'est de l'eau."

## DAY 95 - B1.1.95: Week 19 Review & Assessment

### Metadata

- **CEFR Level:** B1.1
- **Grammar Topic:** Review of Relative and Demonstrative Pronouns
- **Vocabulary Domain:** Review
- **Content Identifiers:** review, assessment, weekly_exam
- **Speaking Tier:** 3
- **Duration:** 30 minutes
- **Day Number:** 95

---

### GRAMMAR REVIEW (15 min)

**1. Lequel (Relative):**

- Replaces noun after preposition (avec, sur, pour).
- Forms: Lequel, Laquelle, Lesquels, Lesquelles.
- **Le stylo avec lequel...**

**2. Auquel / Duquel (Relative Contractions):**

- À + Lequel -> **Auquel**. (Je pense à ce film -> Le film auquel je pense).
- De + Lequel -> **Duquel**. (Only locational/complex de: Près duquel).

**3. Celui (Demonstrative):**

- Replaces noun.
- Forms: Celui, Celle, Ceux, Celles.
- Usage: **Celui de** Paul / **Celui qui** est là / **Celui-ci**.

**4. Mise en Relief:**

- **C'est...qui** (Subject).
- **C'est...que** (Object/Other).
- **Ce qui / Ce que**.
